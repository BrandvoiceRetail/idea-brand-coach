The framework behind the score

IDEA

At the moment of purchase, your customer's brain is running four checks simultaneously. All four must be satisfied for trust to be established. These are the four dimensions of the IDEA Framework, drawn from Trevor Bradford's book What Captures the Heart Goes in the Cart. The Trust Gap Score measures how well your brand is meeting all four. Where the score falls short is where the sale is being lost.

I
Insight

A deep read of why your customers really buy, the emotional drivers under the demographics. Without it, your brand addresses the rational decision while the real one happens somewhere else entirely.

D
Distinctive

Standing for something, not just standing out. Features are irrelevant until identity is established, and identity is established by the brand, not the product.

E
Empathetic

Mirroring what the customer actually feels, safety, belonging, validation. Specs don't reach this state. Recognition does.

A
Authentic

Values and promises kept in actions, not words. A brand that claims what it stands for without demonstrating it loses this buyer before the product is ever considered.
