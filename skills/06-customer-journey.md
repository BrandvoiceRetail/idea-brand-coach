# SKILL 06 — The Customer Journey & Signposts

## Purpose
This skill covers how customers move from awareness to advocacy — and the emotional triggers (Signposts) at each stage. It powers the Journey section of the app. The coach must be able to map a customer's emotional journey and help brands show up with the right message at each stage.

---

## The Core Principle
Customers NEVER buy randomly. Each decision to buy is accompanied by motivations and emotional triggers that help move the customer forward. Understanding these motivations is crucial for creating authentic and effective customer experiences.

The customer journey is an emotional process, shaped by motivations and needs that guide each decision. It is not a funnel — it is a series of emotional moments.

---

## The Five Stages

STAGE 1 — AWARENESS
The customer realises they have a problem or need. The emotional Signpost is curiosity or frustration as they seek a solution to something bothering them.

Example: A customer realises they need a new skincare routine because they feel self-conscious about their skin texture. The emotional Signpost is the desire for self-confidence and self-care. A brand that recognises this need — rather than just selling "a skincare solution" — connects on a deeper level.

Brand task at this stage: Interrupt the pattern, name the problem, show that you understand their world.

---

STAGE 2 — CONSIDERATION
The customer actively looks for solutions, comparing options. The emotional Signpost is hope or anticipation as they seek a product that will meet their needs.

Example: A customer considering different fitness apps wants to feel healthier. One app, using imagery of real people and a warm tone of voice, taps into the customer's aspiration, creating an emotional connection.

Brand task at this stage: Demonstrate understanding, show social proof, build credibility, and create anticipation of the transformation your product delivers.

---

STAGE 3 — DECISION
The customer has narrowed their options and is emotionally ready to make a choice. The Signpost is trust or relief, as they want to feel confident in their decision. They should be anticipating their "Shopper High" as they look forward to making their purchase.

Example: A customer deciding between two travel companies chooses the one that emphasises customer testimonials and offers a clear refund policy. The emotional Signpost of trust reassures them of a positive experience.

Brand task at this stage: Remove risk (guarantees, free returns, clear policies), provide final social proof, reduce friction in the purchase process. Make buying feel effortless and safe.

---

STAGE 4 — RETENTION
After purchasing, the customer seeks satisfaction and validation. They want to feel that they made the right choice.

Example: A clothing brand sends a personalised thank-you email after purchase, including styling tips for the items. This gesture reinforces the customer's satisfaction and makes them feel valued.

Brand task at this stage: Confirm the purchase was the right decision, deliver on the promise, create a positive post-purchase experience. Turn a transaction into a relationship.

---

STAGE 5 — ADVOCACY
The customer feels so connected to the brand that they want to share their positive experience with others. The emotional Signpost is pride or belonging.

Example: Customers of a tech company post online reviews or share unboxing videos because they feel proud to be part of the brand's community, not because they were asked.

Brand task at this stage: Create moments worth sharing. Build community. Reward loyalty. Make the customer feel they are part of something bigger than a purchase.

---

## What Are Signposts?

In the IDEA Framework, emotional triggers and mental shortcuts are called "Signposts" because they act as key emotional moments that guide customers from one stage of the journey to the next.

Signposts are the moments where a brand can either win or lose a customer. Every interaction — an ad, a listing image, a product description, a follow-up email, a packaging insert — is a potential Signpost. Brands that design Signposts intentionally create seamless emotional journeys. Brands that ignore them create confusion and friction.

---

## Overcoming Barriers and Friction

The biggest obstacle to conversion is customer anxiety — the fear of trying something new or changing from an existing solution. People are reluctant to change because they are comfortable with their existing habits ("habit of the present" — Bob Moesta, Jobs-To-Be-Done Framework).

FRICTION REDUCTION STRATEGIES:
- Free trials and try-before-you-buy (Casper's 100-night trial, Warby Parker's home try-on)
- Money-back guarantees (Headspace, Dollar Shave Club)
- Risk-free subscriptions with cancel anytime policies (Netflix, Spotify)
- Performance-based guarantees (Domino's 30-minutes-or-free)
- Simplified checkout (Amazon one-click, Apple Pay autofill)
- 24/7 customer support (Zappos)

Each of these solutions removes an obstacle that might prevent a customer from making a purchase, increasing conversions and satisfaction.

## The Three Customer Types by Journey Stage

PASSIVE LOOKING — just learning and browsing. Need educational content and non-pressured engagement to understand their problem better.

ACTIVE LOOKING — solution-aware and comparing options. Need clear comparisons and specific information about how your product solves their problem.

DECIDING — ready to buy. Need reassurances: testimonials, guarantees, easy paths to purchase. Do not add complexity at this stage.

Mismatch between what the brand is communicating and where the customer is in their journey is a major cause of lost sales. Too much information too early creates overwhelm. Too little too late creates hesitation.

---

## Timing-Sensitive Messaging

Context matters. Customers evaluate products based on their current situation, needs, and timing. Effective brands align their messaging with when the customer is most likely to feel the need.

Examples:
- Tax season messaging: "File early and get your maximum refund fast!" — creates urgency around a natural deadline
- New Year: "New Year, New You — Start Your Fitness Journey Today!" — leverages the resolution mindset
- Back-to-school: targets students and parents at peak shopping intent
- Seasonal timing: "Book your summer getaway before prices rise!" — uses FOMO around natural seasonal behaviour

Lesson: the right message at the wrong time is still the wrong message.

---

## Brand Voice Across the Journey

Different stages require different tones:
- AWARENESS: curious, empathetic, problem-aware — "We get it. This is frustrating."
- CONSIDERATION: educational, inspiring, aspirational — "Here is what becomes possible."
- DECISION: reassuring, confident, trust-building — "You're making the right choice."
- RETENTION: warm, celebratory, value-adding — "You're going to love this."
- ADVOCACY: community-focused, proud, celebratory — "You're part of something special."

---

## Coaching Application

When a user says sales drop after initial traffic or they have high add-to-cart but low conversion, diagnose by stage:

1. High traffic, low awareness engagement → Awareness Signpost failing: the brand is not naming the customer's problem or speaking to their emotional state
2. Consideration drop-off → Not building enough hope or social proof
3. Decision abandonment → Too much friction or too little trust (no reviews, unclear returns policy, complex checkout)
4. Low repeat purchase → Retention failure: no post-purchase relationship being built
5. Zero word-of-mouth → Advocacy gap: nothing remarkable enough to share

Help the user identify the weakest stage and design the right Signpost for it.
