# SKILL 13 — Research Methods & Sources

## Purpose
This skill covers how to gather the customer insights that feed the IDEA Framework and Avatar 2.0. Think like a detective — gather data from multiple sources to uncover hidden insights. The more comprehensive the research, the better the brand positioning will be.

---

## The Research Mindset

We must become detectives and follow the clues to build as complete a picture as possible. We are looking for: emotional language, recurring frustrations, unmet desires, and the gap between what customers say and what they actually do.

"You don't have to use every resource below — but the more we dig, the more buried treasure we will find."

Use AI as much as possible to sift through data for maximum efficiency.

---

## AMAZON-SPECIFIC RESEARCH TOOLS

### 1. Customer Reviews
WHY IT IS VALUABLE: Unfiltered insights into customer motivations, satisfaction, and emotional responses to products. Reviews reveal recurring themes, pain points, and emotional triggers — frustration with packaging, delight with quality, joy at the result.

IDEA APPLICATION: Insight-Driven and Empathetic — directly highlights the emotional drivers behind satisfaction or dissatisfaction. Align messaging to emphasise solutions to the frustrations you find.

TIP: Use AI sentiment analysis tools (MonkeyLearn, Lexalytics) to analyse large volumes quickly.

### 2. Amazon Q&A Section
WHY IT IS VALUABLE: Shows pre-purchase hesitations and customer concerns before they buy. Identifies uncertainty, fear of making the wrong purchase, and areas where additional information or reassurance is needed.

IDEA APPLICATION: Empathetic and Insight-Driven — use this to anticipate objections in product copy.

TIP: Use "People Also Ask" AI tools to generate answers based on the most common customer concerns.

### 3. Amazon Search Term Reports (for Brand-Registered Sellers)
WHY IT IS VALUABLE: Reveals how customers search for solutions, including intent and motivation. Detects functional vs emotional-driven keywords and gaps where competitors are not meeting specific customer needs.

IDEA APPLICATION: Insight-Driven — optimise product descriptions and ad copy with emotional triggers that match search intent.

### 4. Amazon Best Seller Rank (BSR) and Competitor Tracking
WHY IT IS VALUABLE: Shows which products resonate emotionally with customers based on sales trends. Identifies seasonality and emotional triggers (impulse buys, gifting trends).

IDEA APPLICATION: Distinctive — identify what is trending and position the brand to stand out.

TIP: Use Helium 10's Black Box feature to track BSR trends over time.

### 5. Amazon Vine Programme
WHY IT IS VALUABLE: Detailed, high-quality reviews providing deeper insights into product experiences and emotional reactions. Even if you haven't launched your product, check competitors' Vine reviews.

### 6. Amazon Brand Analytics (Brand-Registered Sellers)
WHY IT IS VALUABLE: Provides insights into customer behaviour, search frequency, top search terms, and customer demographics. Identifies patterns in how customers search and what drives their decisions.

### 7. Amazon A/B Testing (Manage Your Experiments)
WHY IT IS VALUABLE: Tests which product listing elements emotionally connect with customers and lead to higher conversion. Determines which messaging, imagery, and assets resonate most.

### 8. Helium 10 and Jungle Scout
Helium 10: Keyword research, product research, listing optimisation. Identifies what emotional and functional attributes customers prioritise when searching on Amazon.
Jungle Scout: Product research and sales analytics. Highlights product opportunities based on customer search trends and buying behaviour.

## BROADER RESEARCH METHODS

### Customer Reviews Beyond Amazon
Platforms: Google, Yelp, Facebook, Reddit, TikTok
HOW TO USE: Analyse emotional language, identify recurring themes, pinpoint triggers. Look for exact phrases customers use to describe their problem and desired outcome — this is gold for copywriting.

### Surveys and Questionnaires
WHY IT IS VALUABLE: Structured insights by directly asking customers about motivations, desires, and emotional responses. Can be conducted via email, post-purchase surveys, or social media polls.

EFFECTIVE SURVEY QUESTIONS:
- "What was the main reason you decided to buy this product?"
- "What were you worried about before you purchased?"
- "How has your life changed since using this product?"
- "What would you tell a friend about this product?"

### Social Media Listening and UGC
WHY IT IS VALUABLE: Social media conversations reflect authentic, real-time emotional reactions to brands and products. Identifies how people describe products in their own words.

WHERE TO LOOK: Twitter/X, Instagram, TikTok, Reddit, Facebook Groups. Influencer and customer video reviews.

TIP: Use Sprout Social or Brandwatch for automated sentiment analysis.

### Competitor Analysis
WHY IT IS VALUABLE: Understanding why customers are converting on competitors' listings and not yours. Identifies the emotional triggers competitors have deployed and the gaps you can exploit.

WHAT TO ANALYSE: Product listings, imagery, reviews, Q&A, A+ content, brand stores, social media, advertising.

## AI-POWERED RESEARCH TOOLS

- Google Trends and Exploding Topics: detect shifts in customer interests and search behaviour
- ChatGPT/Claude: generate customer personas based on aggregated insights, analyse patterns in review data
- Lexalytics and IBM Watson: AI-powered sentiment analysis for product reviews
- Hotjar and Crazy Egg: visual heatmaps to track how customers interact with websites
- ReviewMeta: analyse review authenticity and detect patterns across competitors

## TURNING RESEARCH INTO ACTION

The research process is only valuable if it produces actionable insights. The synthesis steps:

1. FIND PATTERNS: Look for recurring customer frustrations, desires, and emotional language. When ten customers describe the same problem in similar words, that is your messaging.

2. IDENTIFY THE EMOTIONAL TRIGGER: What emotion is driving the most positive reviews? What emotion is driving the negative ones? This defines your primary trigger and your key objection to overcome.

3. LEARN THE CUSTOMER'S LANGUAGE: The exact phrases customers use in reviews and Q&A are more powerful in copy than anything a copywriter invents. Use their words.

4. ADAPT YOUR MESSAGING: Use insights to fine-tune product descriptions, ads, and brand statements. Every insight should have a direct application to a specific piece of communication.

5. REFINE CUSTOMER TARGETING: Adjust marketing based on real behavioural insights, not assumptions. If research reveals a different shopper type than expected, update the Avatar 2.0 accordingly.

## Coaching Application

When a user says they do not know what their customers want, or they are guessing at their messaging, direct them to research before any brand strategy work. The sequence is:

1. Read your own reviews — all of them, positive and negative
2. Read your top three competitors' reviews — look for the gaps
3. Search your category on Amazon, read the Q&A sections
4. Search your category on Reddit and TikTok — find how customers talk about their problem
5. Run a post-purchase survey asking: "What was the main reason you bought this?"

Tell them: "Your customers have already written your best copy. You just have to find it."
