# SKILL 19 — Cialdini: Pre-Suasion

## Source
Robert Cialdini — Pre-Suasion: A Revolutionary Way to Influence and Persuade (2016)
Application to IDEA: The practice of establishing the right frame, context, and attention BEFORE the persuasive message — making people receptive before you ask.

---

## Core Thesis
The most powerful moment of influence is not the message itself — it is the moment immediately before the message. What you do to prime a person's mind before you make a request determines how they receive that request. Cialdini calls this "pre-suasion" — the art of arranging for recipients to be already receptive when the moment of persuasion arrives.

This complements Influence (Skill 15) by adding the temporal dimension: before the six principles are applied, the context must be set. The same message, delivered in a different pre-suasive context, produces dramatically different results.

---

## The Privileged Moment

Cialdini's central discovery: there is a window of heightened receptivity just before a persuasive message. What occupies a person's attention in that window colours how they interpret everything that follows.

The mechanism: whatever holds our attention in the moment feels important. Our minds treat attention as a signal of significance. If I direct your attention to something — safety, value, warmth, exclusivity — you will use that lens to evaluate what comes next.

This is why first impressions are disproportionately powerful. Not because they are more accurate, but because they set the frame for everything that follows.

---

## The Channelling Principle: Where Attention Goes, Value Flows

The first principle of pre-suasion: direct attention to what you want the customer to value BEFORE asking them to value it.

E-COMMERCE APPLICATIONS:

WEBSITE AND LISTING OPENING IMAGE: The first image a customer sees on a product listing or landing page sets the pre-suasive frame for everything they read next. A luxury lifestyle image primes premium perception. A clinical/medical image primes trust and credibility. A community/family image primes belonging and warmth.

ABOVE-THE-FOLD CONTENT: What the customer sees without scrolling is the pre-suasion field. It should prime the primary emotional trigger BEFORE they encounter the features, reviews, or price.

AD CREATIVE: The first two seconds of a video advertisement are the pre-suasion window. They establish the emotional frame before the product message arrives. Disrupting with the wrong emotion at this point poisons everything that follows.

HEADLINE: The headline is read before the copy. If the headline primes a different emotion from the body copy, there is pre-suasive dissonance — the copy never lands effectively.

---

## Attention and Association

Cialdini's research shows that associating your message with positive or relevant concepts immediately before delivery dramatically increases its effectiveness.

Example: In one study, a website with a background image of money caused visitors to spend more but care less about environmental factors. A website with a background image of nature caused visitors to care more about environmental factors but spend less. The background image was pre-suasion — it primed a set of values before the visitor made any conscious decisions.

E-COMMERCE APPLICATION: Every visual element on a product page or advertisement is pre-suasion. The background imagery, the colour palette, the lifestyle photography, the fonts — all of these prime emotional states before the customer reads a single word of copy.

Pre-suasion checklist for a product listing:
- Does the primary image prime the correct emotional state for the purchase decision?
- Does the colour palette support the brand's primary emotional trigger?
- Does the lifestyle imagery show the customer's aspirational self?
- Does the headline prime the most important benefit before the supporting details?

## The Role of Context in Pre-Suasion

Context is a powerful pre-suasive force. The same person, making the same purchasing decision, in different contexts will decide differently.

THE WARM/COLD STUDY: People who held a warm cup of coffee before an interaction judged a stranger as "warm and friendly." Those who held a cold drink judged the stranger as "cold and distant." The cup primed the interpretation of human character.

Brand application: The physical and digital environment in which your brand is encountered creates pre-suasion. A product listed on a premium, visually elegant platform creates different pre-suasion than the same product listed on a discount marketplace with cluttered design. Your brand store on Amazon, your DTC website, and your social media aesthetic are all pre-suasive environments.

## Unity: The Pre-Suasive Power of Shared Identity

Pre-Suasion expands on the Unity principle introduced in Influence. Cialdini finds that establishing a sense of shared identity BEFORE making a request dramatically increases compliance.

"We are the same kind of people" is the most powerful pre-suasive frame.

BRAND APPLICATION:
- Establishing shared values before asking for a purchase
- "For people who believe that what you put in your body matters" — establishes identity alignment before product claim
- Community language: "Like you, we believe..." primes unity before the message
- Founder story that reflects the customer's own values and experiences

This is why Authentically Human Branding is so effective as a pre-suasive strategy. Before the product makes its case, the brand has established: "We are the same kind of people. We share your values. You can trust us."

---

## Reciprocal Pre-Suasion: Giving Before Asking

Cialdini extends the reciprocity principle into pre-suasion territory: giving genuine value BEFORE asking for anything creates a pre-suasive state of obligation and positive feeling that makes the subsequent request land in fertile ground.

The key word is BEFORE. A gift that comes after a request is reciprocity. A gift that comes before a request is pre-suasion — it establishes the emotional and relational context in which the request will be received.

APPLICATIONS:
- Educational content published before any commercial ask (content marketing as pre-suasion)
- Free samples delivered before the request to subscribe
- A genuinely valuable quiz or assessment completed before the product recommendation
- Transparent, helpful information shared before the sales pitch

The brand that gives value first is pre-suading the customer to be receptive before the pitch arrives.

## Attention, Contrast, and the Novel

Pre-suasion research identifies what naturally commands attention — and therefore what can be used to pre-suasively direct it:

NOVELTY: Unusual, unexpected, or surprising elements automatically capture attention. A thumb-stopping image in a feed, an unexpected colour, a counterintuitive headline.

THREAT: Potential danger or loss commands attention (used ethically: highlighting the problem the product solves before presenting the solution).

SEX AND ROMANCE: Powerful attentional draw — but must be relevant to the product category or it creates association rather than relevance.

PERSONAL RELEVANCE: "People like you..." is one of the most powerful attentional triggers because it signals personally relevant information.

E-COMMERCE APPLICATION: The hero image, the listing headline, and the first A+ module are all competing for attention in a crowded environment. Pre-suasion research says: use novelty, personal relevance, and the implied threat of the problem-not-solved to command attention before the solution is presented.

## Pre-Suasion and the Customer Journey

Pre-suasion operates at every stage of the customer journey (see Skill 06):

AWARENESS STAGE: The first time a customer encounters your brand (ad, search result, social post) is pure pre-suasion. What it communicates before the customer has read anything sets the frame for all future interactions.

CONSIDERATION STAGE: The content a customer consumes while researching is pre-suading their decision. Brands that provide the most useful, honest, and valuable pre-purchase content are pre-suading in their favour.

DECISION STAGE: The final moments before purchase are pre-suaded by: the last review read, the guarantee visible at checkout, the image next to the buy button. These are the privileged moments Cialdini identifies.

POST-PURCHASE: The unboxing experience pre-suades the customer's ongoing relationship with the brand. A premium, thoughtful unboxing experience pre-suades the customer to expect quality in everything that follows.

---

## Application to the IDEA Framework

INSIGHT-DRIVEN: Understanding which pre-suasive frames work for your specific customer requires deep insight into their values, emotional state, and attentional triggers.

DISTINCTIVE: Distinctive visual and verbal assets create powerful pre-suasive associations. When a customer sees your brand colours, they are pre-suaded before they have read a word.

EMPATHETIC: The most powerful pre-suasive frame for an empathetic brand is shared identity and shared experience — "we understand your situation" established before the solution is offered.

AUTHENTIC: Authentic brands pre-suade through consistency. Every touchpoint — from the first ad seen to the packaging held — communicates the same values, creating a pre-suasive environment of trust.

## Coaching Application

When a user's copy and claims are strong but conversion is still underperforming, look at the pre-suasion environment:

1. "What is the very first thing a customer sees when they land on your page or listing? What emotion does it prime?"
2. "Does your hero image prime the right emotional state — before they read a single word?"
3. "What are customers doing and thinking immediately before they encounter your brand? What context are they coming from?"
4. "Is there a pre-suasive frame established before your product makes its case? Are you sharing values, acknowledging their world, and establishing trust before asking for anything?"
5. "What does your checkout page look like? The last things a customer sees before clicking 'buy' are pre-suading that final decision."

The practical shortcut: read your product listing as if you have never seen it before. What emotional state does it put you in within the first three seconds? If it is not the right one, you have a pre-suasion problem — not a copywriting problem.
