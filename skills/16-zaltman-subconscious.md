# SKILL 16 — Zaltman: How Customers Think

## Source
Professor Gerald Zaltman — How Customers Think: Essential Insights into the Mind of the Market (2003)
Application to IDEA: The scientific basis for the 95% emotional decision-making principle and the subconscious "why" behind all purchasing behaviour.

---

## Core Thesis
Consumers are largely unaware of the forces that shape their thinking and drive their purchasing decisions. Up to 95% of consumer decision-making occurs in the subconscious mind — not in the logical, verbal, conscious brain that traditional market research attempts to access. The implications for brand strategy are profound.

---

## The 95% Principle

Zaltman's central finding: most consumer decision-making is subconscious and emotionally driven. People do not buy products because they are the best rational choice. They buy because of emotions, associations, metaphors, and deep psychological needs — then justify those decisions with logic afterwards.

This is why:
- Asking customers why they bought a product often produces inaccurate answers (they are accessing System 2 rationalisations, not System 1 truths)
- Focus groups frequently fail to predict real purchasing behaviour
- Customers say they value price above all, but often buy the more expensive option
- Brands that create emotional connection consistently outperform brands with superior products

Brand implication: traditional consumer research asks the wrong questions. You cannot get to the subconscious truth by asking conscious questions. You have to observe behaviour, analyse language, and use projective techniques to surface what customers actually feel.

---

## Deep Metaphors

Zaltman's research identified universal "deep metaphors" — fundamental, largely unconscious ways of perceiving and understanding the world that drive behaviour across cultures:

BALANCE: Equilibrium, justice, the interplay of elements. Brands in wellness, finance, and law tap into this.
TRANSFORMATION: Change and transition in state, character, or circumstance. Skincare, fitness, and education brands are fundamentally transformation brands.
JOURNEY: Life, time, choices, and a course of action. Travel, career, and personal development brands tap into this.
CONTAINER: Inclusion/exclusion, safe/unsafe, inside/outside. Community brands, luxury brands, and security products use containment metaphors.
CONNECTION: Relationships between people, and the desire for belonging. Social platforms, family brands, and community-driven products.
RESOURCE: Acquisition and preservation. Wealth, health, time, and energy brands.
CONTROL: Mastery, order, protection. Technology, insurance, and professional services brands.

Brand application: Identify which deep metaphor your product and customer connect with. The most resonant brand communications tap into these archetypal themes, not just surface-level features.

Example: A hair growth product is not just a serum — it is a TRANSFORMATION metaphor. The customer wants to be different. The before/after is not a product demonstration — it is a transformation story.

---

## The Subconscious Customer Profile

Traditional customer research produces demographic and psychographic data. Zaltman's work points toward a deeper level — the emotional, metaphorical, and subconscious drivers that demographic data cannot capture.

Key subconscious drivers to uncover:
- What does this product represent to the customer at a deeper level?
- What story is the customer telling themselves about who they are by buying this?
- What fear, hope, or desire is being addressed at the subconscious level?
- What social signals does this purchase send?

This is the foundation of Avatar 2.0's emotional triggers field — Field 3. Understanding these drivers is the difference between messaging that converts and messaging that is ignored.

---

## The ZMET Technique (Zaltman Metaphor Elicitation Technique)

Zaltman developed a research methodology that asks customers to select images representing their feelings and experiences with a product or brand. The images bypass verbal, conscious responses and access the metaphorical, subconscious ones.

For e-commerce applications of this principle:
- Ask customers: "If your experience with this product were an image, what would it be?"
- In reviews, look for metaphorical language ("this changed my life," "I feel like a new person")
- In UGC, observe how customers photograph and contextualise the product — the setting tells you more than the caption
- Use open-ended post-purchase survey questions: "Describe in your own words how this made you feel"

---

## Consensus Maps: The Shared Mental Models

Zaltman found that customers within a category share remarkably consistent mental models — sets of beliefs, feelings, and associations that are largely unconscious but drive collective behaviour.

Brand application: When you analyse a large body of customer reviews and find the same words, feelings, and metaphors appearing repeatedly, you are mapping the consensus mental model. This is your brand messaging framework. Use the language customers use. Mirror their mental model back to them.

Example: If 200 customers independently use the word "freedom" when describing their experience with a product, that is not coincidence — it is a deep metaphor surfacing. The brand should speak to freedom.

## Implications for the IDEA Framework

INSIGHT-DRIVEN: Zaltman's work demands that insight goes beyond stated preferences. True insight requires accessing the subconscious drivers — through behavioural observation, metaphorical language in reviews, and projective research techniques.

DISTINCTIVE: Brands that tap into deep metaphors (transformation, connection, control) become emotionally significant in a way that surface-level differentiation cannot achieve.

EMPATHETIC: Understanding the subconscious emotional state the customer is in before purchase — and the subconscious emotional state they desire after — is the foundation of empathetic messaging.

AUTHENTIC: The most authentic brands are those whose values, story, and behaviour align with the deep metaphors their customers hold. Authenticity resonates at the subconscious level before the customer can articulate why.

---

## The Three Most Important Zaltman-Informed Insights for Sellers

1. YOUR CUSTOMER DOES NOT BUY WHAT YOU THINK THEY BUY. They buy a feeling, an identity, a transformation, or a metaphor. Find out what it is and speak to that.

2. LISTEN TO THE LANGUAGE IN THEIR REVIEWS. The subconscious truth surfaces in the specific words customers choose when they are not being asked a direct question. Analyse this language carefully.

3. THE EMOTIONAL BEFORE AND AFTER MATTERS MORE THAN THE PRODUCT SPECIFICATIONS. What emotional state is the customer in before purchase? What emotional state do they want to be in after? Build every communication around this arc.

---

## Coaching Application

When a user says their product is good but they cannot articulate why anyone should care, use Zaltman's framework:

1. "What deep metaphor does your product connect to? Transformation? Relief? Connection? Control?"
2. "Read me the three most emotional reviews you have received — not the most detailed, the most emotional. What feeling is surfacing?"
3. "What would a customer feel about themselves if they owned this product — not what they would think about the product, what they would feel about themselves?"

This reframes the brand strategy conversation from product features to human truth — which is where all the real conversion leverage lives.
