# SKILL 12 — Agentic Commerce & AI Visibility

## How AI Agents Differ from Human Shoppers

AI agents behave differently from human shoppers. They prioritise:
- Price
- User ratings
- Delivery speed
- Real-time inventory

They do NOT prioritise:
- Brand familiarity
- Brand loyalty
- Emotional connection (directly)

This means the emotional and trust signals that win human shoppers must now be encoded in the sources AI agents cite and trust — not just in the brand's own marketing.

## Why Earned Media Has Never Mattered More

The critical strategic insight: AI agents care what OTHER PEOPLE say about your brand — not what YOU say about yourself. Earned media (editorial coverage, affiliate reviews, press releases, blogs) is the dominant citation source across every major AI platform.

"If you are not present in earned media, you risk becoming invisible to AI agents — and therefore to your customers." — Azoma.ai

By 2027, brands are projected to DOUBLE their PR and earned media budgets as a direct response to agentic commerce growth.

This is not the death of PR. This is the resurrection of PR as the most commercially critical brand investment.

## The Four-Phase AI Visibility Framework (Analyse, Target, Implement, Measure)

PHASE 1 — ANALYSE: Understand what questions AI agents are asking in your category
1. Query top AI platforms (Rufus, ChatGPT, Gemini, Sparky, Perplexity) with your category's primary use cases and comparison queries
2. Document the questions AI is answering — not just the keywords consumers type
3. Analyse citation sources by platform
4. Map content gaps where your brand is absent from AI responses
5. Identify specific publications and affiliate sites AI is currently citing in your category

PHASE 2 — TARGET: Build your Tier 1 AI Media List
- Editorial Publications (40% of ChatGPT/Gemini citations): target publications with direct OpenAI licensing deals (AP, Condé Nast, Hearst, Dotdash Meredith, Axel Springer, News Corp, Vox Media, Financial Times, The Atlantic, TIME, Guardian, Axios, New York Times)
- Affiliate & Review Sites (72.7% of Rufus citations): highest-priority for Amazon sellers
- Blogs (15% of citations in supplement category): structured, detailed product content
- Press Release Wires (10-15% of citations): PR Newswire, BusinessWire, GlobeNewswire
- Institutional Sources (2-5%): academic citations, industry reports, government mentions — carry disproportionate authority

PHASE 3 — IMPLEMENT: Structure content for machines, not just media
- Build machine-readable product content: schema markup, FAQs, comparison copy, structured product data
- Distribute AI-optimised press releases: must meet four criteria — Structure (journalistic format), Originality (genuinely newsworthy), Authority (recognised wire service), Recency (published within AI's retrieval window)
- Seed authoritative answers on Amazon Q&A, Reddit, and forums AI references
- Secure positions in listicle and comparison articles AI cites

PHASE 4 — MEASURE: Attribution, not just coverage
Key metrics:
- AI Citation Count: times brand/product cited in AI responses
- Citation Share of Voice: your citations vs competitors
- Authority Mentions: citations from Tier 1 editorial and licensed publishers
- AI-Driven Traffic and Revenue: commercial outcomes attributable to AI recommendations

## The IDEA Framework in an Agentic World

The core principles of the IDEA Framework become MORE important in an agentic commerce era — not less:

INSIGHT-DRIVEN: You now need to understand not just what human customers search for, but what questions AI agents are generating in your category. AI agents generate 5.2 query fan-outs per commerce prompt in ChatGPT.

DISTINCTIVE: Distinctiveness must now be encoded in publicly accessible content that AI can cite. A brand that exists only on its own channels is invisible to AI discovery.

EMPATHETIC: Empathetic brand content — genuine customer testimonials, real problem-solution storytelling, authentic UGC — is exactly what AI agents prioritise in their citations. It is what earned media is made of.

AUTHENTIC: Authenticity signals are amplified in agentic commerce. AI agents surface what third parties say about you, not what you say about yourself. An authentic brand with real customer advocates naturally generates the earned media AI relies on.

## What This Means for Amazon Sellers Specifically

The shift from Search Engine Optimisation to Generative Engine Optimisation (GEO) requires:
1. Semantic SEO: shift from keyword density to meaning that focuses on customer needs and intent
2. Fully completed backend attributes: AI needs complete structured data
3. AI-optimised images: Rufus relies on visual content as much as written descriptions
4. Intent-based optimisation: user-focused phrases like "best noise-cancelling earbuds for travel" over generic keyword strings
5. Leveraging customer Q&A and reviews: Rufus actively scans Q&A sections and reviews
6. Affiliate and review site presence: the #1 leverage point for Rufus citation

The future of Amazon success is not tricking the algorithm. It is building brands that AI agents can understand, trust, and recommend.

## Coaching Application

When a user asks how to improve their Amazon visibility or DTC discoverability, do not default to keyword advice. Instead:

1. Ask: "Are you present in the sources AI agents trust in your category — affiliate review sites, editorial publications, community forums?"
2. Ask: "When someone asks ChatGPT or Rufus about your product category, does your brand appear? Have you tested this?"
3. Ask: "Is your product listing structured to answer the questions AI agents are generating — not just the keywords humans are typing?"
4. Advise: "Start with a citation audit. Find out where AI is currently sending customers in your category. Then get present in those sources."
