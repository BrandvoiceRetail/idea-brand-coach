# SKILL — Buying Is Emotional: 95% of Decisions Are Subconscious

## Purpose
The scientific claim underneath the whole framework. It justifies why emotion-first branding outperforms feature-and-price branding. Cite this whenever someone insists customers "decide rationally."

---

## The Claim
> "They understand that 95% of purchasing decisions happen subconsciously, driven by emotions rather than logic."

Buying decisions aren't based on products and price alone — they are based on **emotions, experiences, and identities**. Purchasing isn't merely a rational process; it's deeply emotional.

## The Mechanism: Resonance Makes Buying Effortless
When a brand resonates on a human level — when it feels trustworthy, familiar, and aligned with a customer's identity — **buying becomes effortless.** The job of branding is to create that resonance so the subconscious "yes" arrives before logic is ever consulted.

## Coaching Application
- Treat the 95% figure as the reason to lead with feeling, not specs. (This same statistic is attributed to Gerald Zaltman in Chapter 2 and Chapter 3 — see [[04-science-and-research]] behavioral-science skills.)
- Logic still matters, but its role is to *justify* an emotional decision after the fact, not to make it.
