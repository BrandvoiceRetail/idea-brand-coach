# SKILL — The TikTok Dilemma: Building on Borrowed Ground

## Purpose
Generalizes the borrowed-trust risk to platform dependence itself. Use it to warn any seller whose business sits entirely on one platform (TikTok, Amazon, Instagram) that they are building on land they don't own.

---

## The Power and the Trap
Few platforms reshaped marketing like TikTok — a single viral video can sell out a product in hours, and brands have become addicted to it.

> "But here's the danger: TikTok is built on borrowed real estate. And right now, that real estate is under threat."

At the time of writing, TikTok had a **90-day reprieve** from a potential U.S. ban over ownership and data-security concerns. If it's removed or crippled by regulation, businesses that rely solely on it are left stranded.

## The Same Amazon Trap
> "This isn't just a TikTok issue — it's the same Amazon trap many brands fall into."

Sellers who build their entire business on Amazon are always at the mercy of the platform's **rules, fees, and algorithmic changes.** A single policy update, suspension, or shift in search rankings can wipe out years of work overnight.

## The Bottom Line: Don't Rent Trust — Own It
TikTok might survive the political battle or it might not; either way it's a wake-up call. Just as Amazon sellers face suspensions, fee hikes, and lost visibility, TikTok-dependent businesses now face impermanence.

> "No brand should be at the mercy of an algorithm, a policy change, or a government decision. Building your own audience, your own community, and your own multi-channel trust strategy is the only way to future-proof your business."

> "The lesson is clear: stop renting trust — own it. The brands that take control now will be the ones still standing, no matter what happens to TikTok, Amazon, or any other third-party platform."

## Coaching Application
Audit the seller's traffic and credibility for single-platform dependence. The remedy is owned assets and a multi-channel strategy — see [[04-four-ways-to-own-your-trust]].
