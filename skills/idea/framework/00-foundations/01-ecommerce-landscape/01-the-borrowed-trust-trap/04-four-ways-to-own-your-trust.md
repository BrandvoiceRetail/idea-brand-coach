# SKILL — Four Ways to Own Your Trust

## Purpose
The four concrete moves a brand makes to take control of its own credibility instead of renting it. This is the actionable answer to the borrowed-trust trap — preserve all four with their sub-steps.

---

## The Four Moves
> "If brands want to stop renting trust and start building it, they must take control of their own credibility. Here's how:"

### 1. Be Authentically Human
Consumers trust people — not corporations. Brands that succeed act like real people rather than faceless entities. This means:
- Speaking like a human, not a PR machine.
- Showing vulnerability and transparency.
- Engaging in real conversations with customers.

### 2. Own Your Story
Develop your own narrative instead of relying on influencers to tell it. Storytelling builds trust because it connects on a human level. Answer:
- Why does your brand exist?
- What values do you stand for?
- How do you make customers' lives better?

> "When customers feel connected to your brand's purpose, they don't need an influencer to convince them to buy — they choose you because they believe in you."

### 3. Build Direct Relationships with Customers
The most trustworthy brands interact directly with customers, without a middleman:
- Investing in community engagement — social media, brand-led forums, live Q&As.
- Encouraging user-generated content — real testimonials and organic brand advocacy.
- Rewarding loyalty and repeat customers — exclusive perks for those who engage consistently.

### 4. Create a Multi-Channel Strategy
No brand should be at the mercy of one platform. Whether TikTok, Amazon, or Instagram, diversification is key:
- Develop owned marketing channels like email lists and SMS campaigns.
- Invest in your own website and direct-to-consumer sales.
- Engage with customers across multiple platforms, so they aren't reliant on a single traffic source.

## Coaching Application
Use this as a build-order for owned credibility. Move 2 (Own Your Story) is operationalized by the Brand Canvas (Chapter 5); Move 1 is the Authentically Human approach (Chapter 4).
