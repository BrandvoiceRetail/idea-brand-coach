# SKILL — Influencer Marketing vs. User-Generated Content (UGC)

## Purpose
The data-backed case for shifting spend from paid influencers to authentic user-generated content. Use the stats verbatim — they are the proof that "authenticity is the new currency."

---

## The Shift
The way consumers engage with brands is changing. While influencer marketing once dominated, a growing number of consumers — especially **Gen Z** — are skeptical of sponsored endorsements and instead trust authentic UGC from real customers. This is "influencer fatigue": curated, polished content feels disconnected from real life and reads as transactional.

## The EnTribe Study (verbatim stat block)
> - 86% of consumers trust brands showcasing authentic UGC.
> - Only 12% trust influencer-promoted products.
> - 81% of customers believe influencers have little to no positive impact on their perception of a brand.
> - 90% trust recommendations from friends or family over influencers.

## Why UGC Is Winning Trust
Unlike influencer marketing (paid partnerships), UGC consists of organic, customer-created content — **reviews, testimonials, unboxing videos, and social media posts** — that is unscripted, largely unpaid, and driven by real users sharing honest experiences.

> - 82% of consumers are more likely to buy from brands incorporating UGC in their marketing.
> - Seeing real customers use and enjoy a product triggers social proof, reinforcing trust and purchase intent.

## The Industry Shift: Authenticity Is the New Currency
Brands are moving away from influencer-heavy strategies and investing in **community-driven content** — leveraging loyal customers, micro-communities, and real experiences.

> "For businesses looking to stand out in an increasingly skeptical market, authenticity is the new currency — and UGC is proving to be the most effective way to earn it."

## Coaching Application
Redirect a seller's influencer budget toward systematically capturing and showcasing real-customer UGC (reviews, testimonials, unboxings). It is cheaper, more trusted, and triggers social proof.
