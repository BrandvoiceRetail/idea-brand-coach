# SKILL — The Rise of Borrowed Trust

## Purpose
Explains how brands came to "rent" trust from influencers, and why that rented trust is fragile. Use it to diagnose a brand that has outsourced its credibility instead of building its own.

---

## Trust Outsourced
Trust is the most valuable asset in business, yet many brands made a dangerous miscalculation: **they outsourced trust to influencers**, hoping for a shortcut to credibility.

> "But trust, when rented instead of earned, is fragile. And when it collapses, it takes the brand down with it."

## Why Influencer Marketing Took Off
It solved a real problem — how to reach skeptical, ad-averse consumers. Traditional advertising had lost its grip on attention, so consumers turned to peer recommendations and user-generated content (UGC). Influencers became the perfect bridge: **trusted voices with loyal followings, making brand promotions feel organic rather than commercial.**

## The Decay: Authenticity → Transactionality
As brands poured billions into sponsorships, something changed:

> "Authenticity gave way to transactionality. Influencers became walking billboards, promoting anything for a paycheck. Audiences noticed the shift."

Examples of the broken illusion: a fitness influencer recommending weight-loss supplements one week and fast food the next; a sustainability advocate suddenly pushing fast fashion.

## Coaching Application
If a brand's credibility lives entirely in who is currently promoting it, it owns nothing. The fix is to build the brand's *own* credibility so it survives when the influencer stops talking (see [[01-why-borrowed-trust-is-risky]] and [[04-four-ways-to-own-your-trust]]).
