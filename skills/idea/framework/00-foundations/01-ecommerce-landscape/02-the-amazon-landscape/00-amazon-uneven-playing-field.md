# SKILL — Amazon: Far From an Even Playing Field

## Purpose
Explains the competitive reality U.S./Western sellers face on Amazon — built on trust, but tilted by low-cost Chinese factory competition and a compliance crisis. Use it to set realistic expectations and to reinforce why differentiation-by-trust (not price) is the only winnable game.

---

## Amazon's Trust-Built Dominance
Amazon's dominance is built on a foundation of **trust**, driven by relentless focus on customer experience — convenience, reliability, personalization. That trust attracts sellers with access to a global audience, but the opportunity comes with **strict rules and fierce competition.**

## The Non-Compliant Import Crisis
> "Recent inspections in the European Union revealed that 85–95% of products from platforms like Shein, AliExpress, and Temu fail to meet EU product legislation."

These products often pose serious risks — from choking hazards to overheating electronics to banned substances. With **over one billion e-commerce parcels** entering the EU annually through the Netherlands, authorities are calling for increased supply-chain accountability.

## The Fairness Problem for U.S. Sellers
> "Chinese factory sellers now account for over 50% of Amazon's marketplace; they are encouraged by Amazon as it favors low-cost products to compete with emerging platforms such as TEMU."

These sellers benefit from advantages such as:
- **State-subsidized shipping**
- **Direct factory access**
- **Tax disparities**

**The double standard:** many U.S. sellers criticize this as unfair competition yet often leverage Chinese-owned platforms like TikTok to drive sales. Critics argue true fairness requires **reciprocal treatment** — letting U.S. sellers access Chinese marketplaces (Alibaba, Temu) while enforcing tax and compliance standards on Chinese factory sellers operating in the U.S.

## Coaching Application
A Western seller cannot win the low-cost game against a state-subsidized factory seller. This is the strategic reason the book insists on competing through trust, brand, and emotional connection (the IDEA Framework) rather than price.
