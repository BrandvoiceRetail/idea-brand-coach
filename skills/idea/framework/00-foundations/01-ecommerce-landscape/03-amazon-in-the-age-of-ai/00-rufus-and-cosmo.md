# SKILL — Rufus and Cosmo: Amazon's AI Discovery Engines

## Purpose
Defines the two Amazon AI tools that changed how products get found, and why they make keyword stuffing obsolete. Foundational for any Amazon listing advice in the coach.

---

## Why This Matters
> "Amazon's AI-driven tools, Rufus and Cosmo, are reshaping how customers search for and discover products, forcing sellers to rethink their strategies. Instead of relying on traditional keyword stuffing, brands now need to create listings that align with customer intent, offering detailed, value-driven content that speaks directly to buyer needs."

## Rufus — the Deep-Analysis Tool
> "Rufus functions as a deep analysis tool, assessing product details, customer reviews, and descriptions to surface the most relevant recommendations."

It prioritizes listings that **answer customer concerns** and provide clear, comprehensive insight. If your product page fails to do this, Rufus is more likely to push competitors ahead.

## Cosmo — Behavioral Search
> "Cosmo, on the other hand, refines search and discovery by interpreting user behavior rather than just matching keywords."

Example: a search for **"comfortable shoes"** might surface options for professionals on their feet all day, expecting mothers, or those with foot pain — **based on the individual's shopping history.**

## The Goal: Trust via Relevance
Amazon's goal with these AI systems is to **build deeper customer trust by delivering more relevant results.** For sellers, this means structuring listings so they are more likely to be surfaced.

## Coaching Application
Treat Rufus and Cosmo as readers who reward genuinely helpful, intent-matching listings. The seller's job shifts from gaming an algorithm to answering real buyer questions — which is the same trust-building goal as the rest of the framework.
