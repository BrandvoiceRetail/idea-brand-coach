# SKILL — How Sellers Must Adapt to AI Discovery

## Purpose
The five concrete listing tactics for winning in Rufus/Cosmo intent-based discovery. Preserve the worked before/after examples — they are the operational genius.

---

## The Principle
Success on Amazon now depends on how well a listing speaks to **buyer intent**. Instead of optimizing for algorithms, design pages that feel like **a natural, helpful conversation** with potential customers.

## The Five Adaptations

### 1. Contextualized Product Descriptions
A generic description filled with keywords no longer cuts it; reflect how customers actually search and what problems they need solved.
- Instead of *"ergonomic office chair,"* include phrases like *"provides lumbar support for extended sitting"* or *"adjustable height for different desk setups."*

### 2. The Power of Enhanced Imagery
Customers process images faster than text, and **Rufus relies on visual content as much as written descriptions.** High-quality, informative images — infographics and feature callouts — help ensure products are prioritized in recommendations.

### 3. Leveraging Customer Q&A and Reviews
Rufus actively scans Q&A sections and reviews to assess quality and relevance. Incorporate common concerns:
- If people ask, *"Does this purifier remove pet dander?"*, the listing should include: *"Effectively removes pet dander, allergens, and household odors."*

### 4. Moving Beyond Keywords to Intent-Based Optimization
Evolve from basic match terms to search-intent alignment.
- Instead of stuffing *"wireless earbuds,"* use phrases like *"best noise-canceling earbuds for travel"* or *"earbuds with long battery life for remote work."*

### 5. Understanding Behavioral Triggers
To maximize conversions, appeal to **both** emotional and logical decision-making:
- **Emotional triggers:** social identity, trust, and storytelling (e.g., testimonials about how a product changed a customer's experience).
- **Logical triggers:** specification-driven reassurance (e.g., highlighting *"24-hour battery life"* instead of just *"long battery life"*).
