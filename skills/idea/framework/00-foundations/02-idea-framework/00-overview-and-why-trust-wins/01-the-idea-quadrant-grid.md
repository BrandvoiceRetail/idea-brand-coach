# SKILL — The IDEA Strategic Brand Framework (Quadrant Grid)

## Purpose
The canonical visual of the whole framework: a 2x2 grid of the four pillars arranged around a central TRUST. Reproduced verbatim so coaching can point at the exact quadrant a brand decision belongs to. Use it as the one-page map of the framework — everything in Chapter 2 and beyond is a deeper read on one of these four quadrants.

---

## THE IDEA STRATEGIC BRAND FRAMEWORK

| **Insight Driven** | **Distinctive/Different** |
|---|---|
| Gather deep insights / data about our customers' motivations and emotional triggers. | Stand out at both a product and brand level. Develop Distinctive Brand Assets |
| **— TRUST (center) —** | **— TRUST (center) —** |
| **Authoritative & Authentic** | **Emotionally Intelligent** |
| Be genuine, transparent, and true to the brand's values and promises. Create an Attractive Brand Persona. | Connect with the audience on an emotional level by understanding and responding to their emotional cues |

> All four quadrants surround a single center: **TRUST**. The grid reads: Insight Driven (top-left) and Distinctive/Different (top-right) above; Authoritative & Authentic (bottom-left) and Emotionally Intelligent (bottom-right) below — with TRUST at the heart that all four are built to produce.

## Coaching Application
When reviewing brand work, place it on the grid: Is this an Insight move (data about motivations/triggers)? A Distinctiveness move (standing out, Distinctive Brand Assets)? An Empathy move (responding to emotional cues)? An Authenticity move (genuine, transparent, true to values)? If the work doesn't strengthen any quadrant — or doesn't ultimately build TRUST — it isn't pulling its weight.
