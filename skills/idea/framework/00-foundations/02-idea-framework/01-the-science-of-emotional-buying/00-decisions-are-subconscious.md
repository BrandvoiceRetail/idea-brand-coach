# SKILL — Buying Decisions Are Subconscious

## Purpose
The behavioral-science foundation of the whole framework: purchasing decisions are made in the subconscious, driven by emotion, and only justified with logic afterward. Carries the canonical Freud and Zaltman citations. Use it whenever a brand owner insists their customers "decide rationally" — the research says otherwise.

---

## The Foundation

Customers make countless decisions every day, from choosing what to eat for breakfast to deciding which brand of shoes to buy. While we like to think these choices are based on logic and careful reasoning, both research and psychology suggest otherwise. As **Sigmund Freud** famously argued, human behavior is often shaped by the subconscious, influenced by hidden biases and psychological triggers we may not even be aware of.

Building on this foundation, **Professor Gerald Zaltman of Harvard University**, in his book *How Customers Think: Essential Insights into the Mind of the Market* (2003), reveals that **up to 95% of purchasing decisions are driven by emotions, not rational thought.** These decisions occur in the subconscious mind, and we typically justify them later by creating logical explanations.

> This underscores a profound truth: emotions and subconscious drivers play a dominant role in how people perceive, evaluate, and ultimately choose products or services.

## Post-Hoc Rationalization

People may believe they're making rational choices, but emotional responses often take the lead, and logic comes afterwards to justify the decision. This is why two people with similar budgets and needs might make entirely different purchasing choices — because the emotional responses they have to a product or brand vary greatly.

## Coaching Application
When a brand owner builds a listing that's all specs and price, point at the 95% figure: they're optimizing for the 5% of the decision that happens last. The emotional layer comes first and decides the purchase; the logical layer (covered in [[02-balancing-logic-and-emotion]]) only justifies it. Lead with emotion, back it with logic.
