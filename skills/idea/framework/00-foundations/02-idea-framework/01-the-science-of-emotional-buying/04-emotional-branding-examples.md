# SKILL — Emotional Branding Examples (Nike, Dove, Lego)

## Purpose
Three worked case studies of emotional branding done right — Nike (achievement), Dove (self-esteem), Lego (creativity/nostalgia). Each shows how a brand sells a feeling and a belief, not a product feature. Use them as reference patterns when coaching a brand owner toward their own emotional positioning.

---

## Nike — Achievement and Self-Belief
Nike is one of the most well-known brands when it comes to emotional branding. While the company's products are athletic apparel and footwear, **Nike doesn't sell shoes; it sells achievement, self-belief, and the pursuit of greatness.** Its famous slogan, **"Just Do It,"** is much more than a tagline — it's a powerful emotional trigger that encourages people to push past their limits. This appeal isn't limited to elite athletes; it resonates with everyday people trying to improve themselves. Nike's marketing often focuses on the emotional journey of athletes, tapping into aspiration and self-belief, building a loyal base that sees Nike as a symbol of empowerment and perseverance.

## Dove — Self-Esteem and Real Beauty
Dove has transformed itself from a soap brand into a symbol of beauty and self-esteem. Its **Real Beauty** campaign, launched in **2004**, was a groundbreaking effort to challenge conventional beauty standards. The campaign didn't focus on product features (how well the soap cleans or moisturizes) but instead addressed the **emotional pain** many women feel about their appearance. By featuring real women of all shapes, sizes, and ethnicities, Dove created an emotional connection, telling its audience that beauty comes in all forms. This message of acceptance and self-love aligned with Dove's core brand promise — genuine care and self-confidence — cultivating loyalty among consumers who feel empowered and validated.

## Lego — Creativity, Nostalgia, and Play
Lego has cultivated a passionate global community by embracing **creativity, nostalgia, and play.** Whether through user-generated designs, collaborations with fans, or sustainability initiatives, Lego fosters deep emotional connections, making its products **more than just toys** — they become a part of personal stories and shared experiences.

> These examples show how emotional branding goes beyond mere product features to tap into the deeper feelings, beliefs, and values of customers. When done successfully, emotional branding can foster a level of loyalty that's incredibly hard for competitors to break.

## Coaching Application
Use these as pattern templates. Nike shows "sell the aspiration, not the object." Dove shows "address the emotional pain, not the feature." Lego shows "build a community around a value." Ask the brand owner which pattern best fits their category, then map their product onto it.
