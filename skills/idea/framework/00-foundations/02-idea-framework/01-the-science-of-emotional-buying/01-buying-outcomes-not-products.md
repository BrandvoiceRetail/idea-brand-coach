# SKILL — Customers Buy Outcomes, Not Products

## Purpose
Reframes what a customer is actually purchasing: not the product, but the outcome and transformation it delivers. Carries the canonical Levitt "drill/hole," Drucker, and Accenture 87% citations. Use it to push brand owners from feature-listing toward selling the after-state — the life without the problem.

---

## Transformation, Not Transactions

Today's shoppers are not just buying products — they're buying **outcomes**. Modern consumers are thoughtful, discerning, and deeply intentional. They don't want to clutter their lives with more stuff; they want solutions. What they're truly seeking is a brand that understands them on a personal level, a brand that not only delivers results but also resonates with their deeper needs and desires.

The key to connecting with today's shoppers lies in understanding what they're really after: **transformation, not transactions.** A face cream isn't just a jar of lotion; it's the promise of renewed confidence and radiant skin. A set of toys isn't just about play; it's about fostering joy, creativity, learning, and family bonding. Shoppers are buying the outcome, not the physical product itself.

## The Citations

As Harvard Professor **Theodore Levitt** is often quoted as saying, *"Marketeers sell the drill, but customers buy the hole,"* in his 1960 work, *Marketing Myopia*. We must therefore show customers what life can look like *without* the problem they are seeking a solution for.

Or as renowned author and management consultant **Peter Drucker** famously said, *"The customer rarely buys what the business thinks it sells them."*

Research shows that more than anything, shoppers want to feel seen and understood. **Accenture Interactive** (now called Accenture Song), in their international study of **8,000 consumers**, notes that **87% of consumers say it's important to buy from a brand or retailer that "understands the real me."** They crave an emotional connection with the brands they choose to support.

> They're asking, "Does this brand get me? Do they understand my challenges and aspirations? Are they offering something that genuinely improves my life?" For brands, the answers to these questions determine whether a customer clicks "Add to Cart" or walks away. Or, as I like to say, **What Captures the Heart Goes in the Cart.**

## Coaching Application
Take any product the brand owner sells and ask: "What's the hole the customer is buying?" Force the answer to be an outcome (confidence, peace of mind, belonging), not a feature. Then check the listing — if it sells the drill and not the hole, rewrite it.
