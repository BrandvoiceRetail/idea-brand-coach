# SKILL — The Emotional Triggers Table

## Purpose
The book's master reference table of emotional triggers — each paired with its expanded psychological effect and a copy-ready example message. This is the single most-used tactical asset in the chapter: it turns the "buying is emotional" principle into concrete, deployable messaging. Reproduced verbatim, every row. Use it to diagnose which trigger a piece of copy is (or isn't) pulling, and to generate on-brand example messaging.

---

## The Table

| Emotional Trigger | Expanded Psychological Effect | Example Messages |
|---|---|---|
| **Survival & Fear of Loss** | Triggers immediate action due to loss aversion. People are wired to avoid loss more than they seek gain. Creating urgency (limited stock, flash sales, FOMO) triggers instant decision-making. | "Only 3 left in stock – order now before it's gone!" |
| **Pain Avoidance & Problem-Solving** | Addresses frustration, discomfort, or stress, leading to impulse purchases. Consumers are more motivated to escape discomfort than to seek pleasure. | "Tired of joint pain? Our supplement provides fast, lasting relief." |
| **Social Belonging & Acceptance** | Creates trust, social proof, and brand loyalty. Humans crave inclusion and feel validated by social proof. | "Join over 500,000 happy customers who trust our brand for their health needs!" |
| **Status, Prestige, & Aspiration** | Elevates brand's perceived value, making it desirable. People associate luxury and exclusivity with high status. | "Worn by elite athletes and high performers worldwide—be part of the movement." |
| **Trust & Credibility** | Increases conversion rates by reducing hesitation and skepticism. Customers need proof before they buy. | "Clinically tested, third-party verified, and doctor-approved for your safety." |
| **Instant Gratification & Convenience** | Drives impulse buying, especially in health, beauty, and tech. The brain craves quick wins and immediate rewards. | "Get results in just 7 days or your money back! Instant download available." |
| **Hope & Transformation** | Drives emotional connection, positioning the brand as a guide to success. Customers are buying a better version of themselves. | "Transform your body in 30 days with our science-backed nutrition program." |
| **Curiosity & Intrigue** | Encourages engagement, clicks, and conversions through psychological pull. The brain hates unanswered questions. | "Discover the one secret top fitness trainers use that nobody talks about!" |
| **Reciprocity & Guilt** | Increases customer commitment, making them more likely to purchase. When someone receives value for free, they feel a subconscious need to reciprocate. | "Get a free guide with your first purchase—our gift to you!" |
| **Nostalgia & Sentimentality** | Triggers deep emotional connections, especially in branding. Memories are deeply emotional. | "Bringing back the classic taste you loved as a kid—now in a protein shake!" |
| **Surprise & Delight** | Increases customer retention and satisfaction. Unexpected gifts create positive emotional spikes. | "Surprise bonus inside every order—because we love to delight our customers!" |
| **Empowerment & Control** | Appeals to self-reliant and goal-driven individuals. Consumers want to feel in control of their choices. | "Take control of your health with personalized nutrition plans designed for you." |
| **Fun & Entertainment** | Adds emotional uplift and delight to the buying experience. Brands that spark joy or inject playfulness create memorable moments, making customers associate the product with happiness and spontaneity. This fosters positive word-of-mouth and increases repeat engagement. | "Turn everyday routines into mini-adventures with our playful designs—because fun isn't just for weekends!" |

## Coaching Application
Run any headline, ad, or listing bullet against the table: which trigger is it firing, and is the psychological effect actually present? Empty copy fires no trigger. Match the trigger to the customer's emotional state (use the Avatar 2.0 outputs), then adapt — don't copy — the example message to the brand's voice. Avoid stacking too many triggers in one message; pick the dominant one.
