# SKILL — Insight Step 2: Behavioral and Emotional Data

## Purpose
The second step of the Insight pillar: combine qualitative and quantitative research to gather both behavioral data (what customers *do*) and emotional data (how customers *feel*). Use it to make sure a brand owner isn't reading only one half of the picture.

---

## Combine the Quantitative and the Qualitative

Brands that succeed with insight-driven strategies often combine **qualitative research** (such as focus groups or customer interviews) with **quantitative data** (such as purchasing patterns, website analytics, or social media engagement).

This combination enables them to gather two distinct kinds of insight:

- **Behavioral data — what customers do.** Purchasing patterns, click behavior, engagement.
- **Emotional data — how customers feel.** The motivations, frustrations, and aspirations behind the behavior.

## Coaching Application
Most brand owners over-index on one source: either gut-feel interviews or cold analytics. The instruction is to triangulate. When reviewing a brand's research, ask for both — what the data says customers *do*, and what conversations reveal about how they *feel*. The "why" lives in the gap between the two.
