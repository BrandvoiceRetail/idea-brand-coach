# SKILL — Insight Step 1: Understand Your Customer Avatar

## Purpose
The first step of the Insight pillar: build a multi-dimensional view of your target audience using the Avatar 2.0 tool, going past demographics into motivations, emotional triggers, and shopping behaviors. Carries the skincare/Millennials worked example. Use it to position Avatar 2.0 outputs as the raw material that feeds the Insight quadrant.

---

## Build a Multi-Dimensional Avatar

Use the **Avatar 2.0 tool** (introduced later in the book) to build a multi-dimensional view of your target audience. While traditional demographic data (age, income, education) is important, Avatar 2.0 digs deeper into your customer's **motivations, emotional triggers, and shopping behaviors.** This understanding allows you to tailor your brand messaging and positioning to align with their values and needs.

> The outputs of the Avatar 2.0 tool sit in the **Insight quadrant** of the framework.

## Worked Example

A **skincare brand targeting Millennials** might discover through Avatar 2.0 that their customers are motivated by the desire for healthy, natural products, driven by concerns about sustainability and self-care. This insight allows the brand to position itself not just as a skincare solution, but as part of a larger **lifestyle choice** aligned with wellness and environmental responsibility.

## Coaching Application
When a brand owner describes their customer in demographic terms only ("women, 25–40, mid-income"), that's not an avatar — it's a census row. Push them to fill in the deeper layers: what motivates this person, which emotional triggers move them, how do they shop? The skincare example shows the payoff — demographics alone would never surface "position as a lifestyle, not a product."
