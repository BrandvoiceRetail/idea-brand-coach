# SKILL — Applying Distinctiveness (Three Moves)

## Purpose
The three practical moves for putting the Distinctiveness pillar to work: identify your differentiators, communicate one clear message, and reinforce distinction at every touchpoint. Carries the Patagonia and Volvo examples. Use it as the action checklist after the brand owner understands difference, distinctiveness, and salience.

---

## 1. Identify Your Differentiators
Think about what makes your brand truly different. This could be based on:
- **The product itself** (e.g., a proprietary formula or technology)
- **The experience you offer** (e.g., customer service, delivery, or personalization)
- **Your brand's values and mission** (e.g., sustainability, inclusivity, or social impact)

**Example: Patagonia** differentiates itself not just through its high-quality outdoor gear, but through its unwavering commitment to environmental activism. Its distinctiveness comes from the brand's values. It's not just selling clothes — it's promoting a sustainable, eco-conscious lifestyle.

## 2. Communicate One Clear Message
Simplicity wins in branding. Once you've identified what makes you unique, distil it into **one clear, simple message** that can be communicated across all customer touchpoints. This helps customers easily remember and recognize your brand.

**Example: Volvo** has positioned itself around the concept of **safety.** While many car brands emphasize features like speed or luxury, Volvo has consistently owned the "safety" message, ensuring that whenever consumers think about vehicle safety, they think of Volvo.

## 3. Reinforce Distinction at Every Touchpoint
It's not enough to have a distinctive idea — you need to reinforce it at **every touchpoint** in the customer journey. From your website design and packaging to social media presence and advertising, every interaction with your brand should remind customers of what makes you unique.

## Coaching Application
Walk the brand through the three moves in order: name the differentiator (product, experience, or values), distil it to one message (Volvo = safety), then audit every touchpoint for consistency. The most common failure is move 3 — a great message that isn't reinforced on the packaging, the site, the ads. Fragmentation kills distinctiveness.
