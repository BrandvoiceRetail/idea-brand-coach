# SKILL — Staying Authentic in Product Development

## Purpose
The four tactics for keeping a product authentic. Preserve the worked examples — Aesop (align with mission), Everlane (be transparent), Lego Ideas (customer involvement), and IKEA (consistency across touchpoints).

---

## The Tactics

### 1. Align Products with Brand Mission
Every product you develop should reflect the **core mission** of your brand. Whether your brand is focused on sustainability, innovation, or inclusiveness, each product needs to reinforce those values. Authenticity is built when **there is no disconnect between what you claim to stand for and what your products actually deliver.**
> Example: Aesop, a skincare brand known for its commitment to quality and minimalist design, ensures every product aligns with its philosophy of sustainability and sensory experience. Using plant-based ingredients, recyclable packaging, and a deliberate rejection of mass advertising, Aesop maintains an authentic, understated luxury that resonates with customers who value thoughtful, ethically made products.

### 2. Be Transparent in Product Development
Customers increasingly expect **transparency** about how products are made, where materials are sourced, and what impact they have on the world. Being open about your development process — even when it involves challenges or trade-offs — builds trust and reinforces authenticity.
> Example: Everlane (www.everlan.com), the fashion brand, has built its reputation on radical transparency. The company discloses the true cost of producing each item, from materials to labor, and shares details about the factories where their products are made. This level of openness helps build an authentic connection with customers who value ethical production practices.

### 3. Authenticity through Customer Involvement
Involving your customers in the development process is another way to stay authentic. Whether through customer surveys, beta testing, or crowdsourcing ideas, inviting your audience to be part of the process not only ensures products meet their needs but also fosters a **sense of ownership and loyalty.**
> Example: Lego (www.lego.com) frequently involves its fan community in product development. Through its Lego Ideas platform, fans can submit their own designs for potential new sets, and the community votes on their favorites. The most popular ideas are considered for production, allowing Lego to create products that are directly shaped by its most passionate customers.

### 4. Consistency Across All Touchpoints
Authenticity also means maintaining **consistency** across all touchpoints — from how you develop products to how you communicate with customers. Every aspect of your brand should reflect the same values, whether it's your product design, marketing campaigns, or customer service.
> Example: IKEA maintains consistency across all touchpoints by staying true to its mission of providing affordable, well-designed, and sustainable home solutions. From its flat-pack furniture and Scandinavian aesthetics to its in-store experience, website, and eco-friendly initiatives, every aspect of IKEA reflects its core values of functionality, accessibility, and sustainability, reinforcing its brand authenticity worldwide.

## Coaching Application
Walk a product through the four tactics: Does it advance the mission (Aesop)? Is the process open, including the hard parts (Everlane)? Were customers involved in shaping it (Lego Ideas)? Does it carry the same values across every touchpoint (IKEA)? Customer involvement does double duty — it both keeps the product authentic and builds the ownership that turns buyers into advocates.
