# SKILL — Authenticity in Product Development

## Purpose
The fourth IDEA pillar applied to product development: in an era of consumer skepticism, every product must align with the brand's stated values. Preserve the definition — authenticity is consistency, transparency, and genuine reflection.

---

## The Principle
Product development should always be **authentic**, staying true to your brand's core values and mission. Authenticity is critical to building trust — particularly in an era where **consumers are increasingly skeptical of brands that don't live up to their promises.** When developing new products, ensure each one aligns with your brand's values and meets the expectations your customers have come to trust.

## The Definition
> "Authenticity in product development is about **consistency, transparency, and creating products that genuinely reflect what your brand stands for.**"

## Coaching Application
Use authenticity as a gate, not a garnish: before shipping a product, confirm there is **no disconnect** between what the brand claims to stand for and what the product actually delivers. In a skeptical market, the gap between promise and product is what customers punish. The four staying-authentic tactics show how to close it.
