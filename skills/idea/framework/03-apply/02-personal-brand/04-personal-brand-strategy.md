# SKILL — Personal Brand Strategy

## Purpose
The capstone: combine all four IDEA pillars into one actionable, sequenced strategy. The asset is the five-step strategy — preserve it verbatim as the execution plan that turns the pillar work into a running practice.

---

## The Five-Step Strategy
Here's how you can combine all elements of the IDEA Framework into a clear, actionable strategy:

### Start with Reflection
Define who you are, what you offer, and whom you serve. Write a one-sentence statement summarizing your brand.

### Identify Key Platforms
Decide where to focus your efforts (LinkedIn, Instagram, YouTube, etc.) based on your audience's preferences.

### Build a Content Plan
Create a 90-day content calendar with a mix of stories, educational posts, and audience engagement activities.

### Engage Consistently
Respond to comments, interact with followers, and participate in relevant conversations online.

### Monitor and Adapt
Use tools like Google Analytics or platform insights to track your audience's engagement and adjust your strategy as needed.

## Why It's Worth the Effort
> "Building your personal brand with the IDEA Framework takes time, but the payoff is enormous. It establishes you as a trusted authority, attracts the right opportunities, and strengthens your relationships with your audience."

By focusing on insights, distinction, empathy, and authenticity, you'll craft a brand that not only stands out but also genuinely connects. In doing so, you'll create a legacy that goes beyond your profession, influencing and inspiring those who follow you.

## Coaching Application
This is the run-order. The one-sentence statement in Step 1 is the compression of all prior pillar work (UVP + mission + values) — if the founder can't write it, an earlier pillar is incomplete; loop back. The 90-day calendar is the only step with a concrete deliverable, so anchor execution there. Steps 4–5 make it a practice, not a launch: consistent engagement plus a measure-and-adjust loop. Use "Why It's Worth the Effort" to re-motivate when the slow payoff tempts the founder to quit.
