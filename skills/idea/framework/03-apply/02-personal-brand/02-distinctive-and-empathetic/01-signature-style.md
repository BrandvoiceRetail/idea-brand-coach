# SKILL — Signature Style

## Purpose
The second Distinctive step: make your visual identity and tone consistent and recognizable. Includes the style-guide action plan and the three-adjectives tone exercise.

---

## Make Your Identity Consistent
> "Your brand's visual identity and tone should be consistent and reflective of your personality."

- Choose colors, fonts, and design elements that align with your values.
- Decide on your voice—are you formal, conversational, or inspiring?

## Action Plan for Building Consistency
- Use tools like Canva or Adobe Express to design a cohesive brand style guide for social media, presentations, and websites.
- Write down three adjectives that describe your tone (e.g., approachable, confident, and inspiring) and use them to guide your communication.

**Outcome:** A distinctive personal brand ensures that your audience immediately recognizes your value and personality in everything you do.

## Coaching Application
The three-adjective exercise is the cheap, high-leverage move — it gives the founder a portable filter for every post, email, and reply ("does this sound approachable, confident, and inspiring?"). Lock the adjectives before touching visuals. The style guide (Canva/Adobe Express) operationalizes consistency across channels so recognition compounds.
