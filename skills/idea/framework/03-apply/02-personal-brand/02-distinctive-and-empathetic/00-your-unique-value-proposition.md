# SKILL — Your Unique Value Proposition

## Purpose
The first Distinctive step: name what makes you unique in one specific, non-generic statement. The core asset is the fill-in-the-blank UVP formula and its worked example — preserve both verbatim.

---

## Find What Differentiates You
A distinctive personal brand doesn't just blend in; it captures attention by highlighting what makes you unique.

- Think about what differentiates you from others in your field.
- Focus on your unique perspective, skills, or personal story.
- Avoid generic statements like "I'm passionate about helping people." Be specific.

## Action Plan for Defining Your UVP
**Exercise:** Fill in the blanks:

> "I help [specific audience] achieve [specific outcome] by [unique approach]."

**Example:** "I help small business owners double their sales by creating data-driven marketing strategies that are simple to implement."

## Coaching Application
The whole value of the formula is its three required specifics. If any blank reads generic ("people," "succeed," "great service"), it fails the test — push for a named audience, a measurable outcome, and a distinctive method. The example is the bar: "small business owners" / "double their sales" / "data-driven marketing strategies that are simple to implement." Draft the founder's UVP straight from their self-reflection (the offer) and audience map (the need); iterate until every blank survives a "be specific" challenge.
