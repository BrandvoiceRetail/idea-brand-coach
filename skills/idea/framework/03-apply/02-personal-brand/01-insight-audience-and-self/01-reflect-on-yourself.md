# SKILL — Reflect on Yourself

## Purpose
The second half of the Insight pillar: define your own strengths, passions, and unique qualities so the brand is authentic. Two verbatim exercises — the self-reflection questions and the personal mission statement — that resolve into the audience-self intersection.

---

## Why Self-Reflection
> "Your personal brand must be authentic, so it's crucial to define your strengths, passions, and unique qualities."

### Ask Yourself
- What am I passionate about?
- What skills or experiences set me apart?
- What values and messages do I want to share with the world?

## Action Plan for Self-Reflection
**Exercise:** Write a personal mission statement. Answer these questions:

- What do I do?
- Who do I serve?
- How do I make a difference?

**Outcome:** By combining audience insights with self-reflection, you'll define the intersection of what your audience needs and what you authentically offer.

## Coaching Application
The two question sets do different jobs: the first three surface raw material (passion, edge, message); the mission-statement three compress it into a usable statement. Run them in order. The real output is the **intersection** — overlay this against the audience Empathy Map and look for where genuine offer meets real need. That overlap is the brand's center of gravity.
