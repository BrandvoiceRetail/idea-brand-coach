# SKILL — Understand Your Audience

## Purpose
The first half of the Insight pillar for a personal brand: deeply understand who you're speaking to before you craft anything. Ends in the Empathy Map exercise — preserve it verbatim as the deliverable.

---

## The Three Moves
Insight is the foundation of any strong personal brand. To craft a brand that connects, you must deeply understand two things: your audience and yourself. This step covers the audience.

### Identify Their Needs
What problems does your audience face? What are their goals, and how can you help them achieve them?

### Segment Your Audience
Not everyone will resonate with your brand. Use the IDEA Customer Avatar 2.0 to create a detailed profile of your ideal audience, including their challenges, values, and emotional triggers.

### Listen and Observe
Engage with your audience on platforms like LinkedIn, Instagram, or industry forums. Take note of their questions, concerns, and interests.

## Action Plan for Understanding Your Audience
**Exercise:** Create an "audience empathy map." Write down what your ideal customer:

- **Thinks**
- **Feels**
- **Says**
- **Does**

## Coaching Application
Run the Empathy Map as a fill-in for the founder's single ideal customer (not a segment in the abstract — one person). Pull directly from their Avatar 2.0 work if it exists; the four quadrants force them past demographics into the customer's inner world. Pair with self-reflection next — insight is the *intersection* of audience need and authentic offer.
