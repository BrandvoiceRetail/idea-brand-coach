# SKILL — Non-Negotiable Values

## Purpose
The first Authentic step: define the principles that guide every decision so the brand stays consistent over time. Includes the top-three-values exercise and the alignment test with its sustainability example.

---

## Define What Guides You
> "Authenticity is the key to sustaining your personal brand over time. People follow and trust those who are genuine, not those trying to emulate someone else."

What principles guide your decisions and actions? Clearly define these values and let them inform everything from your messaging to your business practices.

## Action Plan for Authenticity
- Write down your top three core values.
- Ensure that your content, partnerships, and communication reflect these values. For example, if sustainability is a core value, avoid practices or affiliations that contradict it.

## Coaching Application
Cap it at three — more than three and they stop being "non-negotiable." The test isn't naming the values; it's the alignment audit that follows: walk the founder's actual content, partnerships, and communication and flag anything that contradicts a stated value (the book's example: sustainability as a value rules out contradicting affiliations). Values that don't constrain a real decision aren't doing any work.
