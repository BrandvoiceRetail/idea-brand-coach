# SKILL — Upload Knowledge & Enable Features (Builder Steps 4–5)

## Purpose
The two steps that give the GPT its brand-specific knowledge and its capabilities. Uploading knowledge files (Step 4) is what grounds responses in the brand; enabling features (Step 5) is what extends what the GPT can do.

---

## Step 4: Upload Knowledge Sources
One of the most powerful features of custom GPTs is the ability to upload documents that the AI can reference.

**How to Upload Knowledge Files:**
- In the **Knowledge** section of the GPT builder, click **Upload Files**.
- Select relevant documents, such as:
  - Brand guidelines
  - Style guides
  - Customer personas
  - Product catalogs
- AI will incorporate this information when generating responses.

> Note: If the GPT does not consistently refer to uploaded files, refine the prompt instructions for better accuracy.

## Step 5: Enable Additional Features
Depending on your needs, you can activate advanced capabilities:
- **Web Browsing** — Allows the GPT to search the internet for real-time data.
- **Code Interpreter & Data Analysis** — Enables AI-powered calculations and programming tasks.
- **External API Actions** — Allows the GPT to pull in third-party app data for more dynamic responses.

> Example: Connect your GPT with Zapier integrations to automate workflows across apps like Slack, Google Calendar, and Notion.

## Coaching Application
The IDEA Brand Canvas Worksheet is the single most important file to upload here — it's the knowledge source that makes everything on-brand. If the GPT ignores the uploaded files, the note's fix is correct: tighten the prompt instructions to explicitly reference the documents. Only enable the features the brand actually needs; each one is a surface for off-brand or unverified output.
