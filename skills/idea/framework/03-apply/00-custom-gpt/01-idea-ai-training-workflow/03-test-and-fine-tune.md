# SKILL — Test, Refine, and Fine-Tune the AI (Steps 4–5)

## Purpose
Once the AI is trained, you have to prove it behaves on-brand and then lock that behavior in. This pairs the testing loop (Step 4) with the four fine-tuning parameters (Step 5) that stop the AI from drifting into generic or off-brand output.

---

## Step 4: Test & Refine AI Outputs
After training AI with the IDEA Brand Canvas Worksheet, test it by running sample prompts like those above and reviewing its outputs.

**How to Improve AI Responses Further:**
- Manually correct any inconsistencies.
- Reinforce key messaging principles from the IDEA Brand Canvas Worksheet.
- Refine pre-prompts to ensure AI generates responses that align with the brand's positioning.

## Step 5: Fine-Tune AI for Maximum Accuracy
To prevent AI from generating off-brand or inconsistent messaging, apply fine-tuning parameters:

- **Reinforce Brand Vocabulary** — AI should use key brand phrases, taglines, and messaging styles.
- **Control for Tone Variations** — AI should adjust its tone based on the audience (e.g., casual for social media, authoritative for press releases).
- **Prevent Generic Language** — AI should avoid broad, templated responses and instead deliver brand-specific messaging.
- **Ensure Ethical Guidelines** — AI-generated content must reflect the brand's values (e.g., inclusivity, sustainability).

## Coaching Application
Run real prompts the founder will actually use, not toy ones. When the output drifts, the fix is rarely "the AI is bad" — it is a thin worksheet or a missing fine-tuning parameter. Map each failure: generic copy → Prevent Generic Language; wrong register → Control for Tone Variations; off-vocabulary → Reinforce Brand Vocabulary; risky claim → Ensure Ethical Guidelines.
