# SKILL — The Evolutionary Roots of Triggers

## Purpose
Grounds emotional triggers in evolutionary biology: emotions evolved as adaptive survival mechanisms, and those instincts still drive modern shopping. Use this to explain *why* triggers are so reliable — they're hardwired.

---

## Emotions Are Adaptive Mechanisms
> "From an evolutionary standpoint, emotions evolved as adaptive mechanisms that helped early humans respond to challenges and opportunities."

## Hope → Action, Trust → Cooperation
> "For instance, hope motivates action toward a better future, while trust ensures cooperation within groups—a key to survival in early human communities. These instincts remain with us, influencing even our modern behaviors, including shopping."

## Coaching Application
When you activate hope or trust, you're not inventing a feeling — you're cueing an ancient instinct. That's why these triggers work so consistently: hope still moves people to act, and trust still governs whether they'll cooperate (buy). Design messaging that respects those instincts rather than fighting them.
