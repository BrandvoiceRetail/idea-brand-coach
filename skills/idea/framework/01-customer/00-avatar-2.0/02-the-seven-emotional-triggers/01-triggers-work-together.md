# SKILL — The Triggers Work Together

## Purpose
Explains that the seven emotional triggers are not isolated tricks but a connected system rooted in behavioral science — and that activating them turns transactions into relationships. Use this to frame trigger work as relationship-building, not manipulation.

---

## Rooted in Behavioral Science
> "The concept of emotional triggers originates from foundational theories in behavioral science, particularly the intersection of psychology, sociology, and neuroscience. Emotional triggers are deeply rooted in how humans are wired to respond to stimuli in their environments. These responses are shaped by our evolutionary need to survive, connect, and thrive in social groups."

## Transactions Become Relationships
> "When brands understand and activate these emotional triggers, they move beyond simple transactions to create meaningful relationships. It's no longer about selling a product; it's about connecting with a person."

## The Seven Combine
> "Shoppers today want to feel hope, find belonging, and validation. They seek trust, relief, aspiration, and empowerment. These emotions work together, creating a bond that keeps customers coming back, not just for the product, but for the way it makes them feel."

> "In the end, what shoppers truly want is simple: to be understood, supported, and inspired... Brands that get this don't just sell they serve both their customers' practical and emotional needs."

## Coaching Application
Don't treat the seven triggers as a checklist of separate tactics. They reinforce one another — trust deepens belonging, hope feeds aspiration. Aim to serve both the practical and the emotional need at once, and the relationship (not just the sale) is what compounds.
