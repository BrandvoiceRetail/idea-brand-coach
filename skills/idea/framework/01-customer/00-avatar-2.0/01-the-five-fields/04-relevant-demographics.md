# SKILL — Field 5: Relevant Demographics

## Purpose
The fifth Avatar 2.0 field. Demographics are kept — but reframed as *context* that complements behavioral insight, never as rigid stereotypes. Use generational traits to add nuance to a profile already built on behavior.

---

## Reframe: Context, Not Categories
> "While traditional demographics focus on rigid categories (age, gender, income), Avatar 2.0 reframes them as contextual data points that complement behavioral insights. Generational traits, such as those outlined in cross-generational marketing studies, highlight how shared experiences shape values and preferences."

## Generational Examples
> "For example, Millennials often prioritize purpose-driven brands, while Gen Z seeks authenticity and inclusivity."

## Blend, Don't Stereotype
> "By blending behavioral data with relevant demographics, Avatar 2.0 creates nuanced profiles. A campaign targeting Gen Z's affinity for social justice might feature diverse representation and eco-friendly messaging, resonating deeply with their values."

## Coaching Application
Lead with behavior; use demographics only to add context. Treat generational traits (Millennials' purpose-orientation, Gen Z's demand for authenticity and inclusivity) as shared-experience signals that sharpen an already behavior-rooted profile — never as a substitute for it.
