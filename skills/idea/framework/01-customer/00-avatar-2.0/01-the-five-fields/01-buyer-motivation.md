# SKILL — Field 2: Buyer Motivation

## Purpose
The second Avatar 2.0 field. Motivation is the deeper, often subconscious reason a customer seeks a product — status, convenience, expression, prestige. Use it to market what the product *signifies*, not just what it does.

---

## Definition
> "Buyer motivation is rooted in the deeper, often subconscious, reasons a customer seeks a product. These motivations can range from the pursuit of convenience to a desire for self-expression or prestige."

## Decisions Happen in the Subconscious (Kahneman)
> "Most decisions, as highlighted by Daniel Kahneman in his book *Thinking, Fast and Slow*, occur in the subconscious, driven by emotional and psychological needs rather than pure logic. Motivations such as social status, convenience, or personal expression often dictate purchasing decisions."

## Market the Meaning, Not the Feature (luxury watch)
> "A brand selling luxury watches, for instance, doesn't just market the watch's technical features but its ability to signify success and exclusivity."

## Dig Into the "Emotional Why" (Zaltman)
> "By integrating insights from tools like Zaltman's metaphor elicitation technique, Avatar 2.0 delves into the 'emotional why' that drives purchase behavior."

## Coaching Application
For each product, name the subconscious motivation it serves — status, convenience, self-expression, or prestige — and lead with that meaning. The technical spec supports the story; it isn't the story. Probe the "emotional why," because that, not logic, dictates the decision.
