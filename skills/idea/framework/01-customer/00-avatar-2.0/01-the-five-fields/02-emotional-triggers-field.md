# SKILL — Field 3: Emotional Triggers

## Purpose
The third Avatar 2.0 field. Emotional triggers are the stimuli that resonate with a customer's feelings and subconscious desires — and emotion, not logic, drives most purchases. Use this field to record which triggers to activate; the full catalogue of seven lives in its own folder.

---

## Definition
> "As we have discussed, emotional triggers are stimuli that resonate with a customer's feelings and subconscious desires. As Martin Lindstrom explains in *Buyology: Truth and Lies About Why We Buy*, emotion outweighs logic in most purchasing decisions."

## Emotion Drives 95% of Decisions (Zaltman)
> "Traditional marketing often focuses on rational appeals, ignoring the fact that emotions drive 95% of decisions (*How Customers Think- Essential Insights into the Mind of the Market* by Professor Gerald Zaltman). Successful brands craft stories and messages that activate these triggers, creating lasting connections."

## How It's Applied
> "Brands can harness emotional triggers through storytelling and strategic framing. For instance, campaigns emphasizing loss aversion ('Don't miss out') or scarcity ('Limited edition') directly influence customer behavior. Using Cialdini's principles like reciprocity and social proof enhances this further by embedding trust and urgency into the narrative."

These triggers are powerful forces shaping how consumers perceive and interact with brands:
> "They're not always conscious, but they're undeniably influential."

## Coaching Application
Treat emotion as the lever, logic as the justification. Activate triggers through storytelling and framing — loss aversion, scarcity, reciprocity, social proof — rather than rational feature lists. To go deeper, work the full set of seven triggers individually.
