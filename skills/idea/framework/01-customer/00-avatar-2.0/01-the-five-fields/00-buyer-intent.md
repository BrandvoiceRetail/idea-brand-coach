# SKILL — Field 1: Buyer Intent

## Purpose
The first Avatar 2.0 field. Buyer intent is the "why" behind a search — the problem the customer is actually trying to solve, not just the words they type. Use it to align listings and product descriptions with what the customer truly needs.

---

## Definition
> "Buyer intent reflects the 'why' behind a customer's actions—the purpose driving their search queries, clicks, or shopping behavior. It's not just about what they are looking for but what problem they are trying to solve."

## Infer Intent From Behavior, Not Categories
> "Unlike traditional models that assume intent based on broad categories, Avatar 2.0 leverages search behaviors, purchase patterns, and contextual data to infer precise needs."

**Worked example:**
> "For instance, a search for 'ergonomic office chair' might reveal not just a functional requirement but an underlying need to address back pain or improve productivity."

## Apply It: Cosmo + Competitor Audits
> "Using AI-driven tools like Cosmo (for intent-focused marketing), brands can optimize content and product descriptions to reflect the language and priorities customers use. This alignment increases visibility and relevance, ensuring that customers feel understood and valued at every touchpoint."

> "Regular competitor audits are highly recommended, both online and in store."

## Coaching Application
For every product, push past the literal search term to the underlying problem. Ask: what is the customer *really* trying to solve? Mirror the customer's own language and priorities in the listing, and audit competitors regularly to spot intent you're missing.
