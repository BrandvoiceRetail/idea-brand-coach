# SKILL — The Shopper's High

## Purpose
Establishes that shopping is part cognitive, part biochemical — purchase decisions are shaped by neurochemistry operating beneath conscious awareness. Use this to justify creating *anticipation* of a future feeling as a core messaging goal.

---

## Shopping Is Cognitive + Biochemical
> "Shopping and decision-making are not solely rational processes; they are deeply influenced by the interplay of emotions and neurochemistry. When we encounter a product, brand, or advertisement, our brains process these stimuli through a combination of cognitive and biochemical reactions."

> "These neurochemical responses shape how we feel, think, and ultimately make purchasing decisions, often operating beneath the surface of conscious awareness."

## The Job: Create Anticipation
> "So, to put it plainly, we have to give our customers a sense of anticipation of how it WILL feel once they get what they need or desire."

## Coaching Application
Sell the future feeling, not just the present features. Your messaging's job is to make the customer *anticipate* how good it will feel once they have the product — that anticipation is itself a biochemical event that drives the decision before logic catches up.
