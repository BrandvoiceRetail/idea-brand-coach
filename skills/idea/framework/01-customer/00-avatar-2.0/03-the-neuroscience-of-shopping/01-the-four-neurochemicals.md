# SKILL — The Four Neurochemicals

## Purpose
Maps the four neurochemicals that emotional triggers activate, with what each does and how it shows up in shopping. Use this to connect a chosen emotional trigger to the brain chemistry it fires — and therefore to the message that will move the buyer.

---

> "Emotional triggers in marketing—whether a sense of hope, belonging, or relief—are not just abstract concepts; they activate specific neurochemical reactions in the brain that influence behavior. These biochemical processes help explain why some messages resonate so powerfully, driving us to act or buy without fully understanding why."

## The Four Neurochemicals (verbatim)

| Neurochemical | What It Does | In Shopping |
|---|---|---|
| **Dopamine** — The Driver of Desire and Reward | The brain's "reward" neurotransmitter, released when we anticipate or achieve something pleasurable. It fuels motivation, makes us feel good, and reinforces behaviors we associate with positive outcomes. | Advertisements or products that evoke **hope**—the promise of a better life, a solution to a problem, or the excitement of exclusivity—trigger dopamine. This creates anticipation, motivating shoppers to click "buy now" to experience that reward. |
| **Oxytocin** — Building Trust and Connection | Often called the "bonding hormone," it fosters feelings of trust, safety, and connection. It's the same chemical released during social bonding moments like hugging or sharing meaningful experiences. | Brands that emphasize **belonging, community, or shared values** tap into oxytocin. Messaging that shows how a product connects you to a group or aligns with your identity fosters trust and strengthens emotional attachment. |
| **Cortisol** — Reducing Stress for Comfort and Empowerment | The body's stress hormone, which spikes during tension or anxiety. **Reducing** cortisol creates a sense of relief and calm, allowing people to feel more in control. | Products that offer **relief**—from physical discomfort, emotional stress, or practical challenges—help lower cortisol. Messaging like "easy returns" or "hassle-free solutions" reassures customers, making them feel empowered and confident. |
| **Serotonin** — Reinforcing Positivity and Contentment | Stabilizes mood, promoting feelings of happiness and well-being. Often linked to moments of gratitude, satisfaction, or accomplishment. | Marketing that taps into **gratitude**—testimonials, success stories, or rewards programs—can trigger serotonin, leaving customers with a sense of contentment about their purchase decision. |

## Coaching Application
Pick the trigger, then aim for its chemical: hope/exclusivity for a dopamine hit (buy-now urgency), belonging/shared values for oxytocin (trust and attachment), relief/reassurance to drop cortisol (risk-free confidence), gratitude/social proof for serotonin (post-purchase contentment).
