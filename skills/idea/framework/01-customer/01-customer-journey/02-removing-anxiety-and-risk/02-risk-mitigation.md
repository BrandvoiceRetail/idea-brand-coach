# SKILL — Risk Mitigation as Reassurance

## Purpose
A short principle reaffirming that customers need reassurance to feel confident, and that removing or minimizing risk reduces friction and helps them move forward. It closely overlaps with anticipating barriers, but the book flags it as worth noting in its own right.

---

## The Principle
> "As discussed earlier, customers often need reassurance to feel confident in their decision. By removing or minimizing risk, brands can reduce friction and help customers move forward. Offering things like free trials, guarantees, or lenient return policies lowers the perceived risk of trying something new. In many cases this is similar to anticipating barriers to purchase but is worth noting."

## Coaching Application
At any point a customer hesitates, ask: "What reassurance would make this feel safe?" Then add the matching device — free trial, guarantee, or lenient return policy. Risk mitigation is the reassurance layer that sits alongside anticipating barriers; both lower perceived risk, so deploy them together at the Decision stage.
