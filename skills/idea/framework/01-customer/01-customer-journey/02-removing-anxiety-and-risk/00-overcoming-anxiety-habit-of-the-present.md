# SKILL — Overcoming Anxiety and the "Habit of the Present"

## Purpose
The single biggest barrier to adoption is customer anxiety — the fear of changing from an existing solution. Bob Moesta's "habit of the present" names why: comfort with current habits. The counter-move is to frame the new product as familiar *and* better, while reducing friction in the transition.

---

## Anxiety Is the Top Adoption Barrier
> "One of the biggest obstacles to product adoption is customer anxiety—the fear of trying something new or changing from an existing solution. To encourage customers to choose your product, you must address these anxieties head-on and reduce friction points throughout the customer journey."

## The "Habit of the Present"
> "People are often reluctant to change because they are comfortable with their existing habits. This is described as the 'habit of the present' by Bob Moesta in his Jobs-To-Be-Done Framework. Even if a new product offers better features, customers may feel anxious about learning how to use it or adapting to a new process."

## The Counter-Move: Familiar + Better, Low Friction
> "Brands can address these anxieties by framing their product in a way that feels familiar and accessible, while also demonstrating how the new solution will lead to a better outcome. Additionally, brands can reduce friction by making the transition as easy as possible—whether through intuitive design, step-by-step onboarding, or clear instructional content."

## Examples — Apple iPhone & Slack
> *iPhone:* "When Apple launched the iPhone, there was anxiety about switching from traditional phones to a touchscreen device. Apple alleviated this anxiety by making the iPhone's interface highly intuitive, using design elements that made the transition feel natural. The introduction of the App Store further reduced anxiety by offering users a vast selection of tools that enhanced their daily lives."

> *Slack:* "When Slack entered the workplace communication market, many companies were hesitant to move away from email, fearing disruption to existing workflows. Slack reduced this anxiety by designing an interface that felt familiar, integrating seamlessly with tools like email and project management software, and offering an intuitive onboarding experience. By emphasizing improved collaboration without forcing users to abandon their current habits, Slack eased the transition and became a widely adopted workplace tool."

## Coaching Application
When you ask a customer to switch, name the "habit of the present" they're giving up, then make your product feel like a small, familiar step — not a leap. Show it's *better* and *easy to start*: intuitive design, step-by-step onboarding, clear instructions. Anxiety about change, not lack of features, is usually what kills the switch.
