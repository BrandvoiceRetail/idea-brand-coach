# SKILL — Framing the Alternatives and the Real Competitive Set

## Purpose
Customers weigh more than your direct competitors — they compare any product or service that solves the same problem. Looking at competitors through the *problem* lens (not the product category) reveals the real competitive set and shows where your product truly fits.

---

## Real Alternatives Beyond Direct Competitors
> "Another critical part of customer-centric product development is understanding the real alternatives your customers are considering. Many brands focus too narrowly on direct competitors, but in reality, customers may be weighing other types of products or services to solve the same problem."

## The Problem-Lens Competitive Set
> "Looking at competitors through the lens of the problem the customer is trying to solve—rather than the specific product category—offers valuable insights. For example, someone trying to improve their health might compare gym memberships, at-home fitness equipment, fitness apps, or even dietary supplements. All of these are potential solutions to the same problem, and brands need to understand where their product fits in this broader competitive landscape."

## Example — Airbnb's Broad Competition
> "When developing Airbnb the founders recognized that their competition wasn't just hotels—it was any form of accommodation, including hostels, vacation rentals, and even staying with friends. By understanding this, Airbnb positioned itself as a unique experience, offering not just a place to stay but a way to experience living like a local."

## Coaching Application
Define your competitive set by the *problem* your customer is solving, not your product category. List every alternative they'd weigh — including doing nothing, or a non-obvious substitute. That broader map shows the real trade-offs customers face and tells you which position (e.g., "experience," not "a place to stay") actually differentiates you.
