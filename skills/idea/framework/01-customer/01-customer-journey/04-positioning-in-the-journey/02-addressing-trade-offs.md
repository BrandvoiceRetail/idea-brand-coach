# SKILL — Addressing Trade-Offs

## Purpose
The perfect product is rare; customers make trade-offs — price, convenience, features, or reputation — when choosing one solution over another. Identify the trade-offs your customers weigh, then craft messaging that addresses those concerns and positions your product as the best option.

---

## Customers Make Trade-Offs
> "The perfect product or solution is a very rare beast. Customers may want more of one or another aspect of the product or service but may not want to pay a premium price, so they inevitably make trade-offs when choosing one solution over another. These trade-offs might include price, convenience, features, or brand reputation. By identifying the trade-offs your customers are considering, you can craft messaging that addresses these concerns and positions your product as the best option."

## Example — Dropbox
> "When positioning Dropbox against competitors, the company focused on the trade-off between ease of use and price. Dropbox emphasized its simplicity and accessibility—making file sharing as easy as 'drag and drop'—while offering a freemium model that eliminated the price barrier for many users."

## Coaching Application
Name the specific trade-off your customer is weighing — price vs. features, convenience vs. control, premium vs. value — and address it head-on in your messaging. Like Dropbox neutralizing the price barrier with a freemium model while owning "ease of use," win the trade-off that matters most to your segment instead of pretending none exists.
