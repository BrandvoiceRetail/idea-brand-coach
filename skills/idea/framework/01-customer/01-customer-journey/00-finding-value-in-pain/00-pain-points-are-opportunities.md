# SKILL — Pain Points Are Value Opportunities

## Purpose
The chapter's framing premise: the customer journey is non-linear, guided by "signposts," and shaped by both conscious and subconscious needs. Customer pain points are not obstacles to route around — they are the openings where a brand creates value, by addressing the *emotional* struggle behind the functional task.

---

## The Journey Is Non-Linear
> "In marketing, guiding customers from their first awareness of a product to making a purchase is a nuanced process. Rather than following a straight path, the customer journey unfolds in a series of interconnected experiences, shaped by both conscious and subconscious needs. Successful brands recognize these emotional triggers and respond in ways that make the journey feel seamless and personal."

> "Customers rarely move in a linear progression. Instead, they encounter pivotal moments—what we call 'signposts'—that guide them toward a solution that meets their needs or desires. The challenge for brands isn't just to create outstanding products but to ensure they are positioned as the most relevant choice at these defining moments."

## Pain Points Create Value
> "Customers often experience frustration, confusion, or discomfort as they search for a resolution. These issues create opportunities for brands to introduce solutions that address not just the functional task but also the emotional struggle behind it. It's by recognizing these problems that brands can truly create value for customers."

## Coaching Application
Map the journey as a series of emotional signposts, not a straight funnel. For each point of friction, name two things: the **functional task** the customer is trying to complete, and the **emotional struggle** underneath it. The struggle is where the value lives — solve the functional task while answering the emotion, and the moment becomes an opportunity instead of a leak.
