# SKILL — Look for Problems, Not Products

## Purpose
A discovery tactic that inverts product thinking: don't innovate for novelty's sake — hunt for desires and problems in customers' lives, then build the product that solves them. Solving a real pain point raises the product's *perceived value* in the customer's mind.

---

## The Tactic
> "Rather than focusing on creating new products for the sake of innovation, brands should focus on identifying desires or problems in their customers' lives. By doing so, they can develop products that are directly tied to solving real pain points, increasing the product's perceived value in the customer's mind."

## Example — Dyson
> "Dyson didn't set out to create just another vacuum cleaner. Dyson identified the frustration people felt with traditional vacuum designs—losing suction, being difficult to maneuver, and constantly clogging. The result was a vacuum that eliminated these pain points, positioning Dyson as an innovator in the market."

## Coaching Application
Before scoping a new product or feature, write down the *problem* it removes — in the customer's own words — not the product spec. If you can only describe what it *is* and not what frustration it *eliminates*, you're innovating for novelty. The clearer the pain you remove, the higher the perceived value.
