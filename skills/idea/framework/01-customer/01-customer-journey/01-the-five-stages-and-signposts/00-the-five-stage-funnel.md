# SKILL — The Five-Stage Customer Funnel

## Purpose
The five common stages of the customer journey (the sales/purchase funnel) and their definitions. The load-bearing claim: customers NEVER buy randomly — every decision carries motivations and emotional triggers that move them forward.

---

## The Five Stages
> "The customer journey typically consists of several stages, often called the sales funnel or purchase funnel. While every customer's journey is unique, the common stages include:"
>
> - **Awareness:** The customer realizes they have a problem or need.
> - **Consideration:** The customer starts exploring possible solutions.
> - **Decision:** The customer chooses which solution or product to purchase.
> - **Retention:** After the purchase, the brand works to keep the customer's loyalty.
> - **Advocacy:** A loyal customer becomes an advocate for the brand, recommending it to others.

## Customers Never Buy Randomly
> "Customers NEVER buy randomly. Each decision to buy is accompanied by motivations and emotional triggers that help move the customer forward. Understanding these motivations is crucial for brands looking to create authentic and effective customer experiences. These motivations serve as the 'Signposts' that guide the way."

## Coaching Application
Place every customer-facing asset on one of the five stages, then ask what motivation moves the customer to the *next* stage. If you can't name the motivation, the asset isn't doing its job. The stages are the map; the signposts (next skill) are the emotional fuel that moves customers between them.
