# SKILL — Brand Voice Through the Journey

## Purpose
How to flex brand voice — tone, language, and design — across the journey so it taps the emotion of each stage. Tone shifts by stage; language stays simple, emotional, and relatable; the result is a seamless emotional journey from first-time buyer to loyal advocate.

---

## Voice Taps the Journey's Emotions
> "A critical component of guiding customers through these emotional Signposts is the brand's voice. The brand voice encompasses the overall personality that the brand conveys, including tone of voice, language style, and design elements such as color, typography, and imagery. A well-crafted brand voice can tap into the emotions and motivations driving the customer's journey."

## Tone of Voice: Reflecting Empathy
> "Tone of voice refers to how a brand communicates, not just what it says. Different stages of the customer journey require different tones. A friendly, reassuring tone might work during the decision stage, while a confident tone could be more effective in the consideration stage."
>
> *Example:* "A financial services brand might use a calming and educational tone at the awareness stage to ease fears, then shift to a more empowering tone during the decision phase to boost confidence."

## Language Style: Simple, Emotional, Relatable
> "The style of language plays a key role in creating emotional connections. Brands that use simple, relatable, and emotional language make it easier for customers to understand and feel connected to their product."
>
> *Example:* "Airbnb uses warm, inviting language such as 'Live like a local' to tap into the emotional need for belonging. This simplicity makes customers feel like they're part of a community."

## Conclusion: A Seamless Emotional Journey
> "The customer journey is an emotional process, shaped by motivations and needs that guide each decision. By recognizing these emotional triggers—or, Signposts—brands can create a journey that feels authentic and personal. A strong brand voice, reflecting empathy, simplicity, and thoughtful design, is key to guiding customers through this journey. Brands that align their voice with these emotional Signposts will build lasting relationships with their customers, transforming them from first-time buyers into loyal advocates."

## Coaching Application
Don't write one tone for the whole funnel. Set a tone per stage that matches its Signpost: calming/educational at Awareness, confident at Consideration, friendly/reassuring at Decision. Keep language simple and emotional throughout. Audit each touchpoint — does the voice fit the feeling the customer has *right there*?
