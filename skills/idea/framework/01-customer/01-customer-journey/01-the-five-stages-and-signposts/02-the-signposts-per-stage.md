# SKILL — The Five-Stage Signpost Map

## Purpose
The core map of Chapter 8: each journey stage has an emotional "Signpost" — the feeling the customer needs to fulfill — plus an example move that addresses it. This is the operational heart of the customer journey; preserve and apply it stage by stage.

---

## What a Signpost Is
> "In the IDEA Framework, I call these emotional triggers and mental shortcuts 'Signposts' because they act as key emotional moments that guide customers from one stage of the journey to the next."

## The Signpost Map (verbatim)

> **Awareness:** At this stage, the customer realizes they have a problem or need. The emotional Signpost could be curiosity or frustration, as they seek a solution to something bothering them.
> *Example:* A customer realizes they need a new skincare routine because they feel self-conscious about their skin texture. The emotional Signpost is the desire for self-confidence and self-care. A brand that recognizes this need, rather than just selling a generic "skincare solution," connects on a deeper level.

> **Consideration:** In this stage, the customer actively looks for solutions, comparing options. The emotional Signpost might be hope or anticipation as they seek a product that will meet their needs.
> *Example:* A customer considering different fitness apps wants to feel healthier. One app, using imagery of real people and a warm tone of voice, taps into the customer's aspiration, creating an emotional connection.

> **Decision:** By now, the customer has narrowed their options and is emotionally ready to make a choice. The Signpost is often trust or relief, as they want to feel confident in their decision. They should be anticipating their "Shopper High" as they look forward to making their purchase.
> *Example:* A customer deciding between two travel companies chooses the one that emphasizes customer testimonials and offers a clear refund policy. The emotional Signpost of trust reassures them of a positive experience.

> **Retention:** After purchasing, the customer seeks satisfaction and validation. They want to feel that they made the right choice.
> *Example:* A clothing brand sends a personalized thank-you email after a purchase, including styling tips for the items. This gesture reinforces the customer's satisfaction and makes them feel valued.

> **Advocacy:** In this final stage, the customer feels so connected to the brand that they want to share their positive experience with others. The emotional Signpost might be pride or belonging.
> *Example:* Customers of a tech company post online reviews or share unboxing videos because they feel proud to be part of the brand's community, not because they were asked.

> "Each Signpost reflects an emotional need that customers want to fulfill. Brands that recognize and address these Signposts through their messaging and customer experience can guide customers smoothly through the journey."

## Quick Reference

| Stage | Emotional Signpost | Example Move |
|-------|-------------------|--------------|
| Awareness | Curiosity / frustration | Skincare brand that recognizes the desire for self-confidence, not a generic "solution" |
| Consideration | Hope / anticipation | Fitness app with real-people imagery + warm tone |
| Decision | Trust / relief (+ Shopper High) | Travel company emphasizing testimonials + clear refund policy |
| Retention | Satisfaction / validation | Personalized thank-you email with styling tips |
| Advocacy | Pride / belonging | Customers post reviews / unboxing videos unprompted |

## Coaching Application
For each stage of your funnel, name the Signpost emotion and point to the specific asset that fulfills it. A stage with no Signpost-addressing move is where customers stall. Match the move to the feeling — curiosity at Awareness, trust at Decision, validation at Retention — not the other way around.
