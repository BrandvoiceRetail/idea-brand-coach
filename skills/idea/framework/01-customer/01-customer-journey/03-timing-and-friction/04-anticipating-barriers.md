# SKILL — Anticipating Barriers (8 Barrier→Solution Examples)

## Purpose
To reduce friction, brands must anticipate *why* customers hesitate — by seeing the journey through the customer's eyes. This is the chapter's catalog of eight purchase barriers paired with the brand solution that pre-empts each. Use it to spot hesitation points before the customer hits them.

---

## See the Journey Through the Customer's Eyes
> "To reduce friction, brands must anticipate the reasons why customers might hesitate to make a purchase. This requires looking at the journey through the customer's eyes and identifying points where they might encounter obstacles, doubts, or confusion."

## The Eight Barrier→Solution Examples (verbatim)

> **Eyewear (Warby Parker's Home Try-On Program)**
> Barrier: Customers hesitate to buy glasses online without trying them on.
> Solution: Warby Parker allows customers to order multiple frames to try at home for free before purchasing.

> **Fitness Equipment (Peloton's White-Glove Delivery & Setup)**
> Barrier: Customers worry about assembling heavy, high-tech equipment.
> Solution: Peloton offers professional delivery and setup, ensuring a seamless start.

> **Automotive (Carvana's 7-Day Return Policy)**
> Barrier: Buying a car online feels risky without a test drive.
> Solution: Carvana lets customers return the car within seven days if they're not satisfied, reducing hesitation.

> **Streaming Services (Spotify's Free Tier & Ad-Supported Model)**
> Barrier: Users hesitate to pay for an unfamiliar service.
> Solution: Spotify offers a free, ad-supported version so users can experience the product before subscribing.

> **Grocery Shopping (Instacart's First-Time Order Discount)**
> Barrier: Customers worry about quality and accuracy when ordering groceries online.
> Solution: Instacart offers discounts on first orders to encourage trial and build trust.

> **B2B SaaS (HubSpot's Free CRM & Onboarding Webinars)**
> Barrier: Businesses fear complex onboarding and high upfront costs.
> Solution: HubSpot provides a free CRM and guided onboarding to ease adoption.

> **Luxury Retail (Gucci's Virtual Styling & Try-On Apps)**
> Barrier: High-end purchases feel risky without an in-store experience.
> Solution: Gucci offers augmented reality try-on tools and live virtual consultations.

## Coaching Application
Run a "see it through their eyes" pass: at each step, write the doubt the customer would feel. Then pre-empt it the way these brands do — try-before-you-buy for fit/quality doubt, white-glove setup for assembly worry, generous returns for commitment risk, a free tier for unfamiliar services, first-order incentives for trust-building, free onboarding for complexity fear, virtual/AR try-on for high-end purchases. Anticipated barriers never become abandoned carts.
