# SKILL — Timing-Related Messages (10 Worked Examples)

## Purpose
Ten worked examples of messages that align a product with a specific moment when the customer is most likely to feel the need for it. Each pairs a category and brands with a quoted example message and the timing principle it exploits. Use it as a swipe file for context-driven copy.

---

## The Ten Examples (verbatim)

> **Tax Software (TurboTax, H&R Block)**
> "Tax season is here—file early and get your maximum refund fast!"
> Creates urgency by aligning with tax deadlines.

> **Fitness & Health (Peloton, MyFitnessPal)**
> "New Year, New You—Start Your Fitness Journey Today!"
> Leverages the January fitness resolution mindset.

> **Back-to-School (Apple, Staples, Target)**
> "Get ready for the school year with student discounts on laptops and supplies!"
> Targets students and parents at the peak of back-to-school shopping.

> **Meal Delivery (HelloFresh, Blue Apron)**
> "Too busy to cook this week? Let us handle dinner for you."
> Speaks to busy professionals at stressful moments.

> **Travel (Airbnb, Expedia, Skyscanner)**
> "Book your summer getaway before prices rise!"
> Uses seasonal timing and fear of missing out (FOMO).

> **Retail & E-Commerce (Amazon, Best Buy, Walmart)**
> "Black Friday deals are here—shop now before they're gone!"
> Taps into limited-time shopping events.

> **B2B Software (Salesforce, HubSpot, Monday.com)**
> "Start the quarter strong—streamline your workflow today."
> Appeals to business planning cycles.

> **Streaming Services (Netflix, Disney+, HBO Max)**
> "Cold nights call for great movies—start your free trial today!"
> Connects entertainment to seasonal habits.

> **Insurance (Progressive, Geico, Lemonade)**
> "A new year means new savings—review your insurance rates now!"
> Encourages customers to reassess coverage at a natural reset point.

> **Cybersecurity & Tech (Norton, McAfee, 1Password)**
> "Stay protected this holiday season—secure your accounts before shopping online."
> Ties security to seasonal risks.

> "Each of these messages is effective because it aligns the product with a specific moment when the customer is more likely to feel the need for it."

## Coaching Application
Find the calendar moment or life event when your product's need spikes — a deadline, a season, a reset point, a busy stretch — and write a message that names that moment. The pattern is consistent: state the timing trigger, then the offer. Borrow the closest example above as a template for your own category.
