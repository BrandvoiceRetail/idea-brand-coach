# SKILL — Suggested Further Reading

## Purpose
The book's complete 26-work reading list, reproduced verbatim. Use this as the canonical bibliography when a seller wants to go deeper than the framework, or when sourcing the named authorities the framework draws on.

---

## The 26 Works (verbatim)
- Decoded: The Science Behind Why We Buy by Phil P. Barden
- The Catalyst: How to Change Anyone's Mind by Jonah Berger
- Contagious: Why Things Catch On by Jonah Berger
- Habits of a Happy Brain: Retrain Your Brain to Boost Your Serotonin, Dopamine, Oxytocin, & Endorphin Levels by Loretta Breuning
- Influence: The Psychology of Persuasion by Robert Cialdini
- Thinking, Fast and Slow by Daniel Kahneman
- Hook Point: How to Stand Out in a 3-Second World by Brendan Kane
- Blue Ocean Strategy: How to Create Uncontested Market Space and Make the Competition Irrelevant by W. Chan Kim and Renée Mauborgne
- Don't Make Me Think, Revisited by Steve Krug
- Buyology: Truth and Lies About Why We Buy by Martin Lindstrom
- Building a Story Brand by Donald Miller
- Ogilvy on Advertising by David Ogilvy
- Content Inc.: How Entrepreneurs Use Content to Build Massive Audiences and Create Radically Successful Businesses by Joe Pulizzi
- Alchemy: The Dark Art and Curious Science of Creating Magic in Brands, Business, and Life by Rory Sutherland
- Positioning: The Battle for Your Mind by Al Ries and Jack Trout
- Hacking Growth: How Today's Fastest-Growing Companies Drive Breakout Success by Sean Ellis and Morgan Brown
- Creative Advertising: Ideas and Techniques from the World's Best Campaigns by Mario Pricken
- Breakthrough Advertising by Eugene M. Schwartz
- The Illusion of Choice by Richard Shotton
- Hooked: How to Build Habit-Forming Products by Nir Eyal
- Growth Hacker Marketing: A Primer on the Future of PR, Marketing, and Advertising by Ryan Holiday
- How Customers Think: Essential Insights into the Mind of the Market by Professor Gerald Zaltman
- Made to Stick: Why Some Ideas Survive and Others Die by Chip Heath and Dan Heath
- Shopify_UK_State_of_Commerce_Report_2024
- Using Behavioral Science in Marketing by Nancy Harhut
- Jab, Jab, Jab, Right Hook: How to Tell Your Story in a Noisy Social World by Gary Vaynerchuk

## Coaching Application
- Several of these are the authorities the framework already cites in-text — Cialdini, Kahneman, Lindstrom, Ries & Trout, Zaltman. The rest extend the same emotion-trust-positioning thesis.
- *Alchemy* (Sutherland) appears only here, with no in-text teaching — treat it as reference-only.
