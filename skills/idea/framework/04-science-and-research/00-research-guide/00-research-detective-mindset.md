# SKILL — The Research Detective Mindset

## Purpose
Sets the stance for every research method in the guide: brand insight comes from real data, behaviors, and feedback — not assumptions. Use this when a seller wants to "just go with their gut" on messaging or positioning.

---

## Research Over Assumptions
> "to create a brand that deeply connects with customers, we must understand what drives their decisions, their emotional triggers, and their core motivations. The best way to do this is by analyzing real data, behaviors, and feedback—rather than relying on assumptions."

The research methods that follow exist to **uncover the psychological and emotional drivers behind customer choices** — so brands can refine messaging, positioning, and differentiation. These insights are what make the IDEA Strategic Brand Framework work in practice.

## Think Like a Detective
> "Think like a detective—gather data from multiple sources to uncover hidden insights. You don't need to use every method, but the more comprehensive your research, the better your brand positioning will be."

The detective framing is the operating principle for the whole guide: no single source is enough. Triangulate across reviews, Q&A, social listening, and AI tools to surface what customers feel but rarely state outright.

## Coaching Application
- When a seller asserts what customers "obviously" want, redirect: gather evidence from at least two independent sources before committing to a positioning claim.
- Comprehensiveness beats completeness — you don't need every method, but you do need enough corroboration to trust the pattern.
