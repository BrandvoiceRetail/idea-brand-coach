# SKILL — Lindstrom: Emotion Beats Logic

## Purpose
The book's citation for the claim that emotion outweighs logic in most purchasing decisions — the basis for treating emotional triggers as the primary lever. Grounded only in how the book cites Lindstrom.

---

## Emotion Outweighs Logic (as cited at pp. 69–70)
> "emotional triggers are stimuli that resonate with a customer's feelings and subconscious desires. As Martin Lindstrom explains in *Buyology: Truth and Lies About Why We Buy*, emotion outweighs logic in most purchasing decisions."

The book positions this Lindstrom citation directly alongside Zaltman's 95% statistic — the two anchor the argument that successful brands craft stories and messages that **activate emotional triggers** rather than rely on rational appeals.

## Further Reading Citation
The book lists *Buyology: Truth and Lies About Why We Buy* by Martin Lindstrom in its Suggested Further Reading.

## Coaching Application
- Lindstrom is the book's "emotion > logic" citation specifically in the context of **emotional triggers** — use it to justify trigger-led messaging.
- Pairs with Zaltman (the quantity, 95%) and Kahneman (the mechanism, System 1) to form the book's three-part scientific case.
