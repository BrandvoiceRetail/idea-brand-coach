# SKILL — Cialdini: Influence Principles

## Purpose
The persuasion principles the book invokes to embed trust and urgency into messaging. Cite this when advising on social proof, authority, scarcity, or reciprocity tactics. Grounded only in how the book cites Cialdini.

---

## Reciprocity & Social Proof in Emotional Triggers (as cited at pp. 69–70)
> "Brands can harness emotional triggers through storytelling and strategic framing. For instance, campaigns emphasizing loss aversion ('Don't miss out') or scarcity ('Limited edition') directly influence customer behavior. Using **Cialdini's principles like reciprocity and social proof** enhances this further by embedding trust and urgency into the narrative."

## Social Proof & Authority as Customer-Journey Biases (as cited at p. 139)
The book lists the cognitive biases that shape heuristic decision-making on the customer journey:
> "**Social proof**: People are more likely to follow the behavior of others, especially in uncertain situations.
> **Authority bias**: People tend to trust and follow the recommendations of experts or authority figures.
> **Loss aversion**: People are more motivated to avoid losses than to achieve equivalent gains, making scarcity and urgency powerful motivators."

## Further Reading Citation
The book lists *Influence: The Psychology of Persuasion* by Robert Cialdini in its Suggested Further Reading.

## Coaching Application
- The book pairs Cialdini's principles with framing and storytelling — they amplify an emotional trigger, they don't replace it.
- Reciprocity and social proof build *trust*; scarcity and loss aversion build *urgency*. Use the right lever for the stage.
