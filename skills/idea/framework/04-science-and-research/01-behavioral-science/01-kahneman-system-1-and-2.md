# SKILL — Kahneman: System 1 vs System 2

## Purpose
Explains *why* emotional appeals work so fast — they bypass deliberate reasoning. Cite this to justify emotional, immediate messaging over feature-dense rational copy. Grounded only in how the book cites Kahneman.

---

## Most Decisions Are Subconscious (as cited at p. 69)
> "Most decisions, as highlighted by Daniel Kahneman in his book *Thinking, Fast and Slow*, occur in the subconscious, driven by emotional and psychological needs rather than pure logic. Motivations such as social status, convenience, or personal expression often dictate purchasing decisions."

## Emotional Appeals Bypass System 2 (as cited at p. 75)
> "These neurochemical responses often bypass our logical, deliberate 'System 2' thinking (as described by Daniel Kahneman) and activate our automatic, emotional 'System 1' processes. This is why emotional appeals in marketing can have such an immediate impact, driving decisions before we even consciously process them."

The book attaches concrete examples to this mechanism: a happy-family ad (belonging → oxytocin → trust), a limited-time offer (anticipation → dopamine → action), and a satisfaction guarantee (relief → lower cortisol → risk-free feeling).

## Coaching Application
- Frame messaging for System 1: fast, emotional, immediate — the deliberate System 2 is too slow to be the primary target.
- This is the engine under Zaltman's 95% claim: System 1 is *where* the subconscious decision happens.
