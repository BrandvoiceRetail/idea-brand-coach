# SKILL — Zaltman: 95% of Decisions Are Subconscious

## Purpose
The scientific anchor for the whole emotion-first framework. Cite this whenever someone insists buying is rational. Grounded only in how the book itself cites Zaltman.

---

## The 95% Claim (as cited at p. 41)
> "Professor Gerald Zaltman of Harvard University, in his book *How Customers Think: Essential Insights into the Mind of the Market* (2003), reveals that up to 95% of purchasing decisions are driven by emotions, not rational thought. These decisions occur in the subconscious mind, and we typically justify them later by creating logical explanations."

The book uses this to establish that emotions and subconscious drivers dominate how people perceive, evaluate, and choose.

## Reinforced in Emotional Triggers (as cited at pp. 69–70)
> "Traditional marketing often focuses on rational appeals, ignoring the fact that emotions drive 95% of decisions (*How Customers Think- Essential Insights into the Mind of the Market* by Professor Gerald Zaltman)."

## Metaphor Elicitation (as cited at p. 69)
> "By integrating insights from tools like Zaltman's metaphor elicitation technique, Avatar 2.0 delves into the 'emotional why' that drives purchase behavior."

## Authenticity in a Culture of Distrust (as cited at p. 79)
> "Gerald Zaltman, in his book *How Customers Think*, emphasizes that 95% of consumer decisions occur in the subconscious, making emotional resonance vital."

## Coaching Application
- The 95% figure is the book's master justification for leading with feeling over specs; logic's role is post-hoc justification.
- Zaltman's metaphor elicitation is the named technique the book attaches to uncovering the "emotional why" inside Avatar 2.0.
