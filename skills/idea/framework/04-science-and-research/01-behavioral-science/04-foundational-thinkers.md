# SKILL — Foundational Thinkers (Reference)

## Purpose
A compact reference for the other named thinkers the book cites to support its emotion-and-trust thesis. Each entry is grounded only in the book's own citation — no outside framework detail added.

---

## Freud — The Subconscious Drives Behavior (as cited at p. 41)
> "As Sigmund Freud famously argued, human behavior is often shaped by the subconscious, influenced by hidden biases and psychological triggers we may not even be aware of."

The book uses Freud as the foundation Zaltman then builds on.

## Levitt — Sell the Drill, Buy the Hole (as cited at p. 42)
> "As Harvard Professor Theodore Levit is often quoted as saying, 'Marketeers sell the drill, but customers buy the hole' in his 1960 work, *Marketing Myopia*."

Used to argue brands must show customers life *without the problem* — selling the outcome, not the object.

## Drucker — Customers Buy Something Else (as cited at p. 42)
> "Or as renowned author and management consultant Peter Drucker famously said, 'The customer rarely buys what the business thinks it sells them.'"

## Ries & Trout — Positioning (as cited at pp. 57–58)
> "Ries and Trout emphasize this in their book *Positioning: The Battle for Your Mind*—if you cannot be the first in a category... then find a niche and claim a unique position."

The book's citation for the Distinctiveness pillar: clear positioning via a claimed unique position.

## Bob Moesta — "Habit of the Present" / Jobs-To-Be-Done (as cited at p. 143)
> "People are often reluctant to change because they are comfortable with their existing habits. This is described as the 'habit of the present' by Bob Moesta in his Jobs-To-Be-Done Framework."

Used to explain customer anxiety about switching — the barrier brands must reduce with familiar framing and low-friction onboarding.

## Coaching Application
- These are the book's supporting citations, not full frameworks — invoke them as the author does: Freud + Zaltman for the subconscious, Levitt + Drucker for outcome-over-product, Ries & Trout for positioning, Moesta for switching anxiety.
