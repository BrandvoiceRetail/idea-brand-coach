# SKILL — Insight-Driven Voice (Speaking the Customer's Language)

## Purpose
Applies the Insight pillar to brand voice: build the voice around how the customer wants to be spoken to, not what the brand wants to say. Use this when a seller's copy sounds inward-facing or generic.

---

## Reflect How Customers Want to Be Spoken To
> "A truly effective brand voice begins with insight—understanding what customers need, feel, and respond to. Rather than focusing on what the brand wants to say, an insight-driven voice reflects how customers want to be spoken to, ensuring relevance and resonance."

The starting point is the customer's psychology, not the brand's preferences.

## Mirror the Audience's Language and Tone
> "When a brand mirrors its audience's language and tone, it fosters familiarity and trust."

Customer psychology, emotional triggers, and behavioral patterns all shape brand communication.

## Acknowledge and Validate Emotions
> "Whether addressing excitement, curiosity, or uncertainty, the brand's voice should acknowledge and validate emotions, making every interaction feel personal and meaningful."

## Research How the Audience Communicates
> "To develop an insight-driven voice, brands must analyze how their audience communicates, what words they use, and what emotions drive their decision-making. This research ensures messaging is not only relevant but also deeply compelling, guiding customers naturally through their journey."

## Coaching Application
Have the seller pull real customer language — from reviews, support tickets, social comments — and mirror those exact words and tone in their copy. Then check each line: does it acknowledge the emotion the customer is actually feeling (excitement, curiosity, uncertainty)? If the copy talks about the brand instead of validating the customer's reality, rewrite it.
