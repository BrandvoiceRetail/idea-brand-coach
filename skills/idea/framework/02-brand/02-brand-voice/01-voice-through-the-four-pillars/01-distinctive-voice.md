# SKILL — Distinctive Voice (Standing Out with a Unique Voice)

## Purpose
Applies the Distinctiveness pillar to brand voice: make the voice instantly recognizable so the brand becomes more than a product. Use this when a seller's voice blends in with competitors.

---

## Instantly Recognizable
> "In a crowded market, a distinctive brand voice ensures a brand is instantly recognizable. It goes beyond just having a unique tone—it conveys the brand's identity, reinforcing what makes it different and why it matters."

## Built from Tone, Language, and Visual Identity
> "Distinctiveness is achieved through the strategic use of tone, language, and visual identity. Whether a brand is authoritative and confident, playful and witty, or warm and conversational, this voice should reflect its core values and personality."

These tone choices act as **voice archetypes** — recognizable personalities a brand commits to.

## Infuse the UVP into the Voice
> "The brand's Unique Value Proposition (UVP) should also be infused into its voice—if a brand stands for innovation, its tone should be bold and future-forward; if it champions simplicity, its messaging should be clear and uncluttered."

## Competitor Gap Analysis
> "To establish a distinctive voice, brands must define their personality traits and evaluate competitor voices to identify gaps. A unique, well-crafted voice ensures customers can instantly connect with the brand, making it more than just a product—it becomes an experience."

## Coaching Application
Walk the seller through two steps: (1) name 2–3 personality traits and pick a voice archetype (authoritative/confident, playful/witty, or warm/conversational) that reflects their core values; (2) audit competitor voices and find the gap nobody is occupying. Then pressure-test: does the tone match the UVP? An innovation brand should sound bold and future-forward; a simplicity brand should sound clear and uncluttered.
