# SKILL — Authentic Voice (Staying True to Brand Values)

## Purpose
Applies the Authenticity pillar to brand voice: keep the voice consistent, transparent, and aligned with core values so customers don't sense a sales-driven facade. Use this when a seller's copy feels over-polished or exaggerated.

---

## Consistency, Transparency, Values
> "Authenticity is the foundation of a strong brand voice. Customers today are highly attuned to inauthentic messaging, and brands that feel overly polished or sales-driven risk losing credibility. An authentic voice is consistent, transparent, and aligned with the brand's core values."

## Customers Detect Inauthenticity
Every communication — a product description, a social media post, or a customer interaction — should reflect what the brand truly stands for.

> "Authenticity also requires consistency across all platforms. Customers should experience the same essence of the brand whether they encounter it through an ad, a website, or a customer service representative."

## Align Voice with Mission; Avoid Jargon and Exaggeration
> "To maintain authenticity, brands must align their voice with their mission and avoid exaggerated claims or industry jargon. The most successful brands communicate with clarity, honesty, and a distinct sense of purpose, reinforcing their identity at every touchpoint."

## Coaching Application
Run the seller's copy against two filters: (1) strip exaggerated claims and industry jargon — replace them with clarity and honesty; (2) confirm the voice traces back to the brand's mission. Then test consistency across platforms: an ad, the website, and a customer-service reply should all feel like the same brand. If a customer would sense a "sales mode" switch anywhere, fix it.
