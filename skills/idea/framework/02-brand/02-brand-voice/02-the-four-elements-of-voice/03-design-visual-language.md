# SKILL — Design (A Visual Language)

## Purpose
Shows how design — color, typography, and layout — is the visual extension of brand voice, using color psychology and the TAG Heuer palette. Use this to help a seller make visual choices that carry the same message as their words.

---

## Design Is a Visual Extension of Voice
> "Brand design is a visual extension of voice, reinforcing personality and emotional cues. Every color, typeface, and layout choice convey a message, whether it's about cutting-edge innovation, performance, or timeless elegance."

## Color Psychology
> "Color psychology plays a critical role in shaping perception. Red evokes energy, passion, and urgency, while green conveys prestige and excellence. Black and white offer contrast and sophistication, often used to reflect precision and high performance. TAG Heuer's signature palette of red, green, white, and black embodies speed, endurance, and luxury, perfectly aligning with its motorsport and athletic heritage."

| Color | Evokes |
|---|---|
| **Red** | energy, passion, and urgency |
| **Green** | prestige and excellence |
| **Black and white** | contrast and sophistication (precision, high performance) |

**TAG Heuer's signature palette** of red, green, white, and black embodies speed, endurance, and luxury.

## Typography and Layout
> "Typography further enhances the brand's bold, high-performance identity. TAG Heuer employs sharp, angular typefaces, which convey strength and technical mastery, reinforcing the brand's commitment to precision timing. Its clean, minimal layouts ensure that focus remains on the craftsmanship of its watches and the adrenaline-fueled world of elite sports."

## Cross-Touchpoint Consistency
> "This consistency ensures that whether customers visit the website, see an ad, or walk past a store display, they instantly recognize TAG Heuer's DNA: a fusion of luxury, speed, and uncompromising performance."

## Coaching Application
Have the seller choose colors, typefaces, and layouts that carry the same message as their words. Use color psychology deliberately — red for energy/passion/urgency, green for prestige/excellence, black-and-white for contrast/sophistication — and pick a palette like TAG Heuer's red/green/white/black that maps to the brand's meaning. Then verify the visual DNA is instantly recognizable across website, ad, and physical display.
