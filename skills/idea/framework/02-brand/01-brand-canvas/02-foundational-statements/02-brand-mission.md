# SKILL — Brand Mission

## Purpose
The third brand statement: the actionable steps taken to fulfill the brand's purpose and vision. Captures the definition, the four Key IDEA Principles, the AI prompt, and the worked sample output — all verbatim.

---

## Definition
The brand mission defines the **actionable steps taken to fulfill the brand's purpose and vision.** It reflects how the brand serves its customers while maintaining authenticity and trust.

## Key IDEA Principles
- **Insight-Driven:** Addresses specific customer needs and challenges.
- **Distinctive:** Defines a unique way of delivering value.
- **Empathetic:** Focuses on how the brand improves customers lives.
- **Authentic:** Ensures consistency in messaging, experience, and values.

## AI Prompt (Example)
> "Craft a clear and actionable brand mission statement that details how the company serves its customers while maintaining authenticity and trust. Ensure it highlights the unique value the brand delivers and how it aligns with customers' needs and aspirations, using the IDEA framework to ensure impact and differentiation."

## Sample Output
> "To design innovative, eco-friendly solutions that help individuals and families reduce their environmental footprint without compromising quality or convenience."

## Coaching Application
Mission is the bridge between aspiration (vision) and action — it should read as something the brand *does*, not just believes. Check the sample's structure: it names the action ("design innovative, eco-friendly solutions"), the beneficiary ("individuals and families"), and the without-compromise promise ("without compromising quality or convenience"). Hold the user's mission to that concreteness — vague missions fail the Insight-Driven and Distinctive tests.
