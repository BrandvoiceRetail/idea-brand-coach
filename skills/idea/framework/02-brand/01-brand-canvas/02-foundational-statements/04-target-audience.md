# SKILL — Target Audience

## Purpose
The fifth brand statement: defining who the brand serves by analyzing demographics, psychographics, needs, and buying behaviors. This element links directly back to the Avatar 2.0 work. Captures the definition, the four Key IDEA Principles, the AI prompt, and the worked sample output — all verbatim.

---

## Definition
Defining the target audience means **understanding who the brand serves by analyzing their demographics, psychographics, needs, and buying behaviors.** Refer back to Chapter 3: Avatar 2.0 for a more detailed explanation.

## Key IDEA Principles
- **Insight-Driven:** Identifies real customer motivations and desires.
- **Distinctive:** Focuses on the niche audience that the brand serves best.
- **Empathetic:** Deeply understands customer pain points and aspirations.
- **Authentic:** Builds trust through targeted, relevant communication.

## AI Prompt (Example)
> "Define the ideal target audience beyond basic demographics by exploring their psychographics, buying behaviors, emotional drivers, and aspirations. Use the IDEA framework to ensure a deep understanding of customer motivations and to create an authentic, highly relevant brand positioning."

## Sample Output
> "Eco-conscious Millennials and Gen Z consumers, aged 18-35, who prioritize sustainability, value stylish and practical products, and seek brands aligned with their ethical beliefs."

## Coaching Application
This statement is not a fresh demographic guess — it is the distillation of the completed Avatar 2.0 table. Coach the user to pull intent, motivation, emotional drivers, Shopper Type, and demographics from the Avatar 2.0 outputs into one tight sentence. The sample goes "beyond basic demographics" by layering psychographics ("prioritize sustainability"), behavior ("value stylish and practical products"), and values ("seek brands aligned with their ethical beliefs") onto the age band — hold the user's statement to that same depth.
