# SKILL — Brand Voice

## Purpose
Defines the tone, language, and communication style the brand uses to engage with customers across all platforms. Captures the definition, the four Key IDEA Principles, the AI prompt, and the worked sample output — all verbatim. (Brand voice is expanded into its own full treatment with a case study in Chapter 6.)

---

## Definition
Brand voice defines the **tone, language, and communication style the brand uses to engage with customers across all platforms.**

## Key IDEA Principles
- **Insight-Driven:** Matches the communication style customers respond to best.
- **Distinctive:** Creates a unique and recognizable voice.
- **Empathetic:** Uses language that resonates emotionally with the audience.
- **Authentic:** Ensures consistency across marketing, sales, and support.

## AI Prompt (Example)
> "Describe the brand's tone, language, and communication style to ensure consistent engagement with customers across all platforms. Align the voice with customer psychology and expectations, ensuring it remains distinct, empathetic, and authentic using the IDEA framework."

## Sample Output
> "Optimistic, conversational, and empowering. We speak with clarity and enthusiasm, making sustainability feel exciting and accessible to all."

## Coaching Application
Voice is how the brand personality is expressed in words, so write it after personality is settled. The sample gives both a tone trio ("Optimistic, conversational, and empowering") and a how-we-speak sentence ("We speak with clarity and enthusiasm, making sustainability feel exciting and accessible"). The Authentic principle — "consistency across marketing, sales, and support" — is the operational test: the voice must hold up in a support reply, not just in a hero headline. This is a deliberately brief canvas-level definition; Chapter 6 develops brand voice in full with a detailed case study.
