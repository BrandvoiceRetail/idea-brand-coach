# SKILL — Value Proposition

## Purpose
A clear, compelling statement that defines why a customer should buy from the brand instead of a competitor. Captures the definition, the four Key IDEA Principles, the AI prompt, and the worked sample output — all verbatim.

---

## Definition
The value proposition is **a clear, compelling statement that defines why a customer should buy from the brand instead of a competitor.**

## Key IDEA Principles
- **Insight-Driven:** Understands customer pain points and desires.
- **Distinctive:** Clearly differentiates from competitors.
- **Empathetic:** Addresses both practical and emotional benefits.
- **Authentic:** Reinforces trust through transparency and consistency.

## AI Prompt (Example)
> "Develop a strong value proposition that clearly communicates why customers should choose the brand over competitors. Highlight unique benefits, emotional appeal, and practical value. Ensure alignment with the IDEA framework for differentiation, customer relevance, and trust-building."

## Sample Output
> "Our innovative product line combines modern aesthetics, affordability, and eco-friendly materials to provide guilt-free solutions for everyday living."

## Coaching Application
Positioning says *where you stand*; the value proposition says *why to buy*. The Empathetic principle here is the tell: it must address "both practical and emotional benefits." Note the sample stacks practical value ("modern aesthetics, affordability, and eco-friendly materials") and lands an emotional payoff ("guilt-free solutions for everyday living"). Coach the user to include both — a value prop that lists only features fails the Empathetic test, and one with only feelings fails the practical-value test.
