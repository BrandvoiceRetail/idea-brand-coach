# SKILL — Positioning Statement

## Purpose
A prose-only element (not in the front canvas tables): defines how the brand stands out in the market and why it is the best choice for the target audience. Captures the definition, the four Key IDEA Principles, the AI prompt, and the worked sample output — all verbatim.

---

## Definition
A positioning statement defines **how the brand stands out in the market and why it is the best choice for the target audience.**

## Key IDEA Principles
- **Insight-Driven:** Directly addresses customer pain points and needs.
- **Distinctive:** Highlights the unique solution the brand offers.
- **Empathetic:** Aligns with emotional and practical customer aspirations.
- **Authentic:** Builds credibility through transparency and real brand strengths.

## AI Prompt (Example)
> "Generate a compelling brand positioning statement that differentiates the brand from competitors and highlights the unique value it offers to its target audience. Use the IDEA framework to ensure it directly addresses customer needs, stands out in the market, and builds an emotional connection with customers."

## Sample Output
> "For eco-conscious millennials, our brand provides stylish, sustainable clothing that combines innovative design with ethical practices, reflecting their values and empowering them to make a positive impact on the planet."

## Coaching Application
Positioning sits between the foundational statements and the value proposition — it names the audience, the offering, and the reason-to-choose in one sentence. Note the sample's "For [audience], our brand provides [offering] that [differentiator], [emotional payoff]" shape: it opens with the target ("For eco-conscious millennials"), names the unique solution ("stylish, sustainable clothing that combines innovative design with ethical practices"), and lands the emotional aspiration ("empowering them to make a positive impact"). Hold the user's statement to that structure so it differentiates rather than describes.
