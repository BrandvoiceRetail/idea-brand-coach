# SKILL — Values-Driven Communication

## Purpose
The third of four trust strategies. Align your brand with your customers' personal values — in your actions as well as your messaging, never as a marketing ploy. Preserve the TOMS one-for-one example.

---

## The Strategy
Modern consumers want to support brands that align with their personal values. If your brand stands for something — **sustainability, social justice, or health** — make sure that's reflected in your actions as well as your messaging.

> "This means being authentic and consistent, not using important causes as mere marketing ploys."

## Example — TOMS
> "TOMS built its brand on a one-for-one model, where for every pair of shoes purchased, the company donates a pair to someone in need. This values-driven mission is clear and tangible, and customers feel good about supporting a brand that is actively making a difference in the world."

## Coaching Application
Pick the value the brand can genuinely back with action, not the one that polls best. The test is whether the cause shows up in operations (TOMS donates a pair per pair sold), not just in copy — consumers detect the gap between messaging and action and read it as a ploy. Clear and tangible beats broad and aspirational.
