# SKILL — Transparency and Accountability

## Purpose
The first of four trust strategies for Authentically Human Branding. Be openly transparent about your business practices and own your mistakes when things go wrong — both build the honesty that earns customer trust. Preserve the Everlane example.

---

## The Strategy
Customers are more likely to trust brands that are transparent about their business practices — including **pricing, sourcing, and how they handle data.**

When something goes wrong, such as a product recall or a data breach, brands need to **address the issue directly, own up to their mistakes, and explain how they're going to make things right.**

## Example — Everlane
> "When Everlane calls itself a 'radically transparent' clothing company, it backs this up by openly sharing information about its factories, the true cost of materials, and how much it marks up its products. This level of transparency builds trust, as customers feel like they are being treated with respect and honesty."

## Coaching Application
Audit what the brand currently hides by default — pricing logic, sourcing, data handling — and ask what could be shared openly without harm. Transparency is only credible when it's backed up (Everlane shares factory and cost data, not just the claim). For accountability, have the response-to-failure pattern ready before failure happens: address directly, own it, explain the fix.
