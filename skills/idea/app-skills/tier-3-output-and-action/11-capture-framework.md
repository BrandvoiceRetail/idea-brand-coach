## 1. What CAPTURE is
CAPTURE is the **copy engine** that sits downstream of the diagnostic pipeline. The diagnostic answers *why the customer is not buying and what to fix first*; the Decision Trigger (Skill 09) names the single lever; the Design Brief (Skill 10) tells a designer what to make. CAPTURE is the structure the resulting **words** follow so the finished copy carries the trigger through every line.

It is the book's central conversion logic — *emotion sparks interest, logic sustains the buy* — turned into a fixed sequence. The book's brand-voice chapter says voice must move a customer along **an emotional journey** where "tone, language, design, and imagery align with a brand's emotional messaging" to guide them "subtly toward purchase". CAPTURE is the named ordering of that journey inside a single asset.

The seven elements run as a **sequence**, not a checklist. The order is the point: you earn attention before you name the pain, validate the pain before you promise transformation, prove uniqueness before you remove risk, and only escalate (ask for the action) once trust is built.

## 2. The seven elements

| # | Element | What it does in the copy | The job, in one line |
|---|---|---|---|
| **C** | **Context** | Orients the reader to who this is for and the situation they are in — the frame before the pitch. | *"This is for someone in your exact situation."* |
| **A** | **Attention** | Earns the first three seconds — the hook that stops the scroll. | *"Stop here — this is relevant to you."* |
| **P** | **Pain** | Names the customer's problem in their own words, before any product mention. | *"You're not imagining it — here is what is actually wrong."* |
| **T** | **Transform** | The better version of themselves the customer is buying — outcome, not feature. | *"Here is the life on the other side of this."* |
| **U** | **Uniqueness** | Why *this* brand, memorably and relevantly — the reason to choose it over the alternatives. | *"Only we do it this way, and that matters to you."* |
| **R** | **Reassurance** | The trust signals and risk-removal that let a sceptical buyer commit. | *"Here is why it's safe to believe us and safe to buy."* |
| **E** | **Escalate** | The call to action — the nudge that converts intent into the click, including any honest urgency. | *"Act now, here's exactly how."* |

Each element draws on a distinct part of the book's machinery. **Pain** is the empathetic-voice rule: "acknowledge customer pain points before solutions" (Empathetic Voice). **Transform** is the *Hope & Transformation* trigger — "customers are buying a better version of themselves" (Emotional Triggers Table). **Uniqueness** is the Distinctive pillar: *memorably, relevantly different*, not merely different (IDEA Framework Core). **Reassurance** is *Trust & Credibility* — "customers need proof before they buy" (Emotional Triggers Table). **Escalate** is *Survival & Fear of Loss / urgency* — "loss aversion … creating urgency triggers instant decision-making" (Emotional Triggers Table), used honestly, never as false scarcity.

## 3. Mapping CAPTURE to the IDEA pillars and Avatar 2.0 fields
Each element is powered by one IDEA pillar and fed by one Avatar 2.0 field (Skill 04). The Avatar field supplies the *raw material* (verbatim language, motivation, trust needs, objection); the pillar supplies the *standard* the line must clear.

| CAPTURE element | IDEA pillar | Avatar 2.0 field (Skill 04) | What the element pulls from that field |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Context** | E — Empathetic | Field 2 — Purchase motivation (*Why they're buying today*) | The situational trigger that sent them searching now — set the scene in their reality. |
| **Attention** | D — Distinctive | Field 1 — Customer vocabulary (*How your customer talks*) | A hook in the customer's own verbatim words, so they recognise themselves and stop. |
| **Pain** | E — Empathetic | Field 4 — Top objection (*What stops them buying*) | Name the barrier as the customer would say it — validate before selling. |
| **Transform** | I — Insight | Field 2 — Purchase motivation | The better self they are buying; the outcome the motivation is reaching for. |
| **Uniqueness** | D — Distinctive | Field 1 — Customer vocabulary | The relevant difference, expressed in language the customer already uses. |
| **Reassurance** | A — Authentic | Field 3 — Trust signals needed (*What builds trust for them*) | The ranked, specific proof/credentials/risk-removal they require to commit. |
| **Escalate** | A — Authentic | Field 4 — Top objection | Remove the last barrier and make the action obvious; honest urgency only. |

This is the book's *Insight-Driven · Distinctive · Empathetic · Authentic* lens applied line by line: the copy must **reflect the customer (I)**, **stand out relevantly (D)**, **speak to how they feel (E)**, and **give reason to trust before they buy (A)** — the four-pillar voice test (Brand Voice Overview).

## 4. The Decision Trigger sets the CAPTURE weighting
CAPTURE is always the *full seven-element sequence*, but the **dominant Decision Trigger (Skill 09) decides which element carries the weight** — which one leads, gets the most words, and sets the emotional register for the rest. This mirrors the trigger→design-brief mapping in Skill 10: the same lever that directs the hero image directs the copy emphasis. It also follows the book's rule on the triggers table: *don't stack triggers — pick the dominant one.*

| Dominant Decision Trigger | Pillar | CAPTURE element that leads / carries the weight | Why |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Recognition** | E — Empathetic | **Pain** (then Context) | The buyer must feel understood before they will hear evidence — mirror the emotional reality first. |
| **Permission** | I — Insight | **Reassurance** (authority forward) | A buyer in rational-evaluation mode needs the proof/credentials up front; emotion comes later. |
| **Identity** | D — Distinctive | **Transform / Uniqueness** | They buy who they want to be — lead with the aspirational outcome and the relevant difference. |
| **Belonging** | A — Authentic | **Context / Uniqueness (values)** | Establish shared values and the brand's reason-for-being before the product. |
| **Momentum** | All pillars elevated | **Reassurance (social-proof volume) → Escalate** | They are ready; make the scale of proof visible, then nudge. |
| **Fear-of-Loss** | All pillars elevated | **Escalate** (cost of inaction) | Frame the cost of waiting, not the benefit of buying — honest urgency, no false scarcity. |

> The weighting changes the *order of emphasis*, never drops an element. Recognition still ends with Escalate; Permission still opens with enough Context to be relevant. The trigger tunes the sequence; it does not replace it.

## 5. How CAPTURE sequences by channel
The full sequence is constant; how it is *compressed and laid out* changes with the channel's attention budget. This is the book's "every touchpoint … should reflect the brand's understanding" principle (Empathetic Voice) applied to format.

**Listing (Amazon detail page).** The richest surface; all seven elements have room.
- *Hero image headline / first bullet* → **Attention + Context + Pain** (the trigger-weighted opener; for Recognition, Pain leads).
- *Bullets 1–3* → **Transform**, then **Uniqueness**, then **Reassurance** (the proof point — mirroring Skill 10's "clinical data belongs in bullet 3, not bullet 1").
- *Price / CTA / A+ content* → **Escalate** + reinforcing **Reassurance**.

**Email.** A linear read; sequence one-to-one, top to bottom.
- *Subject line + preview* → **Attention** (in the customer's vocabulary).
- *Opening lines* → **Context + Pain**.
- *Body* → **Transform → Uniqueness → Reassurance**.
- *Button / PS* → **Escalate** (honest urgency in the PS where it fits the trigger).

**Social ad.** The shortest budget; compress, lead hard on the trigger-weighted element, defer the rest to the click.
- *Scroll-stopper (visual + first line)* → **Attention + Pain** (or **Transform** for an Identity trigger).
- *Caption* → **one** of Uniqueness or Reassurance, not both.
- *CTA* → **Escalate**.
- The social-ad surface is the one place CAPTURE may pull **demographics from the Brand Context profile** for channel-specific variations (Skill 04 §5.2 / §5.4) — at Alpha (listing brief) demographics are not referenced; the listing draws only on the Avatar fields.

> Across all three channels the rule is the same as the book's voice close: tone, language, design and imagery must all reinforce **one** core emotion so the experience feels intuitive and guides the customer toward purchase (Aligning Voice with Emotional Triggers).

## 6. Terminology policy (Tier B — carry forward)
- The **CAPTURE Framework™** name is **Tier A** — it is proprietary commercial distinctiveness and appears on every surface, including the app UI, on first use with the trademark symbol.
- The **individual CAPTURE element names** (Context, Attention, Pain, Transform, Uniqueness, Reassurance, Escalate) are **Tier B (opt-in / Beta)**: they may appear only in optional opt-in expansion panels — **never** in primary output panels (Component 0, the Avatar profile fields, the placement instruction) (`_manifest.json` → `terminology_policy`, `hard_rules`).
- **At Alpha the element names are not used at all** — the placement instruction (Skill 09 Component 3) does not name a CAPTURE element, because the user has no context for the framework yet (Skill 09 §4). The element names go live with the Beta CAPTURE generator.
- No visible CAPTURE breakdown, weighting table, or algorithm explanation in any user-facing output (Avatar2 dev brief §9.1). The output is **finished copy**, not a copy lecture.
- Tier C engine internals (buyer-state names, S1–S4 labels, academic citations) never appear, here or anywhere user-facing.

## Coaching Application
Until the detailed doc lands and Beta ships: treat CAPTURE as the downstream copy engine the Avatar, Positioning Statement, and Decision Trigger feed into, and surface it only as a Beta waitlist at Alpha.

When Beta is live: take the dominant Decision Trigger (Skill 09) and the four Avatar fields (Skill 04); run the **full seven-element sequence**, weighted so the trigger's element leads; pull verbatim customer language for Attention and Uniqueness, the named objection for Pain and Escalate, and the ranked trust signals for Reassurance. Compress the sequence to the channel's attention budget (listing → email → social ad). Deliver **finished copy in the brand's voice** — never the framework, never the weighting, never the element labels in the primary panel. The bar to clear is the book's: does the asset register as aspiration, identity, and belonging — not just a product?
