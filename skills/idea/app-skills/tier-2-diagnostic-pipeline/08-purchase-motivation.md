# SKILL — Purchase Motivation

## Purpose
The S2 field in the Avatar 2.0 forensic portrait. Answers one question: **why is this specific customer buying this specific product at this specific moment?** Not the general category motivation, not a demographic, not a functional benefit — the situational trigger.

> **Job-to-be-done and all derivatives (JTBD, struggling moment, job story) are retired.** They belong to Bob Moesta's proprietary framework and must never be used. **Purchase motivation** is the IDEA term — use it consistently across all surfaces, outputs, and coach responses.

---

## 1. The distinction that matters
Purchase motivation is **not what the customer wants** — it is **why they are buying now**. This changes what the brand needs to say and when.

| Not this | But this | Why it matters |
|---|---|---|
| Wants to grow thicker hair | Has tried four products over 18 months and is giving it one last attempt before accepting the hair loss | The first is a demographic fact. The second tells the brand to lead with credibility and past-failure acknowledgement, not hope. |
| Wants storage for trading cards | Just attended their first major tournament and realised their storage is inadequate for serious play | The first is functional. The second reveals community belonging and identity as a serious player as the emotional drivers. |
| Wants better skincare | Developed sensitivity since a new medication; dermatologist told them to stop most mainstream products | The first describes any skincare buyer. The second reveals the Protector state, medical authority as trust signal, Permission as likely trigger. |

## 2. Identifying it from the corpus
Look for these language patterns (especially in three-star and detailed five-star reviews where customers tell their story):
- **Timing:** "I finally decided to try", "after months of", "when I was diagnosed with", "since starting", "just before my wedding".
- **Failure:** "nothing else worked", "I tried X, Y, and Z", "had given up until", "last resort" → situational urgency + the Protector state.
- **Event:** a specific life event, season, milestone, or trigger that sent the customer searching.
- **Social:** "my friend recommended", "saw it in", "my community uses" → the Connector state and social belonging.
- **Professional referral:** "my doctor/dermatologist/coach told me" → the Assessor state and authority in the motivation.

## 3. What purchase motivation is **never**
- A demographic description (age, gender, income, location — context, not motivation).
- A functional benefit statement ("wants to grow hair", "wants organised storage" — that's the outcome, not the trigger).
- A broad category motivation ("interested in skincare" — could describe anyone).
- A Jobs-to-be-done statement — retired; do not use, reference, or structure the output around it.

## 4. The validity test
Could this sentence describe **only** this specific type of customer in this specific situation, or could it describe anyone in the category? If it could describe anyone, it is not specific enough — keep reading the corpus.

## Coaching Application
Within S2, convert corpus timing/failure/event/social/referral language into one or two sentences of situational motivation that passes the validity test. Feed it into the Avatar profile (Field 2) and onward to buyer-state classification. Never reach for JTBD vocabulary.
