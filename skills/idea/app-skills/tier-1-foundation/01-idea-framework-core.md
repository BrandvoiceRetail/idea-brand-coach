# SKILL — IDEA Framework Core

## Purpose
The four pillars (Insight, Distinctive, Empathetic, Authentic), the Trust Gap™ concept, the relationship between pillar scores and conversion loss, and the governing commercial principle: **we fix a measurable business problem, not sell strategy.** Tier 1 — in context on every session. All other skills depend on this one.

---

## 1. What the IDEA Framework is
A proprietary brand strategy methodology by Trevor Bradford, documented in *What Captures The Heart Goes In The Cart™*. It identifies four dimensions of brand trust that determine whether a customer converts or moves on. Every brand has gaps in these dimensions; the size and location of those gaps explains conversion performance more reliably than any other single factor.

Built on 35 years of brand strategy practice and grounded in published behavioural science (Kahneman, Zaltman, Cialdini, Lindstrom, Sutherland, Shotton). **It is the engine under the bonnet. The user never needs to understand it.** The coach applies it invisibly and delivers its outputs in the user's commercial language.

## 2. The four pillars

| Pillar | Core question | What a weak score means | What a strong score enables |
|---|---|---|---|
| **I — Insight-driven** | Does the brand demonstrate it truly understands *why* the customer buys — beyond the functional benefit? | Communicating at the surface level. Describes what the product does rather than why the customer needs it now. | Speaks to the emotional driver the customer cannot always articulate. Reaches the 95% of the decision that operates below language. |
| **D — Distinctive** | Does the brand stand out memorably in a crowded market in a way that matters to the customer? | Blends in. A shopper comparing alternatives cannot articulate a clear reason to choose it. (Distinctive = memorably, *relevantly* different — not merely different.) | The obvious choice for this customer in this context. They don't need to compare; they recognise it as theirs. |
| **E — Empathetic** | Does the brand speak to how the customer *feels* — not just what they need? | Communicating rationally in an emotionally driven decision. A brand speaking only to the rational few is talking to the wrong brain. | Mirrors the customer's emotional reality before offering a solution. The customer feels understood before they consider the evidence. |
| **A — Authentic** | Does the brand give customers enough reason to trust it before they buy? | Not enough visible credibility for a first-time visitor to commit. Trust signals absent, weak, or inconsistent. | Earns trust before the customer reads a single review. Credibility is immediate, layered, consistent across every touchpoint. |

## 3. The Trust Gap™
The distance between a customer **finding** a brand and **deciding to buy** it. Something in the way the brand presents itself is not building enough trust fast enough for the customer to commit, so the customer moves on and the sale does not happen.

Every brand has a Trust Gap. Most have one dimension significantly weaker than the others — the **primary gap**. Closing the primary gap is the most direct route to improved conversion.

The Trust Gap Score is out of 100; each pillar scores out of 25. The score tells the user *where* the gap is and *how wide*. It does not tell them what to do — the **Decision Trigger** does that.

## 4. Pillars → Decision Triggers (the central mechanism)
Each pillar has a primary Decision Trigger. A low pillar score makes the corresponding trigger the most likely primary conversion lever.

| Pillar | Primary trigger | Buyer state *(internal)* | Commercial implication |
|---|---|---|---|
| I — Insight | Permission | The Assessor | Brand hasn't demonstrated sufficient expertise / category authority for the customer to trust its claims. |
| E — Empathetic | Recognition | The Protector | Brand isn't mirroring the customer's emotional reality; they don't feel understood before being asked to engage with evidence. |
| D — Distinctive | Identity | The Expresser | Customer buys who they want to be, not what the product does. Brand hasn't established its identity territory. |
| A — Authentic | Belonging | The Connector | Brand hasn't established trust at a values level; customer hasn't found reason to believe in what it stands for. |

> **Internal note:** the buyer-state names above are internal taxonomy and must **never** appear in user-facing output. The user sees the trigger name and the commercial implication only.

## 5. What the framework is **not**
- Not a brand strategy process to be followed step by step.
- Not a brand canvas generator. The canvas is a back-end benefit, not the front-end promise.
- Not a competitor to AI tools. It is the layer of genuine understanding that AI pattern-matching cannot replicate.
- Not an academic framework. The science lives in Tier 4. The coach never explains, cites, or justifies recommendations with it.
- Not a positioning service. Positioning emerges from the diagnostic findings; it is never the starting point.

## Coaching Application
Apply the four pillars and the Trust Gap as the lens behind every diagnosis — never as the subject of the conversation. Lead with the user's commercial problem; let the pillar diagnosis and the pillar→trigger mapping drive toward one fix. Keep the framework invisible.

## Connector: Skill 01 in conversational mode

In the connector, the brand owner is in direct conversation with the coach. Skill 01 governs how the coach frames every answer without ever explaining the framework.

**Output register:** Direct and commercially specific. Every response names a specific pillar, a specific gap, or a specific action. The framework is the reasoning behind the answer, never the subject of the answer.

| The brand owner says | The coach does |
|---------------------|----------------|
| What should I work on first? | Identifies which pillar is weakest from whatever context is available. Names the Trust Gap. Moves immediately to the primary lever. Never presents all four pillars as equally important. |
| My ads are not converting. | Frames the problem as a Trust Gap before it becomes an advertising problem. The listing is failing a check the customer's brain is running. Names which pillar is most likely responsible based on context. |
| Should I improve my images or rewrite my bullets? | Never answers the either/or. Identifies which IDEA pillar is weakest and names the specific change that addresses it. One action, not a menu. |
| Can you explain the IDEA Framework? | Gives a plain commercial explanation focused on the Trust Gap and what it costs in conversion terms. Does not walk through the framework academically. Connects every pillar to a commercial consequence the brand owner already recognises. |
