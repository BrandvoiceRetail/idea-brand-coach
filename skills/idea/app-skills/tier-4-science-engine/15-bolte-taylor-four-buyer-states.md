## 1. The four characters in the brain → the four buyer states
Four functionally distinct groups of brain cells, two per hemisphere, split thinking vs feeling. Each is a different way of processing a purchase decision. The mapping below is the bridge from the neuroanatomy to the working taxonomy already in Skill 05; it carries no new operational claims — it explains the ones Skill 05 already makes.

| Buyer state *(Skill 05)* | Brain character | Hemisphere / mode | How it processes a purchase | IDEA pillar | Trigger |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **The Assessor** | Left thinking | Left / analytical | Sequences, compares, verifies; wants evidence and specification before committing. | Insight (I) | Permission |
| **The Protector** | Left emotion | Left / cautious-feeling | Guards against loss and past disappointment; scans for risk; needs to feel safe before it will listen. | Empathetic (E) | Recognition |
| **The Expresser** | Right emotion | Right / expansive-feeling | Acts in the present, on impulse and identity; buys who it wants to be. | Distinctive (D) | Identity |
| **The Connector** | Right thinking | Right / relational | Orients to people, values and the bigger picture; buys to belong and to align. | Authentic (A) | Belonging |

> This is the same four-row spine as Skill 05's classifier table — re-read through the brain rather than through review language. Skill 05 is the operational source of truth for the language patterns and calibration directions; this skill must never contradict it.

## 2. Why The Protector is most often dominant at the point of purchase
The master-table description names **The Protector** as the most commonly dominant state at the decision moment. The book's own science explains the mechanism without needing the neuroanatomy spelled out to the user:

- Purchasing decisions are made in the **subconscious** and only justified with logic afterwards — up to 95% are emotion-led (Zaltman; see [[../../framework/00-foundations/02-idea-framework/01-the-science-of-emotional-buying/00-decisions-are-subconscious]]).
- Loss aversion: people are wired to **avoid loss more than they seek gain** (the book's Emotional Triggers Table, "Survival & Fear of Loss"). At the moment of paying a stranger online, the risk-scanning, loss-averse mode is the one most likely in the driving seat.
- The neurochemistry agrees: relief/reassurance copy works by **dropping cortisol** ("easy returns", "risk-free"), which is precisely the Protector's currency (see [[../../framework/01-customer/00-avatar-2.0/03-the-neuroscience-of-shopping/01-the-four-neurochemicals]]).

Commercial consequence (the only thing that leaves this skill): when in doubt, **mirror the fear before offering the solution** — trust signals, guarantees and sceptic-to-convert testimonials — because the brain most often at the till is the one protecting against another disappointment. This is why low **Empathetic** scores so reliably point to **Recognition** as the primary Decision Trigger.

## 3. The Shadow-archetype connection *(pending Trevor's detailed doc)*
The master-table description references a **Shadow-archetype connection** — the idea that each dominant buyer state carries a corresponding "shadow": the unspoken fear, objection, or self-doubt that the state is defending against, and which the right Decision Trigger neutralises. The full mapping (state → shadow → the objection the trigger resolves) **awaits Trevor's science doc.** A faithful provisional reading, consistent with Skills 05 and 09:

| Buyer state | Provisional shadow (the unspoken objection) | What resolves it |
|---|---|---|
| The Assessor | "I can't verify this claim / I'll be fooled." | Evidence and authority → Permission |
| The Protector | "I've been let down before / this won't work for me either." | Recognition of the fear + proof of safety → Recognition |
| The Expresser | "This isn't me / it won't say what I want it to say about me." | Identity and brand energy → Identity |
| The Connector | "I don't trust who's behind this / it doesn't share my values." | Mission, founder story, values → Belonging |

## 4. How the science informs the review-language classifiers
The review-language classifiers in Skill 05 (§1, §3) are downstream of this model: each state's characteristic language is the surface trace of how that brain character processes. The role of this skill is to make the classifier *more precise*, never to add a step the user sees.

- **Assessor language** (comparison, research, specification) = the analytical left thinking mind narrating its verification.
- **Protector language** (scepticism, regret, "wish I'd found this sooner") = the loss-averse left feeling mind reporting relief that the risk did not materialise.
- **Expresser language** (identity, impulse, "this is exactly me") = the present-tense right feeling mind.
- **Connector language** (values, community, "love what this brand stands for") = the relational right thinking mind.

The Recognition-vs-Belonging disambiguation in Skill 05 §3 (inward-facing relief vs outward-facing inclusion) is exactly the Protector (left feeling) vs Connector (right thinking) distinction. This skill is the reason that directional rule holds; Skill 05 is where it is applied.
