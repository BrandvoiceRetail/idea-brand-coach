# SKILL 07 — Brand Purpose & Brand Vision

## Purpose
This skill powers the Brand Purpose and Brand Vision outputs of the IDEA Brand Canvas. The coach must be able to explain what each statement is, why it matters, guide users through creating their own, and evaluate whether a draft statement is strong or weak.

---

## Brand Purpose: The "Why" Behind Existence

A brand's purpose is the core reason it goes beyond just selling products. It is the answer to "why does this brand exist in the world?" — not "what does it sell?" A strong brand purpose, aligned with the IDEA Framework, integrates customer insights, differentiation, emotional connections, and authenticity to create lasting impact.

Purpose is not a marketing exercise. It is the anchor that guides every decision: what products to develop, what partnerships to pursue, how to handle a crisis, what the brand stands for when no one is watching.

---

## Why Purpose Matters

Customers today do not just want products — they want relationships. They are asking:
- Does this brand understand my needs?
- Can I trust this product to deliver what I want?
- Do they really "get" me?
- Does what they stand for align with what I believe?

A clear brand purpose answers these questions before they are asked. It converts browsers into believers and first-time buyers into lifelong advocates.

---

## The Four IDEA Tests for Brand Purpose

INSIGHT-DRIVEN: Does this purpose identify and address customer needs beyond the product itself? Does it reflect a deep understanding of what the audience truly cares about?

DISTINCTIVE: Does this purpose stand out with a higher mission? Does it occupy a unique position that competitors cannot easily claim?

EMPATHETIC: Does this purpose resonate with customer values? Does it build deeper relationships by connecting with what the audience believes?

AUTHENTIC: Does this purpose align with the brand's core identity? Is it genuinely reflected in how the business operates, not just what it says?

---

## Anatomy of a Strong Purpose Statement

A strong purpose statement should:
1. Name who the brand serves
2. Define what transformation or benefit it creates
3. Connect to a value or belief that is larger than the transaction
4. Feel genuine and specific, not generic and aspirational

WEAK: "To provide high-quality products that make life better." (Every brand could say this. It says nothing distinctive, empathetic, or authentic.)

STRONG: "To inspire and empower individuals to embrace sustainable living, making eco-friendly choices accessible to everyone." (Names audience, defines transformation, connects to a belief system, feels specific.)

---

## Common Purpose Statement Errors

- Too vague: "To create products people love." (Meaningless)
- Too product-focused: "To make the best haircare range on Amazon." (This is a mission, not a purpose)
- Inauthentic: Claiming a social purpose the business does not actually demonstrate in its operations
- Generic: Using words like "quality," "innovation," and "excellence" without specific meaning

---

## AI Prompt Template for Brand Purpose

"Generate a brand purpose statement that defines why this brand exists beyond just selling products. Ensure it aligns with customer needs, emotional impact, differentiation, and authenticity. Use the IDEA framework principles — Insight-Driven, Distinctive, Empathetic, and Authentic — to craft a compelling purpose that resonates with the target audience. The brand is [description], serving [customer avatar], and the core emotional trigger for purchase is [trigger from Avatar 2.0]."

---

## Brand Vision: The Future State

Brand vision is an aspirational statement about the future impact the brand seeks to make. Where purpose answers "why we exist today," vision answers "what we are working towards" — the long-term destination that shapes strategy and growth.

A vision statement should be bold, specific, and emotionally resonant. It should describe a future state that the brand is actively building toward, not a generic ambition.

---

## The Four IDEA Tests for Brand Vision

INSIGHT-DRIVEN: Is this vision rooted in a deep understanding of evolving customer expectations? Does it anticipate where the market and the customer are heading?

DISTINCTIVE: Does this vision set a unique direction for brand leadership? Is this a position the brand can credibly own?

EMPATHETIC: Does this vision address emotional and societal needs? Does it foster a loyal community by standing for something beyond profit?

AUTHENTIC: Does this vision align with the brand's long-term commitments and actual values — not just what sounds impressive?

---

## Anatomy of a Strong Vision Statement

WEAK: "To become a leading brand in our category." (Vague, competitive, says nothing about why it matters.)

STRONG: "To become the global leader in sustainable products, transforming the way people shop and live for a healthier planet." (Specific aspiration, defines the transformation, connects to a meaningful cause.)

---

## Purpose vs Vision: The Key Difference

PURPOSE = Why we exist (the unchanging reason the brand is in the world)
VISION = Where we are going (the future we are building towards)

Purpose is stable. Vision evolves as the brand grows and markets change. Both must be authentic or neither will hold.

---

## AI Prompt Template for Brand Vision

"Develop an aspirational brand vision statement that defines the long-term impact and future direction of this brand. Ensure it aligns with market trends, customer expectations, and the brand's commitment to [core value]. Incorporate the IDEA framework to make it distinctive, emotionally compelling, and strategically insightful. The brand serves [audience] and its purpose is [purpose statement]."

---

## Coaching Application

When a user cannot articulate their brand purpose, ask:
1. "Why did you start this brand — beyond making money?"
2. "What would be lost in the world if your brand disappeared?"
3. "What do you want your customers to feel, believe, or be able to do because of your brand?"
4. "What injustice, frustration, or gap in the market are you here to address?"

When evaluating a draft purpose statement, run the four IDEA tests. If it fails Distinctiveness — it is generic. If it fails Authenticity — it is performative. If it fails Empathy — it is self-focused. If it fails Insight — it is disconnected from the customer.

When a user cannot articulate their brand vision, ask:
1. "In 10 years, if everything goes to plan, what will have changed because your brand existed?"
2. "What market, belief, or behaviour are you trying to shift or create?"
3. "What would you like to be the brand that is synonymous with in your category?"
