# SKILL 10 — Brand Personality & Brand Voice

## Purpose
This skill powers the Brand Personality and Brand Voice outputs of the IDEA Brand Canvas. Brand voice is one of the most powerful tools for building connection with customers — and one of the most neglected by e-commerce sellers. The coach must be able to help users define a distinctive, consistent voice and personality that genuinely resonates with their audience.

---

## Brand Personality: The Human Traits of the Brand

Brand personality defines the human-like traits that influence how the brand speaks, interacts, and resonates with its audience. It is based on the principle that customers do not engage with logos and products — they engage with personalities.

When customers say they "love" a brand, they are expressing a relationship with a personality — the way the brand makes them feel, the tone it uses, the values it expresses through its behaviour.

---

## The Four IDEA Tests for Brand Personality

INSIGHT-DRIVEN: Is this personality based on what attracts and engages the target audience? Does it reflect how the customer wants to feel when interacting with the brand?

DISTINCTIVE: Does this personality establish a unique and recognisable brand identity? Could you describe this brand's personality in a way that no competitor could claim?

EMPATHETIC: Does this personality create an emotional connection through relatable traits? Does it make customers feel understood and valued?

AUTHENTIC: Is this personality consistent across all brand communications? Does it reflect who the brand genuinely is — not a persona put on for marketing purposes?

---

## Defining Brand Personality

Choose three to five adjectives that describe the brand's character. These should feel true, be specific, and guide every communication decision.

GENERIC PERSONALITIES (avoid): professional, quality-focused, innovative, customer-centric. These describe what every brand claims to be.

SPECIFIC PERSONALITIES (aim for): bold and irreverent (Old Spice), warm and empowering (Dove), playful and curious (Innocent Drinks), authoritative and precise (TAG Heuer), adventurous and rebellious (Patagonia).

The exercise: if the brand were a person at a dinner party, how would they behave? What would they talk about? What would they refuse to discuss? What would other people say about them?

---

## Personality and Emotional Triggers

The right personality amplifies the right emotional trigger:
- A brand targeting hope and transformation needs an inspiring, motivating personality
- A brand targeting trust and credibility needs an authoritative, transparent personality
- A brand targeting belonging and community needs a warm, inclusive, relatable personality
- A brand targeting status and aspiration needs a sophisticated, confident, aspirational personality

Mismatch between personality and trigger destroys trust. A budget brand adopting a luxury tone, or a wellness brand using corporate language, creates cognitive dissonance that makes customers disengage.

"Define the brand personality traits that influence how this brand interacts with and resonates with its audience. Ensure these traits align with customer expectations and create a distinctive, relatable identity. Use the IDEA framework to make them engaging, emotionally compelling, and consistently reflected across brand touchpoints. The brand serves [target audience], its emotional trigger is [trigger], and its values are [values]."

## Brand Voice: The Expression of Personality

Brand voice defines the tone, language, and communication style the brand uses to engage with customers across all platforms. It is the consistent personality expressed through words.

A brand's voice is more than just words — it is an emotional signal to customers. The way a brand speaks communicates what it values, who it is for, and whether it deserves trust.

---

## The Four IDEA Tests for Brand Voice

INSIGHT-DRIVEN: Does this voice match the communication style customers respond to best? Does it use the language the customer uses to describe their problem?

DISTINCTIVE: Does this voice create a unique and recognisable communication style? Could you identify this brand from the copy alone, without seeing the logo?

EMPATHETIC: Does this voice use language that resonates emotionally with the audience? Does it acknowledge the customer's reality before presenting the solution?

AUTHENTIC: Is this voice consistent across marketing, sales, customer service, and product packaging? Does it sound like a real person, not a corporate committee?

---

## The Four Components of Brand Voice

TONE OF VOICE: The emotional signature — how the brand speaks. Whether authoritative and sophisticated (Rolex), energetic and dynamic (Red Bull), or friendly and playful (Innocent Drinks), tone sets the expectation for how customers engage.

LANGUAGE: The distinctive expression — the specific words, phrases, and vocabulary the brand uses consistently. Taglines, product names, category language. TAG Heuer's "Don't Crack Under Pressure" transforms a functional watch into an emotional experience — a symbol of resilience.

DESIGN (visual voice): The visual extension of voice — reinforcing personality and emotional cues through colour, typography, and layout. Red evokes energy, passion, and urgency. Green conveys health, sustainability, and calm. Black and white offer sophistication and precision.

IMAGERY: The visual storytelling layer — the people, environments, and situations the brand associates itself with. Imagery makes implicit claims about who the brand is for and what it stands for.

All four must align. Inconsistency between any element creates brand confusion.

---

## Voice Tone by Channel

Voice is consistent in character but adapts in tone by context:

PRODUCT LISTINGS: Clear, benefit-led, emotionally engaging — lead with the transformation, support with specifics
SOCIAL MEDIA: More conversational, reactive, community-building — show personality and humanity
EMAIL MARKETING: Personal, value-adding, direct — respect the customer's time
CUSTOMER SERVICE: Empathetic, solution-focused, human — never scripted or defensive
ADVERTISING: Attention-grabbing, emotionally resonant, memorable — create the Signpost

The brand's personality stays constant. The register adapts.

---

## Case Study: TAG Heuer's Voice System

TAG Heuer demonstrates how all four voice components align:
- Tone: bold, assertive, infused with athletic energy
- Language: "Don't Crack Under Pressure" — active, aspirational, transforms product into identity
- Design: red, green, white, black — speed, endurance, luxury, precision
- Imagery: world-class athletes, Formula 1, competitive excellence

Every touchpoint — from the website to the packaging to the advertising — reinforces the same personality: a brand for those who thrive under pressure. The result is not just product recognition — it is a symbol of aspiration and identity.

## Coaching Application

When a user says their brand "feels generic" or "sounds like every other brand," the voice is the problem. Ask:
1. "Read me your homepage headline. Now read me your competitor's headline. What is different?"
2. "What three words would your best customer use to describe how your brand makes them feel?"
3. "If you stripped your logo off your listings, social posts, and emails, would anyone know they came from the same brand?"
4. "Who is the person behind your brand, and does your voice sound like them?"

Then help them define three personality adjectives, a tone guide, and three words or phrases they will always use — and three they will never use. This gives them a simple, actionable voice framework they can apply immediately.
