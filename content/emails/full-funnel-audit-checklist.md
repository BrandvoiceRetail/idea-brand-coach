A camping-stove founder had spent six months fixing things — a rewritten listing, a new main image, an updated welcome sequence. Each fix felt real in the moment. None of it had ever been checked against the whole map.

That's the trap of steady iteration. You can point to real work — the `amazon_listing_copy` rewrite alone went through three drafts — and still have no idea whether the fixes actually closed anything, or just moved effort around a funnel that's still missing entire positions.

Three tools, run in order, answer that:

`run_funnel_audit` overlays the avatar against every live touchpoint and ranks the weakest link — not the one you assume, the one the data shows.

`get_funnel_coverage` lays every canonical position, awareness through advocacy, against what you actually have live, and shows the empty ones plainly.

`compute_trust_gap_lift` checks the fixes you already made, starting with that listing rewrite, against the before score, so you know whether the work moved the number or just changed the words.

For the camping-stove founder, the listing rewrite had genuinely worked — a real, confirmed lift on the Empathetic pillar, not a guess. But `get_funnel_coverage` also showed `welcome_series` and `insert_cards` sitting empty the entire six months, positions that never got touched because all the attention stayed on the one asset that felt most urgent.

The lesson for anyone mid-fix-cycle: the position you keep rewriting isn't automatically the one still costing you the most. Run the audit before planning the next quarter of work, not after, so the next six months go to whatever the map actually shows is missing, not whatever felt most pressing to open first.
