A baby-teething founder we talked to had one lifestyle photo in her gallery. She treated it as a bonus — the slot she'd fill "if there's room" after the product shots, the dimension shots, and the packaging shot.

That's the myth. The lifestyle image isn't decorative filler competing with your other slots for space. It's the only slot in the whole gallery built to do a specific job none of the others can: show the feeling your product actually sells.

Her product shots proved the teether exists and works. Fair enough. But none of them showed the moment she's actually selling — an exhausted parent at 2am, baby finally settled, quiet returning to the room. That's not a feature. It's relief. And relief doesn't photograph as a product-on-white shot; it needs a person, a context, a moment.

`generate_listing_image_brief` builds that slot on purpose — it's called the photoreal "felt moment" slot in the brief, and it's addressable, not optional, the same way the main image and title are addressable. Skip it and your gallery proves the product works while staying silent on why anyone needed it to.

This isn't about swapping in any lifestyle photo. A shot of a baby simply lying near the teether doesn't answer the question either — it still has to show the specific relief, not just a person in frame. That's the difference between a slot that's technically filled and one that's actually doing its job.

The action: open your brief and check whether your felt-moment slot currently has a real assignment, or whether it's a leftover product shot wearing a lifestyle label. If it's the latter, that's the one to re-brief this week — before touching anything else in the gallery.
