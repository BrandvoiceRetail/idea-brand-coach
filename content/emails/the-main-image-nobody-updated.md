A scalp-serum founder's main image hasn't changed since launch. Product on white, dropper tilted just so. It was fine when it went live.

It isn't fine anymore. Three competitors now show the product mid-use — dropper against scalp, hand in frame, a face that reads "this is actually happening to a real head." Her image still reads "here is a bottle."

CTR has been sliding for months and she'd been blaming the algorithm.

`run_trust_gap` scored her listing across the four IDEA pillars and came back with a flat Distinctive score — the pillar that asks "does this look like anyone else's bottle, or does it look like ours." Her main image wasn't losing to a better product. It was losing to a more specific one.

A product-on-white shot answers "what is this." It stopped answering "why this one" the moment three other sellers copied the same white-background convention. The image and the title are supposed to make one claim together — what it is, and the one thing that makes it different — and hers were making the first claim twice.

`generate_main_image_title_plan` treats the image and title as a single positioning statement instead of two separate to-dos. It pairs a new image concept — the dropper-to-scalp moment her competitors already found — with a title rewrite that puts the real point of difference inside the first eighty characters, where the search grid actually reads it.

The action: pull your own main image next to your three closest competitors' current ones, not the ones live when you launched. If yours is the only one still saying "here is the product" while theirs say "here's what it does for you," that's your Distinctive score talking.
