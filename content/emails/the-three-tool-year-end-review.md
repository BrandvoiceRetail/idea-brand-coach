A vitamin-C-serum founder we'll call Priya didn't want a twelve-month retrospective this December. She wanted one clean read: where does my funnel actually stand right now, before I plan next year's calendar?

Three tools, run in that order, gave her that in about ten minutes.

First, `list_funnel_inventory`. This just maps what exists — every touchpoint she's actually built, from her main image through her welcome series. No scoring yet, just an honest inventory. Priya found two touchpoints she'd set up back in Q1 and never touched since.

Second, `get_funnel_coverage`. This flags the gaps — canonical positions with nothing built at all. For Priya, `trust_badges_social_proof` came back as a gap: no dedicated messaging, no addressed reviews, nothing beyond a stray line buried in her bullet points.

Third, `run_funnel_audit`. This overlays her actual customer avatar against every live touchpoint and ranks them by connection strength. It confirmed what coverage had flagged and sharpened it further: trust badges and social proof wasn't just missing — it was her single weakest link, ranked below even the touchpoints she'd been quietly worried about all year.

The order matters. Inventory before coverage before audit. Skip straight to the audit and you're ranking touchpoints you haven't confirmed exist, and you miss gaps that were never live long enough to score in the first place. Run it in sequence and each tool narrows the picture instead of repeating it.

For Priya, the finding wasn't dramatic. She didn't need a rebrand or a new ad agency. She needed one addressable trust-signal block near her buy box, built for a customer who's read enough scare stories about vitamin C serums that "verified purchase" alone doesn't settle it.

That's the point of a year-end read done this way — it names the one thing worth fixing before next year's plan gets written, not a list of forty things to feel guilty about.

Ten minutes, three tools, in order. Then plan next year around whatever they show you.
