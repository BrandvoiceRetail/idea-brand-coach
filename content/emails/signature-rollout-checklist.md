A macrame-kit founder had a genuinely good signature line. Sharp, specific, unmistakably hers. It lived exactly one place: the homepage hero.

That's the whole problem. A signature line earns nothing sitting alone. It earns trust through repetition — the moment a customer sees the same distinctive thought in three places and starts to believe it's actually who you are, not just good copywriting on one page.

She'd already run `generate_signature` months earlier. What she hadn't done was move it anywhere else.

The checklist for getting a signature line actually working:

1. Homepage hero — where it usually starts.
2. `packaging_unboxing` — the line printed somewhere in the box, so the felt moment of opening it echoes what she said online.
3. The About page, near the top, not buried in paragraph four.
4. At least one product listing, worked into the copy rather than pasted as a tagline.
5. One retention touchpoint — an email or insert card — so repeat customers see it again.

Instead of manually rewriting five assets by hand, she used `refine_creative_plan` to propagate the line across the live plans that already existed, rather than treating each one as a separate writing project. One input, five outputs that actually agree with each other.

The distinction worth holding onto: this is propagation of an existing line, not a positioning change. If you're pushing a genuinely new distinctive claim across everything you own, that's a bigger, more deliberate move. Rolling out a signature you've already generated and approved is the smaller, safer use of the same tool.

If your signature line exists but only lives in one place, that's this week's fix. Not writing something new — moving what you already have.
