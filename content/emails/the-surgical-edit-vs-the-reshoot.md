A raised-bed-planter founder is looking at an ad that's clearly tired — CTR sliding week over week on creative that used to carry the account. The instinct is to book a full reshoot. Problem: the reshoot is three weeks out, and the account is bleeding spend every day between now and then.

Here's the thing worth sitting with before you book anything: fatigue on one `paid_social_creative` asset is almost never the whole ad. It's usually one scene, one beat, one visual that's gone stale while the rest of the piece is still doing fine. Treating "the ad is tired" as "reshoot the ad" is like replacing a car because one tire's worn.

`refine_creative_plan` exists for exactly this. It doesn't regenerate the whole storyboard — it does the surgical edit: swap the one scene that's carrying the fatigue, keep everything else in the plan intact. For the planter founder that meant replacing the opening hook shot with a fresher variation of the same trigger angle, same product, same claims, new scene one. That's a same-day fix, not a three-week one.

But a scene swap without measurement is just a guess with better production values. `design_test` turns that swap into an actual hypothesis: is the new scene one outperforming the old one, or was the real cause something outside the creative entirely — seasonality, audience saturation, a bid change nobody flagged? Structure the test before you launch the edit, and you get a real answer instead of a vibe.

The move this week: if a `paid_social_creative` asset is fading, don't default to the reshoot. Run `refine_creative_plan` on the one scene that's actually stale, wrap it in a `design_test`, and let the data — not the calendar — tell you whether the full reshoot was ever necessary.

Sometimes it still is. Usually it isn't.
