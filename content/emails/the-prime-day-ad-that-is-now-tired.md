A protein-bar founder built three ad variants for Prime Day. They worked. Six weeks later, she's still running the same three, and CTR has quietly halved.

Nothing broke. The ad didn't get worse. The audience just saw it enough times that it stopped registering — the oldest problem in paid social, and the easiest one to miss because nothing in the dashboard says "fatigue." It just says a number sliding down that looks a lot like every other number sliding down.

`run_funnel_audit` overlays your customer avatar against every live touchpoint and ranks each one by how well it's actually landing right now, not at launch. Run it a month or two after any seasonal push and creative fatigue at paid_social_creative shows up as that period's weakest link — not because the creative is bad, but because it's the same creative the same people have now seen a dozen times.

The instinct at that point is to scrap the campaign and start a new storyboard from zero. That's usually more work than the fix needs. The hook and the body of an ad carry different jobs — the hook earns the first half-second, the body does the actual persuading — and fatigue almost always hits the hook first, because that's the part repetition burns out fastest.

`refine_creative_plan` lets you swap just the hook and CTA scenes on the existing plan instead of rebuilding the whole storyboard. Same body, same product truth, same trigger logic — just an opening few seconds that hasn't been seen forty times already.

The action: if a Prime Day (or any seasonal) creative is still live six-plus weeks later, run `run_funnel_audit` before you touch the budget. If paid_social_creative comes back as the weak link, don't kill the whole ad — use `refine_creative_plan` to refresh the scenes doing the most repetitive work first.
