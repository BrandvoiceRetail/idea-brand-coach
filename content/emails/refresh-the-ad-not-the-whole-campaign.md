CTR on a portable-stove founder's best-performing video ad had been sliding for two weeks. His instinct was the founder-instinct: the creative's dead, book a new shoot, start over. He'd already messaged a videographer about dates.

Look closer, and the problem wasn't the whole ad. Five of six scenes were still doing their job — the unboxing beat, the flame-in-wind demo, the pack-down shot people keep replaying. It was the opening hook, scene one, that had gone stale. Everything downstream of it was fine. The ad just wasn't stopping the scroll anymore.

That distinction is the whole reason `refine_creative_plan` exists. Most founders treat creative fatigue as an all-or-nothing problem: the ad works or it gets replaced. But a `generate_video_storyboard` plan isn't one asset, it's a set of scenes, and scenes decay at different rates. The hook — the part fighting hardest for attention in a paid_social_creative slot — burns out first because it's seen the most, cold, by people who haven't decided to care yet.

`refine_creative_plan` lets you edit that one scene inside the existing storyboard without touching what's still converting. Same reference kit, same product footage for the scenes that work, one new opening line and shot tested against the same body. No new shoot, no new editor, no three-week wait for a reshoot to justify itself.

He tested two new hook variants against the tired one. One beat it within days. The other four scenes never moved, because they never needed to.

Before you book a reshoot over a sliding CTR, check whether the whole ad is actually broken or just the first three seconds of it. Usually it's the second thing.
