A tea founder we know has a decision-trigger diagnosis sitting in a doc from three months ago. She knows her buyer's trigger. She just never turned it into a sentence anyone reads.

That's the gap most founders hit after running `identify_decision_trigger`. The insight lands, gets nodded at in a planning doc, then sits there because turning a psychological lever into an actual sentence isn't obvious. Here's the checklist that closes it.

First, run `identify_decision_trigger` and get the one lever — permission, recognition, identity, belonging, momentum, or fear_of_loss — this specific purchase turns on. Not "probably urgency." The actual one, from evidence.

Second, feed that trigger into `generate_signature`. You're not looking for ten tagline options to pick from. You're looking for the one distinctive line your brand can actually own, built around that specific lever.

Third, put the line in your amazon_brand_story section, above the origin story, not buried under three paragraphs of how the company started. That section is usually the first place a browsing buyer looks for a reason to trust you past the product photos.

Fourth, and this is where most checklists stop but shouldn't: check the line against the trigger, not against how nice it sounds. A sentence can read beautifully and still miss. The test is whether it hits permission, recognition, identity, belonging, momentum, or fear of loss the way the diagnosis said it should — not whether it sounds clever.

The tea founder's diagnosis had been sitting there the whole time. It just needed the second tool to become a sentence, and a place at the top of the page to live.
