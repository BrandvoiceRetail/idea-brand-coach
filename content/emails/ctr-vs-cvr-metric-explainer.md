Say a beauty-serum founder tells us conversion dropped 15% this month. That's one number covering two completely different jobs.

Click-through rate is the main image's job. It answers: does the search grid make someone stop and tap?

Conversion rate is the listing's job. It answers: does the page then earn the sale?

Treat "conversion" as one flat metric and you're guessing which job broke. Swap the main image when CVR was the actual leak, and you've spent a design cycle on nothing. Rewrite the bullets when CTR was the leak, and you've spent a week fixing a page that was never the problem.

`get_funnel_piece_metrics` splits the blended number back into what happened at each stop. It reports CTR — the `amazon_main_image` moment, where your product either gets tapped or scrolled past — separately from CVR, what happens once someone actually lands. Same funnel, two different diagnoses, two different fixes.

For our beauty-serum founder, splitting the number showed CTR was flat. The image was still doing its job. CVR was the one that had dropped. The leak was on the page, not in the search grid — which meant every hour spent shooting a new lifestyle photo that month would have moved nothing at all.

The action before you touch anything: pull `get_funnel_piece_metrics` and read CTR and CVR as two separate lines, not one blended trend. Whichever one moved is where the fix belongs. The one that didn't move doesn't need your attention yet, however tempting the swap feels.
