A wooden-toy founder never noticed her listing copy has one job: sell to the parent buying for their own kid. Come Q4, a good chunk of her traffic is someone buying for somebody else's kid — a gift-buyer who doesn't know if the paint is safe or whether it fits "an eight-year-old who already has everything."

That's a different read entirely, and most `amazon_listing_copy` was never written to answer it.

The check is quick. `list_funnel_inventory` shows what you actually have live across every touchpoint. `get_funnel_coverage` lays those against the canonical map and flags exactly which positions are thin.

Run it on your listing copy specifically and ask one question: does anything here answer "is this a good gift" instead of just "is this a good toy"? If the copy is still all material specs and safety certifications with no line for the person choosing it as a present, that's a thin `amazon_listing_copy` position even though the touchpoint itself shows up as covered — live on paper, not covered for this buyer.

Q4 traffic doesn't wait for a rewrite. The gap gets filled by whoever answers it first — you, or the near-identical listing one row down. It doesn't need a full relaunch either. One sentence acknowledging the gift-buyer, placed where the current copy assumes only a self-buyer is reading, closes most of it.

Run `list_funnel_inventory` today, then `get_funnel_coverage` against it, and check whether your `amazon_listing_copy` speaks to the gift-buyer at all before the volume doubles. If it doesn't, that's this week's fix, not next quarter's.
