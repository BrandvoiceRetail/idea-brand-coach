A soy-candle founder was about to triple her ad spend. Solid CTR, decent CVR, growth-shaped chart. Before she wired the budget over, we asked one question: is your unboxing moment actually in your funnel, or just in your head?

It wasn't in the funnel. Not officially. She knew the packaging mattered — she'd designed it carefully, tissue paper, a handwritten-style card — but it had never been logged as a tracked touchpoint anywhere. It existed as a vibe, not as evidence.

That matters more at scale, not less. More ad spend means more strangers meeting your brand for the first time at packaging_unboxing, right after the sale is already won and right before the review gets written. If it's quietly weak, tripling spend just triples how many people notice.

Two moves, in order:

1. **Log it.** `upsert_funnel_touchpoint` gets the unboxing moment into the system as a real, trackable piece of the funnel — not an assumption sitting in a Notion doc.
2. **Check it.** `get_funnel_coverage` then tells you whether unboxing is actually counted as covered now, or if it's still showing up as a gap next to the other retention touchpoints nobody's logged either.

Those are different findings, and they call for different action. Still a gap means log it before you judge it — you can't score a touchpoint the system doesn't know exists. Actually covered means you're clear to scale with confidence, unboxing included.

Either way, you want that answer before the ad budget triples, not after.
