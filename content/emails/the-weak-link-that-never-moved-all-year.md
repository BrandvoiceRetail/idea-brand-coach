An electrolyte-powder founder assumed her funnel got better through the year. New creative every quarter, a redesigned listing in spring, a fresh A+ page in summer. Surely something had moved.

She ran `ingest_funnel_analytics` across all twelve months at once, then pulled `get_funnel_piece_metrics` to see the trend per touchpoint instead of one blended number.

Most positions moved. Her main image's CTR climbed after the spring refresh. Her review-request flow ticked up after she added a follow-up email. But her urgency messaging — the "only X left" line near the buy box — scored exactly the same in December as it did in January. Not worse. Not better. Untouched, because nobody had touched it.

That's the trap with urgency messaging specifically. It doesn't throw an obvious red flag like a falling CVR does. It just sits there, quietly failing to do its one job — give a hesitant buyer a reason to decide now instead of adding to cart and leaving. A flat score isn't a stable score. It's a score nobody looked at all year.

The lesson here isn't "check everything more often." It's that year-end is the one moment you can see the full twelve-month line at once, and a flat line at any touchpoint is a tell — not a pass. Things that improve get attention. Things that stay flat usually got skipped, not solved.

The action: pull twelve months into `ingest_funnel_analytics` and read `get_funnel_piece_metrics` position by position, not as one funnel-wide average. Find the line that never moved. That's not your stable touchpoint. That's the one you haven't audited since you built it.

For the electrolyte founder, urgency messaging had been the same generic stock-scarcity line since launch — a line built for a different avatar's decision trigger entirely. It wasn't broken in a way that showed up on a dashboard. It was just never right in the first place, and a flat year of data was the only thing that proved it.

Before you plan next year's creative calendar, find your flat line.
