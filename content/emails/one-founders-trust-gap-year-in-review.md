In January, a mineral-sunscreen founder we'll call Noor ran her first `run_trust_gap`. Her Distinctive score came back at 38 — the lowest of her four IDEA pillars, and the reason, the coach told her, her listing read like every other "reef-safe, non-nano" sunscreen on the page. True claims, zero differentiation. Nothing a skeptical buyer could point to and say "that's why I'm choosing this one."

She didn't rewrite the whole listing that week. She ran `generate_positioning_moves` against what she already had, picked the sharpest move, shipped it on her `amazon_listing_copy`, and waited for real data before touching anything else.

That became the year's rhythm. Four rounds total. Spring: a move built around the specific white-cast complaint buried in her one-star reviews — nobody else in her category was naming it, let alone answering it. Summer: a move reframing "reef-safe" from a virtue claim into a parenting-anxiety answer, tied to the moment a parent is standing in a drugstore aisle reading labels before a beach trip. Autumn: a smaller move, tightening the language around her one genuinely distinctive ingredient. Winter: a move most founders skip entirely — going back and cutting two competing claims that were diluting the sharpest one.

`compute_trust_gap_lift` measured each round against the last, so she wasn't guessing whether a move had worked before committing to the next one. Round one moved Distinctive from 38 to 51. Round two, 51 to 61. Round three added only four points — a reminder that not every round is a big one, and that's fine. Round four, the cutting round, closed out at 71.

Laid out as one chart, the year reads as a single line, not four disconnected experiments: 38, 51, 61, 65, 71. That's the version worth screenshotting before next year's plan gets written — not because 71 is a finish line, but because the line shows the pattern that got her there: one measured move at a time, checked before the next one shipped.

Noor's sunscreen didn't change. Her reviews, her price, her ingredients — all the same. What moved was how precisely her listing named why she's different, round by round, with a number confirming each round before the next began.

If you ran your first `run_trust_gap` this year, pull up the history. See if your line looks like one story, or four scattered guesses.
