A yoga-mat founder we'll call Priya was certain her funnel's problem was her ads. Six months of new creative angles, three different agencies, barely any movement in conversion.

She almost signed with a fourth agency. Instead, she ran `audit_asset` against something she hadn't looked at since she wrote it two years ago: her Brand Store About page.

The score came back rough. Not because the page was badly written — it was fine, professional even. The problem was it had been written for a customer who buys once and moves on. Her actual avatar was someone deciding whether to build a home yoga practice around this brand for years. The About page never spoke to that. It listed the mat's materials and founding year. It never earned the "who's actually behind this" trust her real buyer needed before committing to something they'd use daily.

Priya's assumption wasn't unreasonable. Ads are the thing you can see moving in real time, so they get blamed first. But `audit_asset` scores one specific piece of your funnel against your actual avatar — no waiting for a full audit cycle, just a direct read on the one asset you suspect, at the brand_store_about position or anywhere else.

The lesson isn't "audit everything at random." It's audit the asset your avatar actually visits on the way to buying — not just the one that's easiest to test, or the one with a number attached that's easy to blame.

For Priya, that About page was quietly costing her repeat customers before her ads ever got a fair shot.
