A nonstick-pan founder is about to double her ad budget. Same listing, same funnel, twice the spend — the plan is just "more."

She hasn't audited anything since launch. Eight months of small tweaks, no full look at what's actually live versus what she assumes is live. That's the normal state for a listing that's profitable enough that nobody goes back and checks it.

Doubling spend doesn't fix that. It just pours more traffic through whatever holes are already there, faster.

`list_funnel_inventory` pulls up every touchpoint she actually has live right now — not what she remembers building, what's actually there. `get_funnel_coverage` checks that list against the canonical funnel positions, awareness through advocacy, and flags what's missing.

Ten minutes in, the gap showed up at trust_badges_social_proof. No guarantee language near the buy box, no visible signal that other people bought this and it worked out fine. That's the exact moment — after the click, before "add to cart" — where a colder audience, which is what doubled spend usually buys you, needs the most reassurance and is getting the least.

Before you scale spend, run this checklist:

- `list_funnel_inventory` — what's actually live, not what you think is live
- `get_funnel_coverage` — which canonical positions that inventory doesn't cover
- Take the first gap it flags, not the one that feels most urgent
- Fix that position before the budget doubles, not after

More spend amplifies whatever's already true about your funnel. If trust_badges_social_proof is thin, doubling the budget just buys you a faster, more expensive way to find that out.
