The nursery-decor founder's insert card had one thing on it: a QR code to leave a review.

That's not a touchpoint. It's a hope.

Every card that ships is a funnel position — the moment right after unboxing, when the customer is holding the product and deciding, in about four seconds, whether this brand earned any more of their attention. Most cards waste those four seconds on a code nobody scans, because nothing on the card told them why they'd want to.

`upsert_funnel_touchpoint` logs the insert card as a real position with a real job, not a leftover design task. Once it's logged, you can actually ask what job it should be doing — and the answer isn't "collect a review." That's the advocacy stage's job, later, after trust exists. Right here, at the unboxing moment, the card has two jobs: confirm the customer made a good decision, and give them one clear next move.

The checklist:

- One clear ask — pick ONE thing (join a list, follow an account, register a warranty), not three competing calls to action.
- One trust line — a sentence that answers "was this the right choice," not a slogan.
- Nothing that competes with the unboxing itself — no dense paragraph, no logo wall, no second QR code.
- Log it with `upsert_funnel_touchpoint` so it shows up the next time you check funnel coverage, instead of disappearing into "stuff that ships in the box."

If your card currently does all three at once — review ask, discount code, social handles — it's not a touchpoint, it's a junk drawer. Cut it to the one ask that actually fits four seconds of attention.
