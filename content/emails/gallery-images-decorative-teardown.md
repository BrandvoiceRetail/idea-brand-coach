A knife-sharpening founder sent us his gallery last week. Seven images. Clean lighting, consistent angles, on-brand colour grading. Genuinely nice to look at.

We asked him one question: what is image four's job?

He didn't know. Neither did image two, five, or six. They were all doing the same job — "look premium" — which means five of his seven slots were competing with each other instead of covering ground.

Here's the mini-lesson. Amazon gives you a gallery, not a mood board. Every slot in that grid is a funnel position in miniature: it either answers a specific question a shopper still has, or it's wasted real estate with good lighting. A shopper scrolling your gallery is working through objections one thumbnail at a time — what's included, how big is it really, does it work for my situation, what does it feel like to use. If three images all answer "does it look nice," you've covered one question three times and left the rest of the deck empty.

The fix isn't shooting more photos. It's assigning jobs first. `generate_listing_image_brief` builds the full image-SET brief slot by slot — main image, each gallery position, and the photoreal lifestyle "felt moment" slot that most listings skip entirely. It doesn't hand you seven generic shot ideas. It maps what each position needs to prove, in order, so slot three isn't guessing at the same answer slot two already gave.

The concrete action, today: pull up your live gallery and write one line under each image — the specific question it answers. If you can't finish that sentence, that slot has no job yet. That's the gap `generate_listing_image_brief` is built to fill.
