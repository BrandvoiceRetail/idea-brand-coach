A pet-grooming-clippers founder shipped her listing copy the week she launched. That was eleven months ago. It's still live, word for word.

Nobody decided to leave it alone. She just never had a reason to open the file again — sales were steady enough, nothing was on fire, and there was always a more urgent task than rewriting bullets that "worked fine."

She ran `run_trust_gap` on it anyway, mostly to close out the year. The score came back with the same gap it likely had at launch: weak on Insight-Driven. The bullets described the clippers — quiet motor, five attachment guards, cordless — but never once named the thing her actual buyer is anxious about, which isn't the motor. It's whether they'll nick their dog mid-groom and end up at the emergency vet. Nothing in the copy showed her buyer this brand understood that fear.

That gap doesn't heal on its own. A listing with no Insight-Driven signal in January has no Insight-Driven signal in December, because nothing about static text changes with time. Only a second draft changes it — and a second draft only happens when someone runs the diagnosis that makes the gap visible again.

`generate_brief` turned the finding into something her VA could actually execute: which bullet to rewrite, which phrase to cut, what the new line needs to do instead. Not a vague "add more emotion" note — a designer-ready brief with the specific fix.

If your listing copy has a birthday older than six months, that's not a stability signal. It's a sign nobody has looked. Run `run_trust_gap` on it before you plan next year's launches, and let `generate_brief` turn whatever it finds into draft two.
