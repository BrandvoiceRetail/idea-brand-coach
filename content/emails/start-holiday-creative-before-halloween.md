A reed-diffuser founder does the same thing every year: waits until November, briefs the holiday `amazon_main_image` refresh, then discovers the render queue is three weeks deep because every other seasonal seller had the exact same idea at the exact same time.

It's not a planning failure exactly. November feels early when you're looking at a calendar that says "the holidays haven't started yet." But the render queue doesn't run on the calendar in your head — it runs on when everyone else in your category also decided it was time.

The fix is almost embarrassingly simple: move the brief earlier, not the render. `generate_listing_image_brief` builds the full holiday image-SET now — main plus gallery, including the felt-moment lifestyle slot that actually needs the seasonal styling — while the queue is still short. September feels early for the same reason November felt "on time," but it's the difference between briefing in a quiet queue and briefing in a jam.

Pair it with `generate_main_image_title_plan` at the same sitting. The main image and the title are one positioning statement, not two separate to-dos — if the image goes seasonal and warm while the title still reads generic, the two are working against each other the moment a shopper's eye moves from thumbnail to headline. Lock both together now and you're not scrambling to patch the title later.

This isn't about rushing the creative. It's about giving it the lead time it needs by moving the brief, which costs you an afternoon, instead of moving the render, which you can't control once everyone else's briefs are already in the queue.

The action this week: run `generate_listing_image_brief` and `generate_main_image_title_plan` together for your holiday main image, before Halloween decorations are even up. Next year's founder doing it in November is who you're trying not to be.
