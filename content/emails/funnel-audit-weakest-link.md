Every founder we talk to starts in the same place: the ad number looks wrong, so the ad budget takes the blame.

It's the number you see first every morning, so it's the number you fix first. But an ad's only job is to get someone to click. What happens after the click is a different job entirely, handled by different pieces of your funnel — and none of them show up on the ads dashboard.

Here's the pattern we see constantly: a shopper clicks the ad, lands on the listing, adds to cart, gets to checkout... and bails. Not because the ad was wrong. Because somewhere between "interested" and "paid," something quietly killed their confidence. Shipping and returns policy language is a common, unglamorous culprit — a vague delivery window, a returns process that reads like a legal disclaimer, nothing that reassures a nervous buyer at the exact moment they're deciding whether to trust you with their card.

You can't see that from a bid report. You need to look at the whole funnel, position by position, not just the position with the biggest number attached to it.

That's what `run_funnel_audit` does. It overlays your customer avatar against every touchpoint in your funnel — listing, checkout, shipping policy, unboxing, all of it — and ranks each one by how well it's actually landing with that specific buyer. It doesn't guess. It tells you which position is the weakest link right now, based on evidence.

So before you cut spend on a campaign that might be doing exactly what it's supposed to do, run the audit. If the leak is sitting in your shipping_returns_policy copy, no amount of creative testing upstream fixes it — you'd be optimizing the wrong step.
