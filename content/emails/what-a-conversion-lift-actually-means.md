A standing-desk founder we'll call Ines split-tested two main images last quarter. Variant B won — a real, measurable lift in click-through. She couldn't say why.

That's a common spot to land in. You get a number, but not a diagnosis. Was it the lighting? The angle? Pure noise? Without knowing, you can't repeat it on the next image, and you can't defend the change when someone on your team asks why you switched.

`get_experiment_lift` ties the lift number back to the specific creative change that produced it — not just "B beat A," but which element of B moved the needle at the amazon_main_image position it was tested at. For Ines, the win wasn't the lighting or the crop. It was that Variant B showed someone actually using the desk mid-workday, hand on a coffee mug, glancing at a screen — a felt moment, not a product-on-white shot.

That's where `compute_trust_gap_lift` closed the loop. Run before and after the swap, it showed the lift tracked a 6-point gain specifically on the Empathetic pillar — the score that measures whether your listing shows you understand the customer's actual day, not just the product's specs. The CTR lift wasn't luck. It was Variant B finally proving Ines understood what "using this desk" looks like for her buyer.

That's the difference between a metric and a diagnosis. A lift number tells you something moved. `get_experiment_lift` plus `compute_trust_gap_lift` tells you what moved, and which part of your brand's trust with the customer it actually repaired.

Next time a number moves and you can't explain it, don't just bank the win. Trace it back to the pillar it fixed — then you know how to earn the next lift on purpose instead of by accident.
