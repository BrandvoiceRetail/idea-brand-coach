An insulated-cooler founder's Shopify PDP never once threw a red flag this year. Conversion didn't crater. No angry emails. No obvious drop on any dashboard. So it never made the list of pages to look at — why would it, when nothing was visibly wrong?

That's exactly the gap that survives a whole year untouched. The pages that get audited are the ones that scream. The pages that quietly underperform, month after month, never make it onto anyone's desk, because "quietly underperform" doesn't look like a fire.

She ran `audit_asset` on the PDP cold, at year-end, mostly to close the loop before planning next year. The score came back with a stale Distinctive dimension — the page described the cooler's ice-retention numbers and material specs accurately, but never gave a buyer comparing four nearly identical coolers a real reason this one was different. It read like every competitor's spec sheet with a different logo.

`run_trust_gap` confirmed it wasn't a one-off reading. The Distinctive gap had been sitting there since spring, when three new competitors entered the category and the page's language stopped being different from anyone else's — it just never registered as a problem because nothing about the page itself changed.

That's the mechanism worth remembering: a page can go stale not because it got worse, but because the market around it moved and the page didn't. No red flag will ever catch that. Only a deliberate check will.

The action before next year's plan: pick the one page in your funnel that hasn't been audited simply because nothing's gone wrong with it. Run `audit_asset` on it, then `run_trust_gap` to confirm what it finds. Quiet isn't the same as fine.
