A camping-tent founder sent us four AI-generated ad shots last week. Same product, technically. Four different tents — different pole colors, different rainfly shapes, one with a phantom third door that doesn't exist on the real thing.

That's the tell of missing reference-kit discipline, not a bad model. The generation tool doesn't know what your product actually looks like unless you tell it, the same way, every time.

For paid_social_creative, this matters more than almost anywhere else in the funnel. A shopper who's seen your tent look like three different products across three ad variants has stopped trusting any of them — including the real one waiting on the listing.

The fix isn't a better prompt. It's a kit: a product sheet built from your actual product photo, not a description of it. Character sheets if a presenter or hand model appears, so the same face and state persist scene to scene. And an edit-before-regenerate habit — when one panel drifts, fix that panel, don't reroll the whole render and hope for better luck.

`generate_video_storyboard` is where this discipline earns its keep. Feed it a real reference kit before the next storyboard-image or per-scene handoff, and what Higgsfield renders back actually matches what your customer will unbox. Skip the kit, and you're paying for polish on a product that doesn't exist.

This week's checklist: before your next ad render, build the kit first — product sheet, character sheet if needed — then run the `generate_video_storyboard` handoff. Pull up the ad variants you're running right now. If the product looks different from one to the next, that's not a style choice. That's the tell.
