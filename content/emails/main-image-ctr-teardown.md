An outdoor camp-knife founder had a strange problem. Shoppers who landed on his listing converted well, close to double his category's average. But almost nobody was landing. CTR sat under category average, month after month.

The instinct here is usually a new lifestyle photo. Knife by the campfire. Knife in someone's hand slicing rope. A prettier version of the same idea, shot again.

That's the wrong lever, and it's worth understanding why. In the search grid, the shopper hasn't read anything yet. The main image and the title are the entire pitch, and they're not two separate pitches, they're one positioning statement that either agrees with itself or doesn't. A gorgeous campfire shot paired with a title that leads with a generic keyword isn't one strong pitch. It's two weak ones sitting next to each other.

We ran `generate_main_image_title_plan` against his listing. It treats the image and the first roughly 80 characters of the title as a single unit, built around brand, a real keyword, and the actual difference, and it produces a CTR split-test plan rather than a single new asset to guess with.

The plan didn't call for a new photo at all. It called for a new title that named what actually made this knife different from the ten nearly identical ones sitting above it in the grid, paired with cropping the existing image tighter on the one detail that difference was about.
