A baby-swaddle-blanket founder added two things this year: a welcome series in the spring, and an insert card in the summer box redesign. Both felt like wins at the time. Both went straight into the "done" pile and stayed there.

She hadn't checked either one since.

`list_funnel_inventory` pulls up every touchpoint you've actually logged — not the ones you remember building, the ones that are still on record as live. `get_funnel_coverage` then checks whether each logged position is actually doing its job for your avatar, or whether it's a gap dressed up as coverage.

Here's the checklist before you plan next year:

- Pull `list_funnel_inventory` and read it against your own memory. Anything you built that isn't on the list either never got logged or quietly got dropped.
- Run `get_funnel_coverage` and look specifically at anything you added mid-year. New touchpoints get built with attention and then forgotten the moment the next fire starts.
- Check the insert card by name. It's cheap to design and easy to assume is "handled" once it's printed — which makes it the touchpoint most likely to go stale without anyone noticing.
- Flag anything covered by a touchpoint that no longer matches your avatar. A card written for last year's launch positioning is a gap, even if the box has never shipped without it.

For the swaddle-blanket founder, the insert card had shipped in every single box since summer — logged, present, technically covered. But `get_funnel_coverage` showed it was still written for a first-time-parent avatar, and her actual repeat buyers — the ones this card should have been nudging toward the referral program — never got a version speaking to them.

Coverage on paper isn't coverage that works. Before you plan next year's touchpoints, confirm the ones you already built are still doing their job.
