A yoga-mat founder's welcome series opens at 61% on email one. By email five, that's down to 9%.

Every founder assumes the drop is gradual — a little erosion each send. It rarely is. Usually there's one email where the number falls off a cliff, and everything after it is just people who already checked out.

`get_sequence_performance` shows you the sequence email by email, not just the blended average. Pull it up for your welcome_series and look for the exact step where the line breaks, not the general downward slope.

Here's the tell, once you find it: the email right before the drop is usually still talking to the person who just bought — their reason, their problem, their "finally" moment. The email at the drop is usually the one that starts talking about the brand instead — founder story, values, "here's who we are." Nothing wrong with that email on its own. Wrong place in the sequence.

Buyers open email one because they still feel the purchase. By email three or four, that feeling is fading, and if the copy hasn't given them a reason to keep opening — a use tip, a mistake to avoid, a way to get more out of what they just bought — they stop.

The concrete move: run `get_sequence_performance`, find your cliff email, and read the one immediately before it. Whatever question that email was still answering for the buyer, the next one probably stopped answering it.

Fix that gap before you touch subject lines. A better subject line on a self-referential email just gets more people to open the wrong thing faster.
