An embroidery-kit founder we'll call Priya kept seeing the same comment under her influencer UGC: "I thought this was for experts." Every video showed a finished piece — gorgeous, intricate, obviously the work of someone with steady hands and years of practice. The comment wasn't a compliment. It was someone talking themselves out of buying.

Here's the trap: Priya had built her avatar around who she wanted to reach — "creative, ambitious hobbyists" — not around the fear actually stopping them. Wanting to make something and believing you're capable of it are two different jobs, and only one of them showed up anywhere in her funnel.

That's what `build_avatar_stage` catches that a quick persona sketch doesn't. It moves in stages — vocabulary, then job-to-be-done, then decision triggers, then objections. Stage four is where the real blockers surface: not "is this cute" but "will I embarrass myself and quit." Once Priya actually ran her evidence through it, the pattern was everywhere — comments, DMs, even her own return reasons. The influencer content had been showcasing mastery to people who needed permission to be a beginner.

That's the whole job of the influencer_ugc position: it's the first place a stranger decides whether this brand is "for people like me." Content that only shows the flawless, finished version lets a nervous beginner write herself out of the story before she ever reaches the listing.

Once the objection had a name, the fix wasn't a new creator or a bigger budget. It was the opening line. `generate_ugc_ad_plan` builds scripts straight from the avatar's real, evidenced objections, including the "I thought X, but…" skeptic-flip structure — say the fear out loud first ("I thought this was way too advanced for me"), then flip it in the same breath. Same creators, same kits, one different sentence at the top.

If your influencer content keeps getting liked but not bought, don't reach for more reach. Ask what fear is sitting underneath the silence — that's a `build_avatar_stage` question, not a media-buying one.
