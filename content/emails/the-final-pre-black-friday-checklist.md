A kitchen-knife-set founder we'll call Felix has one week until Black Friday and no single view of what's live, what's weak, or what's missing entirely. He's been fixing things piecemeal for months — a listing tweak here, an updated main image there — without ever stepping back to see the whole funnel at once.

One week out is too late for a rebuild. It's exactly enough time for three tools, run in order.

`list_funnel_inventory` first. It's the inventory check — every touchpoint you actually have live, awareness through advocacy, in one list. No opinions yet, just what exists.

`get_funnel_coverage` next. It lays that inventory against the full canonical map and shows the gaps plainly. For Felix, urgency_messaging came back essentially empty — no countdown, no stock-level signal, no deadline language anywhere in the funnel, despite Black Friday being the single highest-urgency week of his year.

`run_funnel_audit` last, and fast. Not a full forensic pass — a quick overlay of the avatar against what's live, to catch whether the thin spots are actually costing conversions with this year's traffic, not just theoretically incomplete.

For Felix, the order mattered. Coverage told him urgency_messaging was empty. The audit confirmed it was empty at the exact moment shoppers were primed to expect a deal signal and getting silence instead — no "12 left," no countdown, nothing that told a comparison-shopping buyer why today mattered more than next week.

That's a fixable gap with a week left. It would not have been fixable with a day left, and it's invisible if you only ever look at your best-performing asset instead of the whole map.

If you haven't run this pass yet and Black Friday is close, this is the order to run it in: inventory, then coverage, then audit. Each one answers a question the last one couldn't.
