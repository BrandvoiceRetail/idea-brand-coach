An air-fryer founder we'll call Renata watched five near-identical listings undercut her price every month. Same shape, same steel finish, same "6-quart family size" headline. She couldn't out-discount them and didn't want to try.

The clones weren't winning on product. They were winning because nobody's brand story said anything a shopper couldn't get from any of the other five tabs open in the same search.

`generate_signature` didn't ask her to invent a new angle. It asked what her brand actually knows that the clones don't say out loud. The answer wasn't capacity — every listing already claimed that. It was counter space. Her customers weren't buying "cooks six servings." They were buying "doesn't eat my counter for the rest of the day," a fact sitting in a spec table on every competing listing, never said out loud as a claim.

One line: not bigger, just smaller where it counts. That became the signature.

`generate_positioning_moves` then mapped exactly where it needed to land — first line of `amazon_brand_story`, not buried in paragraph three where nobody scrolling five tabs deep would find it.

The clones are still there. They're still cheaper. Renata's listing is the only one that says something the others can't copy without contradicting their own "biggest capacity" claim.

If your category is full of clones, the fix usually isn't a bigger claim. It's the one true thing about your brand nobody else can say without lying about theirs — and it's often been sitting in your own reviews or founder notes the whole time, unclaimed.
