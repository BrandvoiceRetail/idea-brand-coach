A home-office lamp founder sent us her A+ content last week, proud of it. Five beats, clean layout, good photography. She'd approved every slide on her studio monitor.

Then she opened it on her phone.

The second beat's headline wrapped onto three lines and pushed the supporting photo below the fold. The beat order, which made sense left-to-right on a wide screen, read like a non-sequitur stacked vertically. Nothing was *wrong*, exactly. It just stopped working the moment the canvas got narrow.

This is the gap almost every founder misses: A+ content and Shopify PDP modules get designed on desktop and shipped to a phone. Most of your traffic is on that phone right now.

`generate_aplus_content_plan` treats the page as one continuous 1472×3008 editorial composition across five addressable beats — and it carries mobile rules as part of that plan, not an afterthought. Here's the checklist version, three things to check on your own page today:

1. **Headline length at narrow width.** If a headline needs three lines on a phone to say what it said in one on desktop, it's not the same headline anymore.
2. **Beat order that survives stacking.** Side-by-side beats that build an argument left-to-right need to still build that argument top-to-bottom.
3. **The photo that disappears.** Anything cropped tight for a wide layout often loses its subject entirely once it's squeezed into a phone-width frame.

You don't need to rebuild the page to catch this. Pull up your current shopify_pdp A+ module on your own phone right now, scroll it slowly, and note where you'd stop reading. That's your first fix.
