A garage epoxy-kit founder had a video ad on his roadmap for over a year. Every quarter it moved back a slot, always for the same reason: no budget for production.

Here's the thing nobody said to him sooner — the budget isn't the actual blocker. The plan is.

Most founders picture the cost of a video ad as one number: the shoot. Crew, location, editing, all bundled into a single scary line item that never quite gets approved. But the shoot is the *execution* cost. The bigger risk is spending on the wrong scenes because nobody planned the shots first.

`generate_video_storyboard` in per-scene precision mode does the planning before a dollar goes toward rendering. It maps out each scene's visual, voiceover, and on-screen text, scene by scene, so you know exactly what you're paying for and why it's in the ad, before you commit spend to producing it. That's the difference between "video ads are expensive" and "this specific shot list is worth this specific spend."

The action isn't to find the budget. It's to reverse the order: plan the scenes first, price the render second. Run `generate_video_storyboard` against the ad concept you've been shelving, see what the scene-by-scene plan actually calls for, then decide what it's worth spending against a plan you can defend — not a vague idea you've been avoiding pricing out.

Most founders discover the plan needs far less than they assumed once it's broken into scenes instead of one undifferentiated production.
