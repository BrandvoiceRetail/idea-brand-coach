A ceramic-mug founder built her unboxing for one buyer: the person who picked the mug, ordered it, and is now opening their own box. Tissue reveal, a card that says "thank you for choosing us," a QR code to the brand story.

In December, that buyer becomes the minority.

Half her holiday orders will be opened by someone who didn't choose the mug at all — a gift recipient unwrapping something a sister or coworker picked for them. "Thank you for choosing us" means nothing to a person who chose nothing. The QR code assumes a relationship that hasn't started yet.

That's the packaging_unboxing blind spot every gift season creates. The box was designed for intent, and gift season delivers indifference instead. Nothing about the packaging is broken for its original audience — it just has a second audience now, and nobody wrote for them.

`audit_asset` scores one specific piece of your funnel against a specific avatar. Run it against your unboxing sequence, but frame the avatar as the recipient, not the buyer. Does the card explain what the brand is to someone meeting it cold? Does the reveal earn a "who made this" moment, or does it assume the answer's already known?

You don't need to rebuild the box. You need to know whether the copy inside it still lands when nobody chose to be there.

Run `audit_asset` against your unboxing this week — once with your usual buyer in mind, once as the person who just unwrapped a gift. If the second read falls flat, that's the fix worth making before December orders start shipping, not after.
