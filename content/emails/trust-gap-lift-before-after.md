"It feels better" is not a metric. It's a founder's relief after finally shipping a rewrite she'd been putting off for weeks.

A baby-sleep-sack founder we talked with rewrote her listing copy — swapped a bullet about fabric weight for a line about a baby actually staying asleep through the night — and was confident the new version was stronger. It read stronger. But confidence isn't proof.

Listing copy is the funnel position where founders most often declare victory on vibes. There's no simple before/after number sitting on the page the way CTR or add-to-cart rate has one. The real test is whether the specific pillar that was weak — in her case, Empathetic, because the old copy sold the fabric instead of the exhaustion it solved — actually moved.

That's what `compute_trust_gap_lift` measures. It doesn't ask how the new copy feels. It runs the same Trust Gap assessment on the before and after versions and reports the exact delta on the pillar that was weak. If Empathetic went from 44 to 58, that's the lift. If it moved two points, the rewrite didn't do what you think it did, no matter how much better the sentence reads out loud.

This matters more than it sounds like it should, because "the new copy feels stronger" is exactly the trap that leads founders to call a fix finished and move on to the next problem, while the actual weak pillar quietly stays weak underneath a nicer sentence.

Before you call any listing rewrite done, run the before/after. If the number moved on the pillar that was actually broken, ship it with confidence. If it didn't, you've learned that faster than waiting three flat months to find out the hard way.
