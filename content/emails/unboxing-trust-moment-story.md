Dev, a coffee founder we'll call him, spent a year assuming his listing copy was carrying the trust-building work. Reviews kept mentioning the box.

Not the coffee. The box — the way it opened, the card tucked inside, the smell when the bag first came out. Customers were describing an experience his listing never mentioned once.

That's a common blind spot. Founders write for the moment someone's deciding to buy, then treat everything after checkout as fulfilment, not marketing. But the fifteen seconds between a box arriving and a customer deciding whether to leave a review happen entirely inside `packaging_unboxing` — and for a lot of buyers, that's where the actual trust gets built or lost, not the listing photos they scrolled past weeks earlier.

Dev hadn't scripted that moment. It had just been happening, unrepeated and unrecorded, doing real work nobody was capturing.

`generate_ugc_ad_plan` has an unboxing format built for exactly this: it casts the persona from the actual customer avatar, then scripts the beats of that moment — the reveal, the small surprise detail, the reaction — the same way his real customers were already describing it in reviews, just deliberately instead of by accident.

The lesson isn't "add more packaging inserts." It's noticing which fifteen-second moment in your own funnel is quietly doing your best trust-building work, and treating it as an asset worth scripting on purpose, instead of a lucky accident you keep hoping repeats itself.
