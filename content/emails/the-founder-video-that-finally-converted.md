Dez, a camping-hammock founder we'll call him, had posted twelve founder-social videos in four months. Same format every time: him in front of the product, talking through fabric weight, weight capacity, pack size. Views were fine. Nothing converted.

He figured the fix was better production — a tripod instead of handheld, better light. It wasn't the camera.

`identify_decision_trigger` looks at what a purchase is actually asking someone to feel: permission, recognition, identity, belonging, momentum, or fear of losing out. For Dez's hammock, the lever wasn't fear_of_loss or permission. It was belonging — his buyer wasn't shopping for gear, they were shopping for the version of themselves who finally takes the solo trip.

Twelve videos had sold spec. Zero had sold the trip.

Once the lever was named, `generate_ugc_ad_plan` built the script around it — not a features rundown, but a hook cast from the actual avatar: someone who's been meaning to go, alone, for a while now. The talking points still covered fabric and pack size. They just came after the hook earned the feeling, not before it.

None of this needed a bigger budget or a new camera. It needed the trigger named first, so the same founder, same product, same iPhone shot a completely different video.

The lesson for founder_social specifically: you're the most trusted face in your own funnel, which means the wrong angle from you costs more than the wrong angle from a stranger would. Name the trigger before you name the specs.

If you've got a founder video that isn't moving anything, don't reshoot it yet. Find the lever first.
