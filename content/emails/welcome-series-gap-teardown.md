A candle founder had genuinely great unboxing — tissue paper, a handwritten-style thank-you card, a QR code to a founder video. Real craft went into those fifteen seconds.

Then thirty days of nothing.

No welcome email. No second touch. No reason for that customer to think about the brand again until the candle burned down and they were back on Amazon, comparing her listing against six others with the same fresh eyes as a stranger.

Running `get_funnel_coverage` on her funnel didn't surface a subtle issue. It flagged `welcome_series` as flat-out empty — not weak, not underperforming, absent. A canonical funnel position with zero coverage, sitting right after the best-executed touchpoint she had.

This is the teardown worth sitting with: the unboxing moment and the welcome series are the same job, done at two different distances. One earns trust in someone's hands. The other keeps earning it once the box is empty and the candle's on a shelf. Nail the first and skip the second, and you're re-selling every customer from zero the next time they think about buying.

The fix isn't complicated once it's named. It's just been sitting in the "someday" pile instead of the "diagnosed gap" pile — and those get treated very differently. A someday task waits for a slow week. A diagnosed gap gets scheduled this week, because you now know exactly what it's costing you every day it stays empty.

The action: run `get_funnel_coverage` and look specifically at `welcome_series`. If it's empty, that's not a future project. That's a finding, today — and probably the single highest-leverage fix sitting on your list right now.
