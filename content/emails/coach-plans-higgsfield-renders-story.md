A jade-roller founder came into a working session with a request that sounded simple: "make me a better main image." She had a vague picture in her head — soft lighting, maybe a hand holding the roller — and expected the AI to just render it.

That's not how the coach works, and it's worth explaining why, because the assumption is common and it costs founders their best shot at a good image.

`generate_listing_image_brief` ran first. Not an image — a full plan for the listing image set: what the main image needs to prove, what each gallery slot is for, and where the photoreal lifestyle "felt moment" sits in the sequence. It specified the reference kit too — the actual product photo to build a product sheet from, so the render matches her real roller and not a generic jade-colored stand-in. Vague pictures in a founder's head don't specify any of that, which is exactly why "just make me an image" so often comes back looking like nothing in particular.

Only once that brief existed did `generate_image`, on Higgsfield, do its job: execute the plan. Reference kit in, brief in, render out. The coach writes the plan and the prompts; Higgsfield does the rendering. Neither one does the other's job, and skipping the brief to go straight to a render is how founders end up with a pretty image that doesn't say anything on their behalf in the search grid.

The brief is the product here, not the render. A render without a brief is a guess with good lighting. A brief without a render is still a plan you could hand to any designer or generation tool and get the same result. The founder's actual leverage was in the fifteen minutes spent on what the image needed to prove — her difference, her avatar's felt moment — not in the seconds it took to generate it.

For the jade-roller founder, that meant the main image finally led with the thing that made her roller different, not just a nicer photo of a roller.

If your main image was born from a vague prompt instead of a brief, that's worth revisiting before your next round of creative spend.
