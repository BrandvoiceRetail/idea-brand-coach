A cast-iron cookware founder rewrote her opening line last month. Not the bullets, not the A+ content — one sentence.

The old version: "Pre-seasoned cast iron for even heat retention." True, but nobody's afraid of uneven heat. It's a material claim. It answers a question nobody asked.

The new version speaks to the actual fear: sticking, smoking, ruining the first meal you cook in it for guests. `run_trust_gap` had already flagged Empathetic as her weakest pillar — the listing was fluent in cast iron, silent on the moment someone's actually standing at the stove, nervous.

She swapped the line. Nothing else. Then ran `compute_trust_gap_lift` to see the before/after delta instead of trusting the feeling that it read better.

Empathetic moved 14 points.

That's the part worth sitting with. Not the number itself — illustrative, hers, not a promise for yours — but that she isolated ONE variable. If she'd rewritten the whole opening paragraph, changed the hero image, and added a new bullet in the same pass, she'd have a better listing and zero idea which change earned it. Next quarter, when something regresses, she'd be debugging three things instead of one.

This is the discipline most rewrites skip. A founder gets nervous about a stalled CVR, opens the listing editor, and changes everything at once out of urgency. It feels like progress. It teaches you nothing.

The mini-lesson: treat `amazon_listing_copy` like a lab, not a canvas. One line, one measurement, one verdict. Then the next line.

If you're about to rewrite a listing this week, don't. Run `run_trust_gap` first, find the actual weak pillar, change the single sentence that's most responsible for it, and measure with `compute_trust_gap_lift` before touching anything else.
