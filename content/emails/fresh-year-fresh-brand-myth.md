A skincare founder came into January wanting a new brand. Not a new product — a new brand. New name energy, new visual language, the whole reset.

Nothing had actually gone wrong. She was just restless. Eleven months of sameness will do that to anyone.

Here's the myth worth killing: a flat brand doesn't mean a wrong brand. It usually means an under-mined one. You built a real position at some point — that's why customers are still buying — and then you stopped asking what else that position could say.

Instead of a rebrand, she ran `generate_positioning_moves` against her existing signature. It doesn't invent a new identity. It works from the one she already has and finds sharper moves still inside it — angles on the same distinctive line that hadn't been tried yet in her `founder_content`.

Turned out there were three moves she'd never run: one about the ingredient sourcing story she'd mentioned once in a caption and never again, one reframing her "clean beauty" claim around a specific skin fear instead of a general virtue, one built around the founder's own before-photo she'd been too embarrassed to post.

None of that required a new logo. It required actually using the position she'd already earned.

This is the pattern behind most January rebrand urges: it's not the brand that's tired, it's the founder's imagination for what the current one can still do. A rebrand resets the clock and buries months of brand equity nobody had exhausted yet.

Before you touch a logo this year, run `generate_positioning_moves` on what you've already built. If it's genuinely dead, you'll feel that clearly. Usually, it isn't.
