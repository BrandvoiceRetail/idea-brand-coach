A baby-carrier founder doubled the word count on her Amazon brand story. Founding year, sourcing details, a paragraph on the factory, a paragraph on the family behind it. If length read as credibility, this was going to be the most credible brand story in the category.

It didn't move anything. `run_trust_gap` still came back with Distinctive as the weakest pillar, same as before the rewrite.

The myth worth killing: more brand story doesn't mean more trust, it means more words for someone to skim past on a mobile screen. Distinctive isn't a length problem. It's a "could a stranger tell this brand apart from the other twelve in the category" problem, and you can fail that test in 40 words just as easily as in 400.

Her `amazon_brand_story` said real things. None of them were things only her brand could say. Any competitor's founder could swap in their own factory paragraph and their own family photo and the story would read identically.

The fix wasn't more paragraphs, it was one line. `generate_signature` works from what already makes the brand actually different, not a fresh brainstorm, and produces the one distinctive sentence that's genuinely hers. She replaced four paragraphs with that single line, repeated at the top of the brand story and echoed once in the closing.

Shorter page. Sharper pillar. The words she cut weren't wrong, they just weren't distinctive, and distinctive is the thing that pillar is actually measuring.

Before you add another paragraph to a brand story that isn't converting, run `run_trust_gap` and check whether Distinctive is actually the gap. If it is, you need one sharp line, not more of them.
