A tea founder's welcome email is one message: a 10% code and a "thanks for joining!" Then nothing until the next promotion.

That's not a welcome series. That's a discount with a greeting attached.

New customers aren't ready to buy again in the first 48 hours. They're deciding whether the thing they just bought was a good call, and whether this brand deserves the next purchase too. Skip that decision-forming window with a single email and jump straight to "buy more," and you're asking for trust you haven't built yet.

`create_email_sequence` runs a five-step welcome flow built for exactly this position in the `welcome_series`: not five variations on the same discount pitch, but five distinct jobs. Confirm the decision was a good one. Show the product being used the way real customers actually use it. Answer the objection that almost stopped the sale. Introduce who's actually behind the brand. Only then, once trust exists, make the next offer. Each step earns the next one instead of repeating it.

For the tea founder, running the real sequence meant her second email wasn't a coupon — it was three lines on how to actually brew the loose-leaf blend she'd just shipped, something her own product page barely covered. That's the email people replied to.

If your welcome series right now is one email, or five emails that all sound like the first one, `create_email_sequence` builds the structure a single blast can't: reasons to stay, laid out in the order a new customer actually needs them, with the discount saved for last instead of leading with it.
