A tool-organizer founder we'll call Sam swaps his Shopify PDP hero image every few weeks — new background, new angle, sometimes just a different crop. Each time, he'd put off checking whether the change actually held up, because he assumed that meant running a full funnel audit first.

It doesn't. That's what `audit_asset` is for.

`run_funnel_audit` is the right call when you need the big picture: every touchpoint, scored against your avatar, weakest link surfaced. That's a real commitment — you run it occasionally, not every time you tweak a photo.

`audit_asset` is the other tool in that pair, built for exactly Sam's situation. Point it at one asset — his shopify_pdp hero, nothing else — and it scores that single image against his actual avatar in isolation. Pass or fail, with the specific reason, in about the time it takes to check a metric.

For Sam, that meant he could swap the hero, run `audit_asset` right away, and know within minutes whether the new crop still spoke to the buyer who's organizing a garage, not browsing a lifestyle catalog. No waiting for a weekly cycle. No re-scoring five other pages he hadn't touched.

The habit worth building: `run_funnel_audit` for the periodic full picture, `audit_asset` for every small change in between. Most founders only know the first one exists, then skip checking their own edits because the full version feels like too much for one image.

One tweak, one asset, one verdict. That's the whole point of having both tools instead of just one.
