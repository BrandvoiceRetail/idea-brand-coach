Read five coffee roastery About pages back to back. "Small-batch." "Ethically sourced." "Passion for the craft." Swap the logos and nobody would notice.

A roastery founder hit this wall directly: her `brand_store_about` page was technically well-written and completely interchangeable. It said all the right things a coffee brand is supposed to say. It said nothing that was only hers.

That's the gap Distinctive is built to catch. Insight-Driven and Empathetic can be true and still generic — you can accurately describe your beans and your care and still sound like the category, not like a brand.

She ran `generate_signature`, which doesn't ask "what do you want to say about your brand." It works from what's actually distinctive in her story — in her case, that she roasts in five-pound batches specifically so the profile can shift week to week with what's actually good right now, not what's consistent. Everyone in her category brags about consistency. She was quietly doing the opposite, and had never said so directly.

One line came out of it. Something short enough to say out loud to a customer in her shop.

The action isn't "write a better About page." It's narrower: take that one line and put it above the fold, before the origin story, before the founder photo, before anything else. The signature earns its place first. The paragraphs that follow should support it, not bury it under three hundred words of context nobody asked for yet.

Most About pages fail the same way: too much story, no line the reader can repeat back. If your `brand_store_about` reads fine and feels forgettable, that's the tell.

Run `generate_signature`, find the line, put it at the top.
