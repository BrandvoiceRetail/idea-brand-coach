A cat-litter-subscription founder we'll call Renata watched her conversion dip through November and told herself the obvious story: it's Q4, everyone's distracted by holiday spending, it'll sort itself out in January.

That story is comforting. It's also, for most founders who tell it, wrong.

She ran `run_trust_gap` on the same listing she'd been checking all year and compared it against the score she'd logged months earlier, not just this month's read. The gap hadn't opened in Q4. It had been sitting at roughly the same width since spring — March, specifically, right after she'd rewritten her `urgency_messaging` around "subscribe now, price locks in" language.

Q4 didn't create the problem. Q4 just made it visible, because holiday-season buyers are pickier, and a weak urgency message that barely limped along in a quiet market falls apart entirely in a crowded one.

`identify_decision_trigger` showed why the fix she'd been assuming — tighten the urgency copy, add a countdown, push harder on scarcity — was never going to work. Her buyer's actual lever isn't urgency. It's momentum: the feeling of already being partway through a routine that's working, and not wanting to break the streak by switching or lapsing. A cat owner three months into a litter subscription isn't deciding whether to act fast. She's deciding whether disrupting a working routine is worth the risk. Urgency language answers a question nobody's asking. Momentum language — "you're on month three, here's what changes at month six" — answers the one that's actually live.

"We'll fix it in January" is a trap because it assumes the calendar is the cause. The calendar just changes how forgiving the market is of a gap that was already there. If your dip started before Q4 got difficult, January won't fix it either — the same mismatched lever will just find a quieter way to keep costing you.

Before you write this quarter off as seasonal, compare your latest `run_trust_gap` score against the one you logged a few months back, not just last month's. If the gap predates the dip, the fix isn't a January reset. It's the lever.
