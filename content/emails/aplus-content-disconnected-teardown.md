A pet-grooming founder showed us her A+ module last week. Five modules, each one clean and on-brand. Module one: brand intro. Module two: product benefits. Module three: how it works. Module four: more benefits. Module five: comparison chart.

Read top to bottom, it doesn't build anywhere. It restates the same two or three points in different layouts. That's what happens when A+ content gets designed as five separate briefs handed to five separate design requests, stapled together in Seller Central's module order — because that's usually exactly what happened.

The mini-lesson: your amazon_brand_story position is one continuous scroll, not five independent slides. A shopper who's already scrolled past your bullets and is now reading A+ content is asking one unbroken question — "convince me this is the right brand, not just a working product." That question needs a beginning, a middle, and a close. Five modules that each restate "we're great" from a slightly different angle answer the same shallow version of that question five times and never get deeper.

`generate_aplus_content_plan` builds the module as one continuous 1472×3008 editorial composition instead of five disconnected requests. It plans five addressable beats that each move the story forward — not five variations on the same beat — plus the mobile rules that keep the sequence coherent on a phone screen, where most of this traffic actually reads it.

The action this week: read your current A+ content top to bottom in one sitting, out loud if you can stand it. If beat three could swap places with beat five and nothing would feel wrong, you don't have a story yet — you have five slides. That's exactly what a fresh `generate_aplus_content_plan` pass is for.
