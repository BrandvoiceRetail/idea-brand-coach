## Strategy

Her felt pain: *"I built my gallery like a fabric swatch book. Nine shots. Every one proved the materials. None of them proved the relief."* She was proud of the polish — glass-bead texture macros, tidy stitching close-ups — and none of it answered why a shopper who can't sleep would actually buy this.

The funnel position is `amazon_listing_copy`: the gallery order, where someone buying a product meant to solve a felt problem needs to see a person actually experiencing the relief, not just fabric quality. `generate_listing_image_brief` names the exact missing slot — someone actually asleep under it — the photoreal lifestyle "felt moment" the tool is built to surface.

The hook runs on **belonging** — customers need to see someone like them getting the relief, not just proof the blanket is well made.

## Hook variants

1. **Belonging:** "Every shot in my gallery proves the materials. Not one proves someone actually got relief from it."
2. **Identity:** "I was proud of every texture shot. None of them looked like the person buying it."
3. **Recognition:** "I've scrolled past plenty of 'well-shot' listings without a second thought. Turns out mine was getting the same scroll."

## Production notes (Higgsfield)

- **Mode:** Per-scene precision — talking-head beats (1, 2, 5) alternate with exact screen-UI states (3, 4), needing individual scene control rather than one storyboard panel.
- **Reference kit:** Founder character sheet to keep face, wardrobe, and bedroom setting consistent across scenes 1 and 2 (her only on-camera beats); screen-UI mock of the coach's chat and the listing-image brief slot list for scenes 3-4; real product photos of the weighted blanket (folded and texture macro) for the gallery-swipe beat and the cold side of the scene-5 split screen; a warm lifestyle reference (person asleep under a blanket) for the missing-slot beat and the warm side of the scene-5 split screen.
- **Negative prompt / no-text-in-frame:** No captions, labels, or UI text baked into generated frames — all copy added as post overlay per the TEXT ON SCREEN field. The "person asleep" reference must read as a staged, comfortable scene, not a real customer photo or claim.
- **Diegetic audio:** Quiet bedroom ambience, soft fabric rustle when she gestures to the blanket, laptop trackpad/click sounds under scenes 3-4, a light ambient pad rising only under the end card.
