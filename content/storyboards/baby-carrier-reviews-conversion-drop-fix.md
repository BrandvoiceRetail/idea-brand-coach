## Strategy

Her felt pain: *"4.6 stars. I figured that number would carry the page on its own."* She stopped looking past the star average because it looked healthy — and a healthy-looking average is exactly the kind of number that hides a slow, real leak underneath it.

The funnel position is `displayed_reviews`: the section a hesitant new parent reads specifically to resolve doubt before buying, not to admire a rating. `run_trust_gap` scores that section and names **Insight-Driven** as the weakest pillar — the visible reviews never touch the exact safety-anxiety question new parents type first.

The hook runs on **fear_of_loss** — parents scrolling reviews for a baby carrier aren't looking for reassurance in general, they're hunting specifically for the thing that could go wrong, and a review section that never names it loses them at the one moment it should be winning them.

## Hook variants

1. **Fear_of_loss:** "4.6 stars. And still, the exact question new parents type first goes unanswered."
2. **Recognition:** "I figured the star average would carry the page. It didn't. Here's why."
3. **Permission:** "More five-star reviews won't fix this. The one that answers her real fear will."

## Production notes (Higgsfield)

- **Mode:** Per-scene precision — the ad mixes a real analytics dashboard (1), a review-list scroll (2), and exact screen-UI states (3-6); each beat needs individual control rather than one storyboard panel.
- **Reference kit:** Founder character sheet for continuity in any cutaway framing; screen-UI mock of the Amazon Business Reports conversion chart; screen-UI mock of the coach's chat and Trust Gap scorecard; real product photo of the ergonomic baby carrier; a reference of a generic displayed-reviews list layout for scenes 2 and 6.
- **Negative prompt / no-text rule:** No baked-in captions, star-rating widgets, or UI text in generated frames beyond what's specified — all overlay copy is added in post per the TEXT ON SCREEN field. Do not let the model invent extra certification badges, review counts, or reviewer names.
- **Diegetic audio:** Quiet desk/office ambience, mouse-click and keyboard sounds under the UI beats, no underscore until the end-card sting.
