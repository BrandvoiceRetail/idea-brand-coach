## Strategy

Her felt pain, in her own words: *"My image looks exactly as good as theirs. So why do they get the click and I don't?"* She did everything the checklists tell you to do — clean background, on-brand colors, product centered — and still loses the scroll.

The funnel position is `amazon_main_image`: the split-second scroll decision inside the search grid, before a shopper ever reaches the listing page. "On-brand" and "technically correct" don't survive that decision if the image is indistinguishable from the three competitors a shopper already scrolled past.

The hook runs on **recognition** — she already senses something's off; the ad's job is to name the exact thing (a specific IDEA pillar, not a vague "make it pop") and hand her a next step instead of another redesign guess.

## Hook variants

1. **Recognition:** "My image looks exactly as good as theirs. So why do they get the click and I don't?"
2. **Fear_of_loss:** "Every scroll past my listing is a sale I'll never see again."
3. **Permission:** "You don't need a redesign. You need to know which pillar is actually failing."

## Production notes (Higgsfield)

- **Mode:** Per-scene precision — this is a screen-capture-led demo (real UI states, real search-grid comparison) that needs exact control scene by scene, not one multi-panel storyboard image.
- **Reference kit:** Founder character sheet (consistent face/wardrobe for any cutaway); screen-UI mock of the coach's chat + Trust Gap scorecard component; real product photo of the embroidery starter kit box; competitor-thumbnail reference set for the grid comparison beat.
- **Negative prompt / no-text rule:** No text, labels, or captions baked into the generated frames — the UI mocks and thumbnails must render clean; every caption in this storyboard is a post-production overlay per the TEXT ON SCREEN field. Do not hallucinate extra chart numbers, badges, or logos onto the UI mock.
- **Diegetic audio:** Soft trackpad taps and mouse-click sounds timed to cursor movement, quiet home-office room tone, no music bed until the end-card sting.

## CTA & measurement

**End card copy:** "Run your Trust Gap score — free." **Destination:** ideabrandcoach.com/diagnostic. **Metric that proves the ad worked:** click-through rate from the ad to /diagnostic — the same click behavior the ad is diagnosing, so a lift there proves the hook (and the tool reveal) earned the tap.
