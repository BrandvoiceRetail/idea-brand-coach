## Strategy

Nora's felt pain, in her own words: "She DM'd me first. Said she loved the planner layout, asked where to buy. I replied once — and then nothing." At `organic_social_profile`, every one of those DMs is a warm lead who raised her own hand. There's no sequence catching her once the conversation stalls, so the warmth Nora already earned just evaporates.

`create_email_sequence` builds the welcome flow that picks up exactly where the DM left off, timed to fire while someone is still primed to buy. The hook runs on **permission**: the customer already said yes once, unprompted — the brand just never built anything to catch that yes.

## Hook variants

1. **Permission:** "She DM'd me first. Said yes. And I never sent her anything back."
2. **Recognition:** "You've had this DM — warm, excited, gone by the weekend."
3. **Momentum:** "The lead was already warm. I just never built anything to catch it."

## Production notes (Higgsfield)

- **Mode:** Storyboard-image default — one multi-panel storyboard covers the DM scroll, the stalled thread, and the sequence reveal in a single video job; the problem-solution format is simple enough to hold in one panel set.
- **Reference kit:** Persona character sheet for Nora (consistent face, cozy stationery-desk setting); product reference sheet for the planner/journal line from real product photos; a flat screen-UI mock of the DM thread and of the coach chat with the rendered 5-step sequence for scenes 1-4.
- **Negative prompt / no-text-in-frame:** Do not render legible DM copy, timestamps, or UI labels inside generated frames — supply the DM thread and the sequence card as flat reference mocks and add every TEXT ON SCREEN line as a post overlay. On-screen disclosure: "Actor portrayal. Composite scenario for illustration." — Nora is a fictional composite founder, not a verified customer exchange.
- **Diegetic audio:** Quiet room tone, a phone notification chime opening scene 1, soft trackpad clicks under scene 3, no music bed until the end card.

## CTA & measurement

End card copy: "Free Trust Gap diagnostic. See where your warm leads go cold." Destination: `ideabrandcoach.com/diagnostic`. **Metrics that prove it worked:** hook rate and diagnostic-start rate among ad-attributed clicks prove the permission angle landed; the metric that proves the connection problem itself is fixed is open and click rate on the new welcome sequence's first step — whether the email actually catches the DM's warmth before it cools.
