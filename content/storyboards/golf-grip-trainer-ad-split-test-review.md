## Strategy

The felt pain, in her words: "I had three good hooks and no way to know which one to put real budget behind — so I was basically about to guess with my ad spend." The funnel position is `paid_social_creative` — the exact moment a scroller either stops on the ad or keeps scrolling. `design_test` solves the connection problem by turning three untested trigger angles (recognition, identity, belonging) into a structured comparison with a defined sample and one CTR metric, so scaling decisions come from a number, not a favorite.

The hook plays **momentum** — the promise isn't "we found the perfect hook," it's "we found out fast enough to scale this week instead of next quarter."

## Hook variants

1. **momentum** — "I had three ad hooks and 48 hours to find out which one to scale."
2. **recognition** — "If you've got three good ad angles and no idea which to trust with real budget, watch this."
3. **belonging** — "Every founder I know who's tested their ad hooks finds the same thing: it's never actually about the product."

## Production notes (Higgsfield)

- **Mode:** marketing-studio preset for the UGC review format (talking-to-camera founder, natural handheld framing, review-style pacing); screen-recording overlay in scenes 2-3 composited separately, not generated as part of the UGC plate.
- **Reference kit:** persona character sheet (same founder face, garage-workshop setting with golf gear visible, consistent across scenes 1 and 4); product reference sheet built from the real grip-trainer photo; a screen-UI mock of the `design_test` comparison panel showing three labeled trigger-angle test cells.
- **Negative prompt / no-text-in-frame:** no burned-in text, logos, or chart labels inside generated video/image plates — all TEXT ON SCREEN elements are a post overlay pass.
- **Diegetic audio:** light garage-workshop ambience under scenes 1 and 4-5; near-mute under the screen-recording beats (2-3) so VO and a subtle UI-tap sound effect carry it; a small "win" chime under the results reveal in scene 3.
