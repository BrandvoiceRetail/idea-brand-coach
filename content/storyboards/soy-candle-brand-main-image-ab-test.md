## Strategy

The felt pain, in her words: "I just knew the new photo looked better. I didn't think I needed to test it — I just launched it." A week later CTR is down 15% and she can't tell if it's the new image, seasonality, or a fluke, because she never set up a comparison. The funnel position is `amazon_main_image` — the single frame that decides whether a shopper scrolling the search grid even opens the listing. `design_test` solves the actual connection problem here: not "which image is prettier" but "which image earns the click," structured as a hypothesis with a defined sample size and one success metric, before spend gets committed either way.

The hook plays **permission** — she wasn't wrong to have a hunch, she was wrong to skip the step that would have told her if the hunch was right. That's a relief, not a scolding, and it's the emotional door into a coach that turns instinct into a checkable claim.

## Hook variants

1. **permission** — "You don't have to know which photo wins. You just have to be willing to test it."
2. **recognition** — "If you've ever swapped your main image on a hunch and watched CTR drop, this one's for you."
3. **momentum** — "One bad guess cost me a week of clicks. One test would have cost me a day."
