## Strategy

The felt pain, in the founder's words: *"4.8 stars. Nine hundred reviews. And conversion still lags everyone I compete with."* The reviews look fine on the surface — plenty of them, mostly glowing — which is exactly why the real problem stays hidden.

The funnel position is `displayed_reviews`: the section a shopper reads to confirm a decision they're already leaning toward. `run_trust_gap` scans that section and names **Empathetic** as the weakest pillar — every visible review answers "does it work," and not one shows anyone actually understood the fear a dog owner types first: "will my dog actually eat it."

The hook runs on **fear_of_loss** — the review section is confidently confirming the wrong worry, which means every shopper carrying the real fear scrolls past reassurance that was never meant for them.

## Hook variants

1. **Fear_of_loss:** "My reviews all say it works. Not one of them says my dog will actually eat it."
2. **Recognition:** "4.8 stars. Nine hundred reviews. Repeat purchase still lagging. Sound familiar?"
3. **Permission:** "Skip the fifth good review. Get the one that answers what she's actually afraid of."

## Production notes (Higgsfield)

- **Mode:** Marketing-studio preset (UGC review) for scene 1 — a persona-on-camera, product-in-hand testimonial is exactly that preset's target shape; scenes 2-4 switch to per-scene precision for the founder's screen-capture beats.
- **Reference kit:** Persona character sheet for the customer-actor and her dog (consistent face/setting for the review beat); separate founder character sheet for scenes 2-3; screen-UI mock of the coach's chat and Trust Gap scorecard; real product photo of the joint-supplement bag.
- **Negative prompt / no-text rule:** No legible review-platform chrome, star-rating widgets, or UI text rendered inside generated frames — supply those as flat reference images and add every TEXT ON SCREEN line as a post overlay. On-screen disclosure required: "Actor portrayal. Composite scenario for illustration." — never labeled or implied as a verified buyer or real customer testimonial.
- **Diegetic audio:** Warm living-room room tone under scene 1, a dog collar jingle or paw-tap as a light touch, laptop trackpad clicks under scenes 3-4, no music bed until the end-card sting.

## CTA & measurement

**End card copy:** "Find the fear your reviews aren't answering. Free diagnostic." **Destination:** ideabrandcoach.com/diagnostic. **Metric that proves the ad worked:** click-through rate from the ad to /diagnostic proves the hook landed; sessions-to-orders on the listing is the metric that proves the fix worked — the ad argues that strong star ratings can hide a real trust gap, so a lift in click-through now, and a lift in sessions-to-orders once the reviews get reordered, is what proves the argument held up.
