## Strategy

Her own words: *"Every review request I send gets one word back. 'Great product.'"* High reply rate, zero story — proof the ask itself is asking for the wrong thing.

The funnel position is `review_request_flow`: the exact moment a customer is invited to say why the purchase mattered, and instead gets handed a generic "leave a review" prompt that produces a generic answer. `identify_decision_trigger` names the ONE psychological lever this specific purchase runs on — recognition, not relief — so the ask can prompt the actual story instead of a rating.

Hook trigger: **recognition** — she already senses "great product" isn't the review she wanted; the ad names the exact reason the ask never got there.

## Hook variants

1. **Recognition:** "Every review request I send comes back the same two words: 'Great product.' That's not a story. That's a shrug."
2. **Identity:** "My review requests don't ask who they are as a cat owner. So I get nothing back but 'great product.'"
3. **Permission:** "You don't need more reviews. You need one that isn't two words long."
