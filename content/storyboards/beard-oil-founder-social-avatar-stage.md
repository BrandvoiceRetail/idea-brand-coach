## Strategy

His own words: *"Forty-six posts in. Every idea I had left was another bottle shot."* The felt pain isn't lack of effort — it's that every post on `founder_social` was about the product, because he genuinely didn't know why anyone bought it beyond "nice bottle."

The funnel position is `founder_social`: the feed built to carry his voice, not another catalog shot. `build_avatar_stage` runs its four-stage forensic build — S1 vocabulary, S2 job map, S3 triggers, S4 objections — against his actual customers and surfaces the real reason underneath every purchase: it smells like a candle, not a man. That one line is four posts he didn't have yesterday.

Hook trigger: **identity** — the content problem was never a shortage of ideas, it was a shortage of knowing who's actually holding the bottle.

## Hook variants

1. **Identity:** "Forty-six posts of the same bottle. I didn't even know why anyone bought it."
2. **Recognition:** "My content calendar was empty. My customers' actual words weren't."
3. **Belonging:** "Every post I made was about the bottle. Not one was about the guy holding it."

## Production notes (Higgsfield)

- **Mode:** Per-scene precision — the ad alternates a desk performance (1-2, 6) with exact screen-UI states across the four avatar-build stages (3-5); each needs individual control rather than one storyboard panel.
- **Reference kit:** Founder character sheet for continuity across scenes 1, 2, and 6; real product photo of the beard oil bottle; screen-UI mock of the coach's chat and the `build_avatar_stage` stage-progression panel (S1-S4) for scenes 3-5.
- **Negative prompt / no-text-in-frame:** No legible dashboard chrome, stage labels, or review-quote text baked into generated frames beyond flat reference — every TEXT ON SCREEN line is added as a post overlay. Do not let the model invent extra review quotes beyond the one specified.
- **Diegetic audio:** Quiet desk/office ambience and keyboard clicks under scenes 1-4, a soft confirmation chime at the S4 reveal in scene 5, no music bed until the end-card sting.
