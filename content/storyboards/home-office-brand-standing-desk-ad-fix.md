## Strategy

"My ad went semi-viral and almost nobody clicked the link" is the felt pain —
reach without a reason to act. The diagnosis sits at **paid_social_creative**: the
ad earned attention but never made the specific case for why this converter beats
the dozen others in the feed, so views piled up while the link sat untouched.
`generate_main_image_title_plan` fixes the connection problem by building a
positioning statement straight from real customer language, which becomes the
ad's own hook instead of a generic pitch. The hook plays **identity** — viral
reach without a click is just noise with good production value.

## Hook variants

- **identity** — "My ad hit two hundred thousand views. I still don't know if a single one of those people needed my desk."
- **fear_of_loss** — "The DMs came in. The actual link? Nobody touched it."
- **recognition** — "Viral doesn't mean it converts. I found that out the expensive way."

## Production notes (Higgsfield)

- **Mode:** marketing-studio preset — founder-story talking-head format benefits from the UGC preset's persona-led pacing.
- **Reference kit:** persona character sheet for the founder (consistent face, wardrobe, home-office setting across all 5 scenes); product reference sheet from the real standing-desk-converter photo so the unit in scene 1 matches the actual product; screen-UI mock of a generic short-video analytics view and of the coach's tool output card.
- **Negative-prompt / no-text-in-frame:** no real TikTok or platform-trademarked UI chrome rendered; no baked-in text inside generated frames — all TEXT ON SCREEN added as a post overlay; view/click numbers framed as this composite founder's illustrative scenario, never presented as a verified real result.
- **Diegetic audio:** natural room tone, single handheld-mic-style voice take, no underscore until the CTA card.

## CTA & measurement

End card: "Reach isn't the same as resonance. Free diagnostic, 6 questions, no
account." Destination: **ideabrandcoach.com/diagnostic**. The one metric that
proves this ad worked: **click-through rate on the ad's own link** — proof that
naming the real positioning statement, not raw reach, is what drives the click.
