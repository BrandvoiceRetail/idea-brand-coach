## Strategy

Elena's felt pain, in her own words: "I have twelve unboxing videos of this serum. Every single one shows the box, the tissue paper, the bottle. Not one shows the thing everyone's actually afraid of before they click buy." At `packaging_unboxing`, the content is polished and pretty — and it never touches the real objection sitting in her own comment section: *will this irritate my skin?*

`generate_ugc_ad_plan` scripts a try-on-style ad built straight off the customer avatar's real objection — a persona cast from the avatar, a scripted "I thought this would irritate me, but—" skeptic flip, and claim-gated talking points that stay inside what the product actually does. The hook runs on **fear_of_loss**: every scroll past a polished box-only clip is a hesitant buyer who never got shown the moment that would have convinced her.

## Hook variants

1. **Fear_of_loss:** "Every scroll past a hesitant face is a customer talking herself out of buying."
2. **Recognition:** "You've filmed the box a hundred times. Never the second before she almost put it back."
3. **Permission:** "She almost didn't buy. That's the only part of this story worth showing."

## Production notes (Higgsfield)

- **Mode:** Marketing-studio preset (UGC try-on) — the talking-to-camera application beat plus the scripted skeptic-flip line is exactly the preset's target shape; use per-scene precision only for the plan-reveal beat (scene 4), where the skeptic-flip text needs to stay legible and consistent.
- **Reference kit:** Persona character sheet for Elena (founder, desk setting) and a separate character sheet for the try-on UGC actor (consistent face/skin state across scenes 5-6); product reference sheet for the serum bottle from real product photos; a flat screen-UI mock of the coach chat and the rendered ad plan for scenes 3-4.
- **Negative prompt / no-text-in-frame:** Do not render legible comment text, plan copy, or lower-third captions inside generated frames — supply the comment thread and the plan card as flat reference mocks and add every TEXT ON SCREEN line as a post overlay. On-screen disclosure required across scenes 5-7: "Actor portrayal. Composite scenario for illustration." — the UGC actor is never presented as a verified buyer, and no specific irritation or timeline result is claimed as real.
- **Diegetic audio:** Quiet desk room tone under scenes 1-4 (laptop trackpad clicks, a scroll-swipe sound); soft bathroom-counter ambience and a bottle-cap click opening scene 5; no music bed until the end card.

## CTA & measurement

End card copy: "Free Trust Gap diagnostic. Find the objection your ads keep skipping." Destination: `ideabrandcoach.com/diagnostic`. **Metrics that prove it worked:** hook rate and diagnostic-start rate among ad-attributed clicks prove the fear_of_loss angle landed; the metric that proves the connection problem itself is fixed is repeat-purchase rate on the serum — whether naming the hesitation up front turns more once-hesitant, first-time buyers into second-bottle customers.
