## Strategy

The felt pain, in the founder's own words: "People land on my Brand Store, they scroll the About page, and then... nothing." A composite founder we'll call Owen runs a camping-gear brand — tents, packs, the works. His Brand Store traffic looks healthy. Orders from that traffic don't.

The connection problem sits at `brand_store_about`: this is the page a curious visitor reads *before* deciding whether to click back to a listing and buy — the moment the brand either earns "this is for me" or doesn't. Owen's copy says "quality outdoor gear, built to last." So does every other camping brand's About page. The coach runs `run_trust_gap` on the page and isolates **Distinctive** as the weakest pillar — technically fine, forgettable in practice.

The hook plays **belonging**: nothing on the page tells this specific camper — the one scrolling at 11pm before a trip — that they're in the right place.

## Hook variants

1. **belonging:** "My Brand Store gets browsed every day. Nobody who lands there ever feels like it's for them."
2. **recognition:** "People stop. They scroll. They leave. I never knew why until I saw the score."
3. **fear_of_loss:** "Every visitor who leaves without buying? Gone. That's a sale I'll never get back."

## Production notes (Higgsfield)

- **Mode:** Per-scene precision — the real UI states (analytics dashboard, live scorecard animation, page-vs-competitor comparison) need exact control that a single storyboard-image pass would flatten.
- **Reference kit:** Founder character sheet for Owen (consistent face, garage setting — on camera in scene 1 only; scenes 2–4 stay on his screen, not his face); screen-UI mock of the coach's chat and Trust Gap scorecard component; a Brand Store About-page mock (real product photography of tents/packs, generic-copy headline) for scenes 2 and 4; a composite "competitor page" reference for the scene-4 comparison — not a real, identified brand.
- **Negative prompt / no-text-in-frame:** No text, captions, or logos baked into generated frames — the UI mocks and page mockups render clean; every caption in this storyboard is a post-production overlay. Do not hallucinate extra scorecard numbers, badges, or brand names onto the mocks.
- **Diegetic audio:** Quiet garage ambience, soft trackpad and mouse-click sounds timed to the screen-record beats, no music bed until the end-card sting.

## CTA & measurement

**End card copy:** "Find your weakest pillar — free." **Destination:** ideabrandcoach.com/diagnostic. **Metric that proves the ad worked:** click-through rate from the ad to /diagnostic — a brand-trust ad has to earn the click before it can claim it fixed anything. **Metric that proves the underlying fix worked:** sessions-to-orders on the Brand Store once the About page copy changes — the ad's job is the click, the diagnosis's job is the sessions-to-orders lift.
