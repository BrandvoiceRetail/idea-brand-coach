## Strategy

The felt pain, in her words: "4.7 stars. People who buy it come back. And in the search grid, almost nobody clicks through to find any of that out." The reviews are doing their job once someone lands on the page — the problem is nobody's landing, because her main image looks identical to five competitor bottles in the grid, so there's no reason to stop scrolling and see the rating at all.

The specific funnel position is `amazon_main_image`. `generate_main_image_title_plan` gives her one positioning statement — brand, "vitamin C," and the real difference (fermented, not synthetic) — built into image and title together, instead of a rating that only pays off after the click she isn't getting.

Hook trigger: **identity** — she wasn't speaking to "skincare people" in general, she was speaking to people done with irritation, and the image never said so.

## Hook variants

1. **identity:** "My customers aren't 'skincare people.' They're people done with irritation. My old image talked to the wrong crowd."
2. **recognition:** "4.7 stars. Healthy repeat rate. And a search-grid CTR under 0.3%. Sound familiar?"
3. **fear_of_loss:** "Every day my serum sits at 0.3% CTR, I'm losing shoppers to a bottle that looks exactly like mine."

## Production notes (Higgsfield)

- **Mode:** Marketing-studio preset (UGC review). The talking-head-to-camera monologue with a phone-screen cutaway is exactly the preset's target shape — persona on camera, one product in hand, one screen-share cutaway.
- **Reference kit:** Persona character sheet for the founder-actor (same face, wardrobe, bathroom-counter setting throughout); product reference sheet for the serum bottle from the real product photo; a clean mock-UI reference of the search-grid cutaway and the coach chat/plan card for scenes 2-4.
- **Negative prompt / no-text-in-frame:** Do not render legible search-grid labels or plan UI text inside the frame — supply those as flat reference images and add every TEXT ON SCREEN line as a post overlay. On-screen disclosure required: "Actor portrayal. Composite scenario for illustration." — this is a fictional founder persona, never labeled or implied as a verified buyer or real customer testimonial.
- **Diegetic audio:** Close, intimate room tone (bathroom acoustics) under scene 1; a phone-unlock tap under scene 2; soft trackpad clicks under scenes 3-4; VO is a single continuous monologue take, not narration over music.

## CTA & measurement

End card copy: "Free Trust Gap diagnostic. See what your listing's really saying." Destination: `ideabrandcoach.com/diagnostic`. The one metric that proves this ad worked: **the ad's own CTR to `/diagnostic`** (link clicks ÷ impressions) — the ad argues that good reviews can't save a CTR problem, so it has to prove itself on CTR, not on watch time.
