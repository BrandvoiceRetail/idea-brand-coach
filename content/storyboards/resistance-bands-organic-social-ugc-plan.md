## Strategy

Her own words: *"Six posts. Same band, same shot. Zero comments under any of them."* She kept assuming the fix was a better photo. It wasn't the photo — it was that every post on her `organic_social_profile` was written like an ad buy, not like a person talking.

The funnel position is `organic_social_profile`: the feed where a follower expects a voice, not a catalog. `generate_ugc_ad_plan` scripts that same post as a customer would actually say it — persona cast from her real avatar, one body, a trigger-angled hook — so what goes up reads like a review someone chose to post, not copy she was paid to write.

Hook trigger: **identity** — the post that gets comments is the one that sounds like it was written by someone who looks like the person reading it, not by the brand.

## Hook variants

1. **Identity:** "Six posts. Same product shot. Zero comments — because none of them sounded like a person."
2. **Recognition:** "I kept blaming the photo. It was never the photo."
3. **Permission:** "You don't need a better camera. You need a post that isn't written like an ad."

- **Mode:** Marketing-studio UGC preset (`ugc_review` format) — a persona-to-camera post with the product in hand is exactly this preset's target shape; scene 3's screen-capture beat switches to per-scene precision.
- **Reference kit:** Persona character sheet for the founder (consistent face/setting across scenes 1-2 and 4); real product photo of the resistance bands; screen-UI mock of the coach's chat and the `generate_ugc_ad_plan` output card for scene 3.

On-screen disclosure required: "Actor portrayal. Composite scenario for illustration." — persona never implied as a verified buyer.
