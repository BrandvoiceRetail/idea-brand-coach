## Strategy

Her own words: "My nicer video sits ignored. The rough one gets almost every click." The irony stings because she spent the budget on the wrong one.

The funnel position is `seo_content`: the embedded video inside her buying-guide post is the click decision point. The rough, specific video opens on the exact problem ("phone falling off the dash on a pothole"); the polished one opens on the brand logo. `generate_video_storyboard` rebuilds the polished video's opening scene around that same specific problem, instead of the brand intro.

Hook trigger: recognition. Viewers click the video that opens on their exact annoyance, not the nicest one.

## Hook variants

1. **Recognition** — "My polished video. My rough video. Guess which one gets all the clicks."
2. **Identity** — "I made the professional video. My customers picked the rough one instead."
3. **Momentum** — "One scene swap, and the polished video finally earned its clicks."

## Production notes (Higgsfield)

- **Mode:** storyboard-image mode (one multi-panel storyboard covering the before/after opening-scene contrast, compiled to a single video job — the ad is a comparison, not a multi-location shoot).
- **Reference kit:** persona character sheet (same founder desk setup, consistent across all five shots); screen-UI mock of the coach's `generate_video_storyboard` scene-rebuild output for scene 4; product reference sheet from real car-mount photography for the dashboard shots.
- **Negative prompt / no-text-in-frame:** no burned-in text in generated frames — the thumbnail click counts and coach UI are screen-capture composites, not AI text; TEXT ON SCREEN overlays added in post only.
- **Diegetic audio:** consistent desk room tone across scenes 1, 3-5; brief ambient road/pothole SFX under the rough-video clip in scene 2 to sell the contrast; no music bed.
