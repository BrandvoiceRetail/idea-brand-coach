## Strategy

Her own words: "Every ad I run is dead in two weeks. Not because the product's wrong — because it's one angle, said once."

The funnel position is `founder_social` — the content she's posting as the face of the brand is a single script repeated with different footage, so the algorithm and the audience both burn out on it fast. There's no variation for it to test against itself. `generate_ugc_ad_plan` gives her three trigger-angled hooks over one body — same footage, three different reasons to stop scrolling — so the creative doesn't have one shelf life, it has three.

Hook trigger: momentum. She's not shooting more content. She's getting more out of the content she already has.

## Hook variants

1. **Momentum** — "Two weeks in, every ad I run just stops moving."
2. **Recognition** — "Every ad I've ever run dies in two weeks. Sound familiar?"
3. **Fear_of_loss** — "I was about to reshoot a whole new ad for a problem that wasn't the footage."

## Production notes (Higgsfield)

- **Mode:** Marketing-studio UGC preset (talking-head problem-solution format; keeps face, voice, and desk setting consistent across the single continuous take).
- **Reference kit:** Persona character sheet (same founder, same desk, same lighting throughout); product reference sheet for the electrolyte mix packaging from the real product photo; screen-UI mock of the coach chat and rendered three-hook plan card for scenes 3-4.
- **Negative prompt / no-text-in-frame:** No legible graph labels, UI copy, or hook text rendered inside generated frames — the dashboard, ad library, and plan card are supplied as flat reference mocks; every TEXT ON SCREEN line is a post overlay.
- **Diegetic audio:** Quiet home-office ambience under scenes 1-2; a soft trackpad-click SFX under scene 3 as the plan renders; no music bed.

## CTA & measurement

End card copy: "Stop reshooting for fatigue. Free diagnostic, 6 questions, no account." Destination: `ideabrandcoach.com/diagnostic`. The one metric that proves this ad worked: **hold rate past scene 4 (the three-hook plan reveal)** — proof that watching one body split into three distinct angles, not just hearing the pitch, is what keeps a founder-marketer watching to the CTA.
