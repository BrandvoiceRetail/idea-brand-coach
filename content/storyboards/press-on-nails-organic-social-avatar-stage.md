## Strategy

Her own words: "Every slot on my content calendar is the same shot — nails, flat lay, ring light. I don't know what else to say about them."

The funnel position is `organic_social_profile` — the founder's own posting cadence is entirely product photography because she doesn't actually know the customer's language yet: the real objection, the real job the nails are hired for, the real moment someone reaches for them. Without that, there's nothing to write except "here's the product" on repeat. `build_avatar_stage`'s four-stage build — vocabulary, job map, triggers, objections — hands back the customer's actual words, which is what turns a flat lay into a review-style post people stop scrolling for.

Hook trigger: recognition. She's not missing creativity. She's missing the customer's own language to build with.

## Hook variants

1. **Recognition** — "My whole content calendar is the same flat lay. I ran out of things to say."
2. **Permission** — "Turns out I didn't need more creativity. I needed my customer's actual words."
3. **Identity** — "I kept posting product shots because I didn't know who I was actually posting to."

## Production notes (Higgsfield)

- **Mode:** Marketing-studio UGC preset (talking-head review format holds face, voice, and setting consistent across the continuous eight-beat sequence).
- **Reference kit:** Persona character sheet (same founder, same desk/lighting throughout); product reference sheet for the press-on nail set from the real product photo; screen-UI mock of the coach chat and the four-stage avatar build output for scenes 4-5.
- **Negative prompt / no-text-in-frame:** No legible caption text, comment text, or stage-output copy rendered inside generated frames — supply the calendar grid, comment, and stage progression as flat reference mocks; every TEXT ON SCREEN line is a post overlay.
- **Diegetic audio:** Quiet desk ambience (tablet taps, a soft sigh at scene 3) under scenes 1-3; a soft chime as each stage completes in scene 5; no music bed until the closing card.

## CTA & measurement

End card copy: "If your calendar's just product shots, you're probably missing the same thing. Free diagnostic, 6 questions, no account." Destination: `ideabrandcoach.com/diagnostic`. The one metric that proves this ad worked: **diagnostic-start rate among viewers who watch past the stage-4 avatar reveal (scene 5)** — proof that seeing the customer's real language surfaced is what drives someone to check their own avatar gap.
