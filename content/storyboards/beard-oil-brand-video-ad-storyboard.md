## Strategy

Her own words: "Forty thousand views, comments everywhere, and the link click count says three." That's the felt pain — an ad performing like content, not like a directed sale.

The funnel position is `paid_social_creative`: the UGC video itself is the touchpoint. It was built to entertain, not to move a viewer through a click decision, so it had watch time and comments but no hook, no claim, no reason to act now. `generate_video_storyboard` rebuilds that same footage's structure — hook, claim-gated talking point, CTA beat — so the entertainment she already earned turns into clicks.

Hook trigger: momentum. She doesn't need a reshoot. She needs the plan the footage never had.

## Hook variants

1. **Momentum** — "Forty thousand views. Three clicks. That's not a views problem — that's a missing plan."
2. **Recognition** — "Forty thousand views, and I still couldn't tell you what I wanted people to do next."
3. **Fear of loss** — "Every comment on that video is a click I never got."

## Production notes (Higgsfield)

- **Mode:** marketing-studio UGC preset (talking-head review format holds face and voice consistent across the single continuous monologue).
- **Reference kit:** persona character sheet (same actor, same shelf backdrop across all five shots); screen-UI mock of the coach's `generate_video_storyboard` output grid for scenes 3-4.
- **Negative prompt / no-text-in-frame:** no on-screen text rendered inside generated video frames — every TEXT ON SCREEN line is a post overlay, never baked into the shot.
- **Diegetic audio:** one continuous room-tone take under the monologue, no music bed; a soft UI-tap SFX when the storyboard grid populates in scene 3.
