## Strategy

"Six weeks, same ad, and the click rate dropped a little more every week" is the
felt pain — a slow leak, not a sudden crash. The diagnosis sits at
**paid_social_creative**: the ad was selling "premium detailing" while her actual
buyers were searching "fix my car before I sell it," a mismatch between the pitch
and the real purchase reason. `generate_main_image_title_plan` fixes the
connection problem by testing her positioning statement against real search
language and catching exactly where it diverges. The hook plays **recognition** —
the fix wasn't a new video, it was finally sounding like the customer's actual
problem instead of the product's features.

## Hook variants

- **recognition** — "My ad kept talking about 'premium detailing.' My customers were googling 'fix my car before I sell it.'"
- **fear_of_loss** — "Six weeks running the same ad, and every week the click rate dropped a little more."
- **permission** — "I didn't need a new video. I needed the ad to finally sound like my customer, not my product page."

## Production notes (Higgsfield)

- **Mode:** marketing-studio preset — ugc_review talking-head format.
- **Reference kit:** persona character sheet for the founder and garage setting (consistent across all 5 scenes); product reference sheet built from the real detailing-kit photo for the bench cutaways; screen-UI mock of the coach's tool output comparing the positioning statement to search-language.
- **Negative-prompt / no-text-in-frame:** no baked-in text inside generated frames — every TEXT ON SCREEN line added as a post overlay; no real Google/Facebook trademarked UI chrome; no fabricated "verified six weeks of real data" claim rendered on screen — framed as this composite founder's scenario.
- **Diegetic audio:** garage ambience (light tool clatter, no music), single handheld take, no underscore until the CTA card.

## CTA & measurement

End card: "Say what your customer's actually searching for. Free diagnostic, 6
questions." Destination: **ideabrandcoach.com/diagnostic**. The one metric that
proves this ad worked: **week-over-week CTR trend on the ad** after the
positioning-statement swap — the same number the ad opens on, now moving the
other direction.
