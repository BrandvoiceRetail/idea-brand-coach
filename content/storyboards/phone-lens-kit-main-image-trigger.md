## Strategy

The felt pain, in the founder's own words: "The keyword's on fire. The impressions are there. Nobody's clicking." A composite founder we'll call Raj sells a clip-on phone lens kit. His main image is a clean flat-lay — three lenses, a case, laid out like a parts diagram. Technically correct. Completely un-clickable.

The connection problem sits right at the `amazon_main_image` position: the scroll-and-decide moment in the search grid. Shoppers searching a broad photography keyword aren't asking "what's in the box" — they're asking "will this actually make my photos look good." Raj's image answers a question nobody's asking.

The coach runs `identify_decision_trigger` and it names **recognition** as the lever this purchase turns on: buyers want to see themselves as a better photographer, not read a parts list. The hook plays that trigger straight back at the viewer — showing them the shot they wish they'd taken, not the hardware that took it.

## Hook variants

1. **recognition:** "Three lenses lined up in a row isn't a main image. It's a packing list."
2. **identity:** "Your buyer isn't shopping for a lens kit. They're shopping to become the friend with the good camera roll."
3. **fear_of_loss:** "Every scroll past you, someone's already picturing that shot. Just not with your lens."

## Production notes (Higgsfield)

- **Mode:** storyboard-image (single multi-panel storyboard image → one video job) — this is a tight 15s problem/solution beat, not a per-scene shoot.
- **Reference kit:** founder character sheet (Raj, consistent face/wardrobe across scenes 1 and 3, his only on-camera appearances); a screen-UI mock of the coach's `identify_decision_trigger` result card as a locked reference asset so the on-screen tool output stays visually consistent; a product reference sheet built from the real lens-kit photos for the "new concept" comparison in scene 4.
- **Negative prompt / no-text-in-frame:** exclude baked-in text, watermarks, or UI chrome from every generated frame — all copy is added as a separate TEXT ON SCREEN overlay layer, never rendered into the image/video itself.
- **Diegetic audio:** soft laptop-typing and a single UI "ping" when the trigger result card renders in scene 3; no music bed under the VO until scene 5's CTA card, where a short brand sting plays under the logo.
