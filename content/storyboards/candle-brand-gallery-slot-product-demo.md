## Strategy

The felt pain: *"My gallery tells you the scent notes and the jar size. It never tells you what your living room looks like with it lit."* A candle is bought for the atmosphere it creates, not the wax it's made of — and the gallery never once shows the atmosphere.

The funnel position is `amazon_listing_copy`: the gallery slots specifically, where a shopper buying a candle needs to picture their own room, not read another spec sheet. `generate_listing_image_brief` produces the exact gallery brief naming the lifestyle "room context" slot most candle listings skip entirely.

The hook runs on **momentum** — this is one slot to fill this week, not a redesign project.

## Hook variants

1. **Momentum:** "This isn't a redesign. It's one slot, and you can fill it this week."
2. **Recognition:** "Scent notes. Jar dimensions. Not one shot of what your room actually looks like lit."
3. **Permission:** "You're not behind. You just didn't know which slot to fix."

## Production notes (Higgsfield)

- **Mode:** Per-scene precision — this is a screen-capture-led demo (real UI states, a specific before/after slot comparison) that needs exact control scene by scene, not one multi-panel storyboard image.
- **Reference kit:** Founder character sheet for the brief cutaway; screen-UI mock of the coach's chat and the listing-image brief slot list; real product photo of the candle jar for the "jar on white" reference; a warm lifestyle reference (lit candle, windowsill, dusk light) for the new-slot mock.
- **Negative prompt / no-text-in-frame:** No text, labels, or captions baked into the generated frames — UI mocks and product shots must render clean; every caption in this storyboard is a post-production overlay per the TEXT ON SCREEN field. Do not hallucinate extra badges, review counts, or logos onto the UI mock.
- **Diegetic audio:** Soft trackpad taps and mouse-click sounds timed to cursor movement, quiet home-office room tone, no music bed until the end-card sting.
