## Strategy

Her felt pain, in her own words: *"My reviews are good. I checked — 4.6 stars, people say it works. So why isn't that carrying the listing?"* She read her reviews the way most founders do: skim the stars, feel reassured, move on.

The funnel position is `displayed_reviews`: the exact section a hesitant new customer scrolls to answer one question — will this actually work for me, and how long will it take. A rating that says "yes" without saying "here's how long you'll have to wait" leaves that shopper's real objection unanswered.

The hook runs on **recognition**: she needs to see, specifically, that "generally positive" was hiding a second story her reviews were already telling — one the listing never pre-answered.

## Hook variants

1. **Recognition:** "I thought my reviews were just glowing. I never noticed they were also warning me."
2. **Fear_of_loss:** "Seven customers almost gave up at week six. My listing still doesn't warn the eighth one to expect it."
3. **Permission:** "You don't need new reviews. You need to actually read the ones you have."

## Production notes (Higgsfield)

- **Mode:** Marketing-studio UGC review preset for the on-camera founder beats; per-scene precision for the review-scroll and `ingest_evidence` screen recording, since those beats need an exact UI state, not a generated composite.
- **Reference kit:** Persona character sheet for the founder (consistent face/wardrobe across all on-camera beats); screen-UI mock of the coach's chat, the review-scroll UI, and the pattern-surfacing card; real product photo of the scalp serum bottle for any product insert.
- **Negative prompt / no-text rule:** No baked-in text, star icons, or caption chrome in generated frames — the "Pattern found" and rating figures are post-production overlays exactly as specified in TEXT ON SCREEN; do not fabricate a specific star count or review volume beyond "7 mentions."
- **Diegetic audio:** Warm, unpolished room tone for the founder's on-camera beats, soft trackpad click timed to the review-scroll cut, subtle interface tap on the screen-recording cut, no music bed until the end card.
