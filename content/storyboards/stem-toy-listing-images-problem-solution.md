## Strategy

The felt pain: *"Six slides of piece counts and dimensions, and not one answer to the only question a parent actually has — will my kid play with this."* Her gallery is accurate and completely unpersuasive, because it answers a spec question nobody's asking at that point in the scroll.

The funnel position is `amazon_listing_copy`: the gallery order specifically, where a parent scrolling for proof of engagement gets specs instead. `generate_listing_image_brief` restructures that gallery order, moving the one lifestyle image that answers "will my kid actually play with this" several slots earlier, instead of burying it after the infographics.

The hook runs on **fear_of_loss** — the fear of buying another toy that ends up ignored in a bin.

## Hook variants

1. **Fear_of_loss:** "I've bought toys that got played with once and buried in a bin. I needed to know this wouldn't be another one."
2. **Recognition:** "Six slides of piece counts. Not one slide of a kid actually playing."
3. **Momentum:** "The fix wasn't a new photoshoot. It was moving one photo I already had, four slots earlier."

## Production notes (Higgsfield)

- **Mode:** Per-scene precision — the before/after gallery-order comparison and the screen-share brief reveal each need exact staging, not one multi-panel storyboard image.
- **Reference kit:** Parent-shopper character sheet for scenes 1-2; founder character sheet for scenes 3 and 6; product reference sheet from the real STEM-block set's photos, including the existing lifestyle shot; screen-UI mock of the coach's image-brief output and gallery-order diagram for scenes 3-5.
- **Negative prompt / no-text-in-frame:** No baked-in captions, UI chrome, or fabricated review counts inside generated frames — every TEXT ON SCREEN line is a post overlay; gallery mockups shown as illustrative, never implying real Amazon UI trademarks.
- **Diegetic audio:** Quiet living-room ambience for scenes 1-2, home-office room tone with trackpad clicks for scenes 3-5, a light ambient pad rising only under the end card.

## CTA & measurement

**End card copy:** "See what your gallery order is actually saying. Free diagnostic." **Destination:** ideabrandcoach.com/diagnostic. **Metric that proves the ad worked:** click-through rate from the ad to /diagnostic proves the argument landed.
