## Strategy

Her felt pain, in her own words: *"My rating is fine. So why does every returns report have the same three words in it — 'lighter in person'?"* She's been treating each complaint as a one-off, not a pattern, because no single review is bad enough to flag on its own.

The funnel position is `displayed_reviews`: the section shoppers scroll specifically to de-risk a shade purchase they can't verify from a photo. When the same fear repeats across dozens of reviews and the listing never answers it back, every new shopper walks into the identical gap the last one did.

The hook runs on **fear_of_loss**: the exact worry — *this will look nothing like the swatch* — that's already sitting in her own review section, unanswered, costing her a sale every time it surfaces.

## Hook variants

1. **Fear_of_loss:** "'The photos looked lighter than this in person' — nine different customers wrote that, almost word for word."
2. **Recognition:** "My rating is fine. So why did the same complaint show up nine times?"
3. **Permission:** "You don't need better lighting on your photos. You need to actually read your reviews."

## Production notes (Higgsfield)

- **Mode:** Marketing-studio UGC preset — a phone-in-hand, natural-light try-on selfie video is squarely a UGC try-on format; use the preset for the on-camera beats and per-scene precision for the screen-recording insert.
- **Reference kit:** Persona character sheet (same face, same lighting, same wardrobe across the try-on cutaways); screen-UI mock of the coach's chat and the `ingest_evidence` pattern-card output; real product swatch/packaging photo for continuity between the on-camera shot and any product insert.
- **Negative prompt / no-text rule:** No text, subtitles, or UI chrome baked into generated frames — captions and the pattern-card numbers are post-production overlays per the TEXT ON SCREEN field only; do not invent review counts or star ratings beyond what's specified.
- **Diegetic audio:** Handheld room tone, natural voice with a slight phone-mic presence (no studio polish), soft UI tap sound on the screen-recording cut, no music bed until the end card.

## CTA & measurement

**End card copy:** "Find what your reviews are already telling you — free." **Destination:** ideabrandcoach.com/diagnostic. **Metric that proves the ad worked:** click-through rate from the ad to /diagnostic — the ad's job is to make the fear_of_loss hook land specifically enough that a founder in the same spot taps through to check her own reviews. Downstream, once she rewrites the review-facing copy, sessions-to-orders on that listing is the number that confirms the trust gap actually closed, not just the click.
