## Strategy

His own words: "I don't want to redo my whole funnel. I just know something's off with one module, and I need to know what before I relaunch." He's not looking for a full audit — he's looking for a fast, specific answer on the one asset he's about to bet a relaunch on.

The specific funnel position is `amazon_brand_story`. `audit_asset` scores that single A+ content module against his customer avatar and returns one precise miss: no beat in the module addresses knife-maintenance anxiety — the thing his avatar actually worries about before buying a set they'll need to sharpen and store correctly.

Hook trigger: **permission** — you don't need a full audit to get an answer; one asset, checked properly, is enough to move.

## Hook variants

1. **permission:** "I didn't need to redo my funnel. Just this one module."
2. **recognition:** "Looks good. Reads fine. Still not converting. Sound familiar?"
3. **momentum:** "I was about to relaunch on a module I'd never checked."

## Production notes (Higgsfield)

- **Mode:** storyboard-image mode (ONE multi-panel storyboard image compiled to ONE video job) — fits the product-demo format's tight, sequential "one module, one verdict" narrative without needing per-scene precision.
- **Reference kit:** Persona character sheet for the founder-actor (same face, kitchen setting, wardrobe throughout); product reference sheet for the knife set from real product photography; screen-UI mock of the A+ content module and the `audit_asset` verdict card for scenes 2-4.
- **Negative prompt / no-text-in-frame:** Do not render legible A+ content copy or audit UI text inside generated frames — supply as flat reference images, add every TEXT ON SCREEN line as a post overlay. On-screen disclosure required: "Actor portrayal. Composite scenario for illustration." — fictional founder persona, never implied as a verified buyer.
