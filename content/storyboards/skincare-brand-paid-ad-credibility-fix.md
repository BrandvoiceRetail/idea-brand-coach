## Strategy

Her felt pain: *"My targeting is right. My interest overlap is strong. Nobody's clicking anyway."* She's done the media-buying homework — the audience is correct — and the ad still gets scrolled past, which is a harder problem to diagnose than "wrong audience."

The funnel position is `paid_social_creative`: the ad itself has to earn a skeptical retinol buyer's trust before any offer lands, because retinol buyers are primed to distrust "glass skin" claims. A generic, polished creative reads as one more unproven promise in the feed.

The hook runs on **permission** — giving the skeptic scrolling past the ad the exact objection-answer they're already waiting for, instead of another glossy claim.

## Hook variants

1. **Permission:** "My targeting is right. My interest overlap is strong. Nobody's clicking anyway."
2. **Fear_of_loss:** "Every skeptical scroll-past is ad spend I'm never getting back."
3. **Recognition:** "I kept blaming the algorithm. It was the ad talking past the exact doubt in their head."

## Production notes (Higgsfield)

- **Mode:** Per-scene precision — the ad mixes screen-capture UI moments (3, 4, 5) with a creative-comparison split screen (6); each beat needs individual control rather than one storyboard panel.
- **Reference kit:** Founder character sheet for continuity across scenes 1 and 2; screen-UI mock of the coach's chat and Trust Gap scorecard; the real ad creative reference (retinol serum packshot and glass-skin ad mockup); product sheet built from the real serum bottle photo.
- **Negative prompt / no-text rule:** No baked-in captions or UI text in generated frames beyond what's specified — all overlay copy is added in post per the TEXT ON SCREEN field. Do not let the model invent extra logos, review-platform chrome, or fake certification badges.
- **Diegetic audio:** Quiet desk/office ambience, mouse-click and keyboard sounds under the UI beats, no underscore until the end-card sting.
