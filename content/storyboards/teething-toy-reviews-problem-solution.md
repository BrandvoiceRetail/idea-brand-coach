## Strategy

Her felt pain, in her own words: *"Nine different parents asked the same safety question in my reviews. Nine. And my listing still doesn't answer it."* Each review looked like an isolated question when she skimmed it; nobody had ever added them up.

The funnel position is `displayed_reviews`: for a product that goes in a baby's mouth, this is where anxious parents go hunting specifically for the reassurance the bullet points didn't give them — and where a repeated, unanswered question compounds instead of fading.

The hook runs on **fear_of_loss**: a parent scanning reviews for a safety answer who doesn't find one doesn't ask a follow-up question — she closes the tab and buys the competitor's toy instead.

## Hook variants

1. **Fear_of_loss:** "Nine parents asked if the material was actually safe. My listing never answered a single one."
2. **Recognition:** "I kept seeing the same question in my reviews. I just never added them up."
3. **Permission:** "You don't need a new safety certification. You need to say the one you already have, out loud."

## Production notes (Higgsfield)

- **Mode:** Per-scene precision — this is a screen-capture-led problem/solution demo needing exact control over the review-scroll and coach-UI states; not suited to a single multi-panel storyboard-image generation.
- **Reference kit:** Founder character sheet for continuity across the laptop-scrolling cutaways; screen-UI mock of the coach's chat and the `ingest_evidence` pattern card; real product photo of the silicone teething toy for the listing-bullet comparison beat.
- **Negative prompt / no-text rule:** No text, review-star icons, or caption chrome baked into generated frames — the "9 mentions" figure and all captions are post-production overlays per the TEXT ON SCREEN field; do not invent a specific certification name or safety claim not already established.
- **Diegetic audio:** Quiet home-office room tone, soft trackpad clicks timed to the scrolling, subtle UI tap on the coach screen-recording cut, no music bed until the end-card sting.

## CTA & measurement

**End card copy:** "Find the question your reviews keep asking — free." **Destination:** ideabrandcoach.com/diagnostic. **Metric that proves the ad worked:** click-through rate from the ad to /diagnostic — the fear_of_loss hook is aimed at founders who recognize the "isolated question" pattern in their own reviews and click through to check. Downstream, sessions-to-orders is the number that confirms adding the safety line actually closed the gap, not just the click.
