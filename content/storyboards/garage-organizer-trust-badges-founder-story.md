## Strategy

The felt pain, in the founder's own words: "I blamed the price for a month. It wasn't the price." A composite founder we'll call Dale sells a garage tool-organizer system. Shoppers add to cart at a decent rate, then a chunk of them just vanish before checkout — no coupon code entered, no support ticket, just gone.

The connection problem sits at `trust_badges_social_proof`: the buy-box moment where a shopper decides whether this brand deserves their card number. The coach runs `run_trust_gap` and finds **Authentic** scoring lowest, tracing the abandon point exactly there — no warranty language anywhere near the button, while three competitors selling the same category all show one.

The hook plays **momentum**: naming the exact stalling sentence turns a month of despair into a same-day to-do list.

## Hook variants

1. **momentum:** "I spent a month guessing. It took one score to find the exact sentence that was missing."
2. **fear_of_loss:** "Every shopper who added to cart and vanished was a sale I'd already half-won and let go."
3. **recognition:** "I blamed the price for a month. It was never the price."

## Production notes (Higgsfield)

- **Mode:** Per-scene precision — the garage-to-laptop-to-scorecard-to-comparison progression across 7 scenes needs controllable, distinct transitions a single storyboard-image pass would flatten.
- **Reference kit:** Founder character sheet for Dale (garage setting with his own organizer panels visible, consistent across scenes 1, 2, 5, and 6); real product photography of the tool-organizer system for garage cutaways; screen-UI mock of the coach's chat and Trust Gap scorecard flagging `trust_badges_social_proof`; a buy-box comparison mock (his vs. three composite competitors) for scene 4.
- **Negative prompt / no-text-in-frame:** No baked-in text, real competitor names, or real Amazon trademarked UI chrome in generated frames — captions are post overlays only; competitor buy boxes are generic composites, not identified real brands. No fabricated "real revenue recovered" figure rendered on screen — the dashboard uptick in scene 6 stays illustrative and unlabeled with a specific number.
- **Diegetic audio:** Light garage ambience (tool clatter, no music) under scenes 1–2 and 5–6; soft mouse-click sounds under the screen-record beats (3–4); brand sting on the end-card scene.
