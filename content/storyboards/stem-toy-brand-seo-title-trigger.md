## Strategy

The felt pain, in the founder's own words: "My rank hasn't moved. My click share keeps shrinking anyway." A composite founder we'll call Angela runs a blog post targeting "best building toys for 6 year olds." Position is stable — the ranking algorithm still likes her — but more competitors have entered the same query, and her share of the clicks on that result keeps sliding.

The connection problem sits at `seo_content`: the search-results snippet is the whole pitch, and hers still talks specs — piece count, material safety, age range. The coach runs `identify_decision_trigger` on the query itself and finds parents searching this exact phrase are driven by **fear_of_loss** around screen time, not curiosity about toy features. Three newer competitors' snippets already speak to that anxiety directly; hers doesn't.

The hook plays fear_of_loss back at the searcher in the meta title itself, screen-recorded as a live before/after.

## Hook variants

1. **fear_of_loss:** "Before another rainy day turns into another tablet day."
2. **recognition:** "You didn't search 'building toys.' You searched for a reason to put the tablet down."
3. **permission:** "It's okay the toy aisle didn't fix this. That's exactly what the right search result is for."

## Production notes (Higgsfield)

- **Mode:** storyboard-image — this is a screen-recording-style product demo with no live-action performer; a single multi-panel storyboard image driving one video job cleanly handles the search-results and dashboard mockups in sequence.
- **Reference kit:** a screen-UI reference sheet locking the Search Console dashboard styling and the Google search-results-page styling consistent across scenes 1, 2, and 4; a screen-UI mock of the coach's trigger-result card for scene 3.
- **Negative prompt / no-text-in-frame:** the search snippet copy and dashboard numbers in scenes 2 and 4 are the actual subject being shown, not overlay text — render them as in-frame UI content, but keep all separate TEXT ON SCREEN captions (scene labels, the CTA line) on a distinct overlay layer so the two never compete.
- **Diegetic audio:** soft cursor-click and scroll sounds under the screen-recording beats; a single UI ping when the trigger result card renders in scene 3; brand sting under the scene 5 CTA card.

## CTA & measurement

- **End card copy:** "Find the trigger behind your search traffic." with the ideabrandcoach.com wordmark.
- **Destination:** ideabrandcoach.com/diagnostic
- **The one metric that proves the ad worked:** click-through rate on the ad itself — an ad about fixing organic CTR only earns its claim if its own CTR beats a specs-led control creative.
