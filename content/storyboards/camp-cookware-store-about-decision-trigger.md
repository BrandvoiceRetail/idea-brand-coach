## Strategy

His felt pain, in Ray's words: "I built this cookware in my garage. I've told that story on every page of my brand store — and not one customer has ever mentioned it when they tell me why they bought." The About page does exactly what About pages are supposed to do — garage photo, first prototype, the five-year timeline — and none of it is the reason anyone actually clicks "add to cart."

The funnel position is `brand_store_about`: the moment a shopper who already likes the product decides whether the brand behind it is *for them*. `identify_decision_trigger` names the one psychological lever this purchase actually turns on, so Ray can see whether his founding story is doing that job or just filling space.

The hook runs on **identity** — the story he keeps telling is about him; the trigger that moves buyers is about who *they* get to be at the campsite.

## Hook variants

1. **Identity:** "I built this cookware in my garage. Turns out nobody buys it for the garage."
2. **Recognition:** "I've told my founding story so many times, I forgot to ask if it's even why people buy."
3. **Fear_of_loss:** "Every visitor who reads my About page and still doesn't know why to buy is a sale I lost without knowing it."

## Production notes (Higgsfield)

- **Mode:** Storyboard-image default — one multi-panel storyboard image covering the garage talking-head, the About-page scroll, and the tool-result reveal, rendered as a single video job. The founder-story format doesn't need shot-to-shot precision.
- **Reference kit:** Persona character sheet for Ray (same face, flannel/work-jacket wardrobe, garage lighting across all panels); product reference sheet for the cast-cookware line from real product photos; a flat screen-UI mock of the About page and of the coach chat with the `identify_decision_trigger` result card for scenes 2-4.
- **Negative prompt / no-text-in-frame:** Do not render legible About-page body copy or UI labels inside generated frames — supply the About-page scroll and the trigger-result card as flat reference mocks and add every TEXT ON SCREEN line as a post overlay. On-screen disclosure: "Actor portrayal. Composite scenario for illustration." — Ray is a fictional composite founder, never a verified customer.
- **Diegetic audio:** Quiet garage room tone (a fan hum, a metal clink as he sets down a pan), soft trackpad clicks under scene 3, no music bed until the end card.

## CTA & measurement

End card copy: "Free Trust Gap diagnostic. Find the trigger your story's missing." Destination: `ideabrandcoach.com/diagnostic`. **Metrics that prove it worked:** hook rate and diagnostic-start rate among ad-attributed clicks prove the identity angle landed; the metric that proves the connection problem itself is fixed is branded-search lift — once the About page names the real reason to buy, more shoppers who already like the product start searching for the brand by name instead of just the product category.
