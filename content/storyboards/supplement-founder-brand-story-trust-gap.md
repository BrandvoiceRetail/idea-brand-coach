## Strategy

His felt pain, in his own words: *"I built my brand story page to sound clinical. Credible. Like a lab wrote it. I was proud of that."* He treated "sounds scientific" as the same thing as "sounds trustworthy," and spent real time polishing paragraphs nobody actually asked him to write that way.

The funnel position is `amazon_brand_story`: the A+ content module where a shopper who already clicked through is deciding whether to believe the brand, not just the ingredient panel. `run_trust_gap` scores that module and names **Empathetic** as the weakest pillar — nowhere on the page does anyone sound like they've actually tasted the product.

The hook runs on **identity** — the question isn't whether the page is accurate, it's whether it sounds like the founder or like a compliance form, and those are two very different people to buy from.

## Hook variants

1. **Identity:** "Does my brand story page sound like me? Or like a compliance form?"
2. **Recognition:** "Five modules. Not one sentence that sounds like a person actually drank it."
3. **Permission:** "Skip the redesign. Go find the one paragraph that actually sounds like you."

## Production notes (Higgsfield)

- **Mode:** Per-scene precision — the ad alternates a talking-head founder beat (1, 2, 5) with exact screen-UI states (3, 4); each needs individual scene control rather than one storyboard panel.
- **Reference kit:** Founder character sheet to keep face, wardrobe, and kitchen setting consistent across scenes 1, 2, and 5; screen-UI mock of the coach's chat and Trust Gap scorecard; real product photo of the greens supplement tub; a reference of the actual A+ module layout for the scroll beat.
- **Negative prompt / no-text rule:** No captions, labels, or UI copy baked into generated frames — all text is added as post overlay per the TEXT ON SCREEN field. Do not add fabricated lab certifications, badges, or logos to the A+ module reference.
- **Diegetic audio:** Quiet kitchen ambience, a mug or tub set down softly under scene 1, laptop trackpad clicks under scenes 3-4, no music bed until the end-card sting.

## CTA & measurement

**End card copy:** "Find the pillar that's actually costing you trust. Free diagnostic." **Destination:** ideabrandcoach.com/diagnostic. **Metric that proves the ad worked:** click-through rate from the ad to /diagnostic proves the hook landed; sessions-to-orders on the listing is the metric that proves the fix worked — the ad's thesis is that a polished, clinical-sounding page can hide the real trust problem, so a lift here proves founders who felt "credible enough" actually went and checked, and a recovered conversion ratio proves the rewrite held up.
