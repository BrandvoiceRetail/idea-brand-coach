## Strategy

The felt pain, in Dana's words: "I redesigned the thumbnail twice. Twice. And CTR still sits below where the category average says it should be." She did the obvious fix — a cleaner, calmer-looking image — and the number didn't move, because the redesign never touched the real problem: her new image promises "calm" while her title still leads with a generic keyword. Two different pitches on one listing, splitting the click decision instead of making it.

The specific funnel position is `amazon_main_image` — the shopper reads image and title together in under a second, and right now Dana's are contradicting each other. `generate_main_image_title_plan` fixes this by locking image and title to ONE positioning statement, and hands her a CTR split-test plan so the next change is measured, not another guess.

Hook trigger: **momentum** — the fix is a same-day swap of what the image and title say, not another redesign cycle.

## Hook variants

1. **momentum:** "Two redesigns didn't move CTR. The real fix took one afternoon."
2. **recognition:** "You've done this — swapped the photo, watched CTR do nothing anyway."
3. **fear_of_loss:** "Every scroll past my listing, some other gummy brand just made a sale."

## Production notes (Higgsfield)

- **Mode:** Per-scene precision. The thumbnail-comparison beat (scene 2), the title-vs-image contradiction (scene 3), and the plan/split-test UI (scenes 4-6) each need a specific mock to stay legible and consistent across cuts.
- **Reference kit:** Persona character sheet for Dana (same face, home-office wardrobe, lighting throughout); product reference sheet for the gummy jar from the real product photo; two flat mock-ups of the old thumbnail versions; a clean mock-UI reference of the coach chat, the rendered plan card, and the split-test tracker.
- **Negative prompt / no-text-in-frame:** Do not let the model render legible title text, chart labels, or UI copy inside any frame — supply the old-thumbnail comparisons and the plan/split-test cards as flat reference images and add every TEXT ON SCREEN line as a post overlay. On-screen disclosure required: "Actor portrayal. Composite scenario for illustration." — Dana is a fictional composite founder, not a verified customer or real case study.
- **Diegetic audio:** Quiet home-office ambience under scenes 1-3 (a chair creak, a phone unlock chime); soft trackpad clicks under scenes 4-6 as Dana works the plan; no music swell during the plan-reveal beat — let the VO carry it.
