## Strategy

The felt pain, in the founder's own words: "The post is good. It's accurate, it's ranked, and people still aren't clicking it." A composite founder we'll call Priya runs a scalp-treatment blog. Her post is genuinely well-written, sits top-five for the target query, and answers the question correctly — but organic CTR sits at roughly half the category benchmark for that position.

The connection problem lives at `seo_content`: the content answers the question accurately without giving the searcher a reason to click *now*. The coach runs `identify_decision_trigger` and flags **momentum** as the missing lever — scalp-health searchers are usually mid-frustration and ready to act, and Priya's clinical, patient tone reads as "read later," not "fix it today."

The hook plays momentum directly: rewrite the snippet to meet someone already reaching for their card, not someone still researching.

## Hook variants

1. **momentum:** "You're not researching. You're mid-scratch, wanting this fixed tonight."
2. **fear_of_loss:** "Every day you wait on this is a day the flaking gets more obvious."
3. **permission:** "You don't need to read three more comparison posts. You need one answer you can act on tonight."

## Production notes (Higgsfield)

- **Mode:** per-scene — the 30s runtime and the before/after CMS-and-dashboard progression (scenes 5–7) need distinct, controllable transitions that a single storyboard-image render would flatten; per-scene precision keeps the "one edit, same content" beat legible.
- **Reference kit:** founder character sheet for Priya (consistent face and desk setup across scenes 1, 4, and 6, her on-camera appearances); a screen-UI reference sheet locking the analytics-dashboard and search-results-page styling across scenes 1, 3, and 7; a screen-UI mock of the coach's trigger-result card for scene 4.
- **Negative prompt / no-text-in-frame:** in-frame UI content (the actual snippet copy, dashboard numbers, CMS toast) is the subject being demonstrated and stays in-frame; all separate TEXT ON SCREEN captions and the CTA line render on a distinct overlay layer, never baked into the same pass as the UI mockups, to keep the two legible against each other.
- **Diegetic audio:** quiet office ambience and soft typing under scenes 1–2 and 6; cursor-click sounds under the screen-recording beats (3, 5); a single UI ping when the trigger result renders in scene 4; a subtle rising tone under the scene 7 CTR uptick; brand sting under the scene 8 CTA card.
