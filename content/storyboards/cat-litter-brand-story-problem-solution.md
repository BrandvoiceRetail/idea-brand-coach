## Strategy

Her felt pain: *"Three slides. One claim, said three times. No wonder none of it landed."* Her A+ content was built to sound thorough — three beats, all restating "superior odor control" — and it doesn't move anyone because it never speaks to the ONE household actually anxious about her category: multi-cat homes worried about litter tracking everywhere.

The funnel position is `amazon_brand_story`: where an undecided multi-cat owner is looking for proof this brand gets her specific mess, not a generic "cat owners" pitch. `generate_aplus_content_plan`'s 5 addressable beats force the page onto that real objection — the one her own reviews keep naming and her page never answers.

Hook trigger: identity — the page has to name the specific household, not the generic category.

## Hook variants

1. **Identity** — "My A+ content talked to 'cat owners.' My customers have three cats and a tracking problem nobody addressed."
2. **Recognition** — "Three slides. One claim, said three times. No wonder none of it landed."
3. **Fear of Loss** — "Every multi-cat household that scrolled past that page went and bought the brand that mentioned tracking."
