## Strategy

The felt pain, in her words: *"The box opens on a mat and a thickness spec card. That's it."* At `packaging_unboxing`, the moment that should feel like the payoff of her purchase decision instead reads like a warehouse checklist.

`identify_decision_trigger` names the ONE psychological lever this purchase actually turns on — not specs, but **momentum**: proof she made the right call, timed to make the next purchase (a second mat, a strap, a block) feel like the obvious next step instead of an ask.

The hook runs on **momentum** — the box should open on the reason she bought, not a parts list.

## Hook variants

1. **Momentum:** "The box should open on the reason she bought. Not a spec sheet."
2. **Recognition:** "She already knew why she bought it. The unboxing never said it back to her."
3. **Fear_of_loss:** "The moment the box opens, she decides whether to buy again. Mine said nothing."

## Production notes (Higgsfield)

- **Mode:** Marketing-studio preset (UGC unboxing) for scenes 1 and 4 — the box-opening reveal is exactly that preset's target shape; scenes 2-3 switch to per-scene precision for the founder's screen-capture beats.
- **Reference kit:** Customer-actor character sheet plus a product/packaging reference sheet for the yoga mat and box, built from the real product photo (consistent across scenes 1 and 4); separate founder character sheet for scenes 2-3; screen-UI mock of the coach's chat and the `identify_decision_trigger` result panel.
- **Negative prompt / no-text-in-frame:** No legible dashboard or UI text baked into generated frames — supply as flat reference, add every TEXT ON SCREEN line as a post overlay. On-screen disclosure required: "Actor portrayal. Composite scenario for illustration." Never implied as a verified buyer.
