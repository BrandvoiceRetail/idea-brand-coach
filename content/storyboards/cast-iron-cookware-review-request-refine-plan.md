## Strategy

Sam's felt pain, in his own words: "This email hasn't changed in over a year. My whole brand has." At `review_request_flow`, the ask that goes out after every order still leans on a positioning line Sam dropped from the listing months ago — the email is asking for a review in a voice that doesn't match the brand anymore.

`refine_creative_plan` fixes exactly that: a surgical edit to the one email component, not a rebuild of the whole sequence. The hook runs on **recognition** — the kind of stale asset that's easy to stop noticing because nothing about it is actually broken, it's just quietly out of date.

## Hook variants

1. **Recognition:** "My review-request email hasn't changed in over a year. I hadn't noticed either — until now."
2. **Momentum:** "I didn't need to rebuild the whole sequence. Just the one line that had gone stale."
3. **Fear_of_loss:** "Every review ask that mentions a positioning I dropped is a customer politely ignoring it."

## Production notes (Higgsfield)

- **Mode:** Storyboard-image default — one multi-panel storyboard covers the email pull-up, the outdated-copy close-up, and the refine-tool reveal in a single video job; the product-demo format doesn't need per-scene precision here. The refine itself mirrors Higgsfield's edit-before-regenerate discipline: only the one email panel gets touched, the rest of the reference kit and prior renders stay untouched.
- **Reference kit:** Persona character sheet for Sam (consistent face, kitchen/home-office setting); product reference sheet for the cast-iron cookware line from real product photos; a flat screen-UI mock of the email template and of the coach chat with the refine-plan result for scenes 1-4.
