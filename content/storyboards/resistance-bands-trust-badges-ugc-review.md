## Strategy

The felt pain, in a shopper's own words: "I almost didn't buy it. I couldn't tell if the brand was even legit." A composite shopper we'll call Priya nearly abandons a resistance-bands listing at the buy box — decent reviews, decent photos, but nothing near the buy button tells her the brand is real.

The connection problem sits at `trust_badges_social_proof`: the last screen before checkout, where hesitation either resolves or the tab closes. The founder later runs `run_trust_gap` on that exact section and gets the same read Priya felt instinctively — **Authentic** scores lowest: no certification, no guarantee, nothing that proves this is a real company right where the decision gets made.

The hook plays **fear_of_loss**: the near-miss almost cost the sale, and it's happening to shoppers who never say a word about it.

## Hook variants

1. **fear_of_loss:** "I almost closed the tab. Not because of the price — because I couldn't tell if this brand was real."
2. **recognition:** "Good reviews. Good photos. And I still hesitated right at the buy button."
3. **permission:** "Nobody tells you it's OK to buy from a brand you've never heard of. Something near the buy button has to say it."

## Production notes (Higgsfield)

- **Mode:** Marketing-studio preset — ugc_review talking-to-camera/phone-in-hand format for scenes 1–2, cut to per-scene precision for the founder's screen-record beats (3–4).
- **Reference kit:** Persona character sheet for the shopper actor and a separate one for the founder (consistent faces/settings within their own scenes); a real resistance-bands product/listing reference for the phone-screen shots; screen-UI mock of the coach's chat and Trust Gap scorecard flagging `trust_badges_social_proof`; a buy-box before/after mock for scene 4.
- **Negative prompt / no-text-in-frame:** No baked-in text or real Amazon trademarked UI chrome in generated frames — captions are post overlays only. The shopper is a paid actor performing a composite, illustrative scenario — never present or label her as a "verified buyer" or real customer.
- **Diegetic audio:** Quiet living-room ambience under scenes 1–2 (no music); soft mouse-click sounds under the founder's screen-record beats; brand sting on the end card.

## CTA & measurement

**End card copy:** "Find what's missing near your buy button — free." **Destination:** ideabrandcoach.com/diagnostic. **Metric that proves the ad worked:** click-through rate from the ad to /diagnostic — the same hesitate-or-click moment the ad dramatizes, now working in the ad's favor. **Metric that proves the underlying fix worked:** sessions-to-orders at the buy box once the certification badge and guarantee line are live — CTR proves the story landed, sessions-to-orders proves the trust signal closed the sale.
