## Strategy

The felt pain, in the shopper's own words: *"I almost didn't buy it. Every photo showed me parts. Not one showed me what I'd actually make."* A craft kit is sold on the outcome, not the ingredients — and this gallery only ever shows ingredients.

The funnel position is `amazon_listing_copy`: the gallery order, where a shopper deciding whether to buy a craft kit needs proof of the finished thing before they'll trust the raw materials are worth the money. `generate_listing_image_brief` catches the exact gap and adds the missing gallery slot — a finished-piece "felt moment" shot — right at that position.

The hook runs on **recognition** — she'd bought kits like this before and knows exactly what a drawer of unused resin looks like.

## Hook variants

1. **Recognition:** "I almost scrolled past this kit — every photo proved the parts were real. Not one proved I'd actually finish something with them."
2. **Fear_of_loss:** "I've bought craft kits before that turned into a drawer of unused resin. I needed proof this one wouldn't."
3. **Permission:** "I'm allowed to want proof I'll actually finish this, before I buy it."
