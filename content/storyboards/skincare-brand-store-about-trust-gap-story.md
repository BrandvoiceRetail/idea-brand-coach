## Strategy

Her felt pain: *"Six rewrites. My About page's never looked better. Nothing moved."* Every pass made the copy smoother, more confident, more "on-brand" — and the conversion number sat exactly where it started, because polish was never the thing missing.

The funnel position is `brand_store_about`: the module a shopper visits specifically to decide if they trust the brand behind the product, not to admire the writing. `run_trust_gap` scores that module and names **Authentic** as the pillar dragging the whole score down — the page reads like marketing copy, not like the founder.

The hook runs on **identity** — the version that read best on paper was the version that sounded least like a real person, and that's exactly the version that converted worst.

## Hook variants

1. **Identity:** "The version that read best on paper converted worst. That should have told me something sooner."
2. **Recognition:** "Six rewrites. Every one more polished. And less true."
3. **Permission:** "Rewrite seven won't fix this. The one true sentence buried in rewrite one will."

## Production notes (Higgsfield)

- **Mode:** Per-scene precision — the ad alternates a talking-head founder beat (1, 2, 5) with exact screen-UI states (3, 4); each needs individual scene control rather than one storyboard panel.
- **Reference kit:** Founder character sheet to keep face, wardrobe, and studio setting consistent across scenes 1, 2, and 5; screen-UI mock of the coach's chat and Trust Gap scorecard; a reference of a generic Brand Store About layout for the version-history beat.
- **Negative prompt / no-text rule:** No captions, labels, or UI copy baked into generated frames — all text is added as post overlay per the TEXT ON SCREEN field. Do not add fabricated certifications, badges, or logos to the Brand Store reference.
- **Diegetic audio:** Quiet studio ambience, a laptop lid or notebook set down softly under scene 1, trackpad clicks under scenes 3-4, no music bed until the end-card sting.

## CTA & measurement

**End card copy:** "Find the pillar the polish is hiding. Free diagnostic." **Destination:** ideabrandcoach.com/diagnostic. **Metric that proves the ad worked:** click-through rate from the ad to /diagnostic proves the hook landed; sessions-to-orders on the Brand Store is the metric that proves the fix worked — the ad's thesis is that rewriting for polish can hide the real trust gap, so a lift in click-through proves founders stuck in a rewrite loop actually went and checked, and a recovered conversion ratio proves the honest version was the right call.
