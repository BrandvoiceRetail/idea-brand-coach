## Strategy

Her own words: "My how-to video ranks number one. Almost nobody clicks the thumbnail." A page-one ranking nobody clicks is a wasted position, not a win.

The funnel position is `seo_content`: the click decision happens at the search-results thumbnail and title, before anyone ever presses play. The thumbnail and title got bolted on after editing, never planned as part of the pitch. `generate_video_storyboard` rebuilds the opening scene and its on-screen text specifically for that moment — treating the first three seconds as `seo_content` real estate, not just video content.

Hook trigger: fear of loss. The exact bad-day scenario campers search for becomes the implied stakes, instead of a generic product shot.

## Hook variants

1. **Fear of loss** — "Filter fails. No clean water. Hours from the car."
2. **Recognition** — "Page-one ranking, and my own thumbnail didn't make me want to click it."
3. **Momentum** — "I didn't need a new video. I needed the first three seconds fixed."

## Production notes (Higgsfield)

- **Mode:** per-scene precision mode (each of the seven scenes needs distinct staging — analytics dashboard, thumbnail comparisons, coach UI — a single storyboard-image pass would flatten the before/after contrast).
- **Reference kit:** persona character sheet (same founder across desk and outdoor-gear shots); screen-UI mock of the coach's `generate_video_storyboard` opening-scene output for scenes 3-4; product reference sheet from real water-filter photography for the thumbnail comparison in scene 5.
- **Negative prompt / no-text-in-frame:** no text rendered inside any generated video frame — the analytics dashboard, thumbnails, and coach UI are real screen-capture composites, not AI-generated text; TEXT ON SCREEN overlays added in post only.
- **Diegetic audio:** quiet home-office room tone for desk scenes; one soft UI-click SFX at the `generate_video_storyboard` run in scene 3; no music bed under VO.
