## Strategy

Her felt pain: *"I paid for a real studio shoot. My click rate didn't move an inch."* She did the "premium" thing — a clean, professionally lit product-only shot — and it made no difference, because clean and premium were never the actual barrier.

The funnel position is `amazon_main_image`: the scroll-grid moment where a shopper's gut reads a thumbnail as safe or not before any spec gets read. A cold, clinical product shot can read as *less* trustworthy next to a competitor's cozier, more human staging — the opposite of the "premium" bet she made.

The hook runs on **belonging** — shoppers scrolling for a weighted blanket are scrolling for comfort, and her "professional" image was accidentally answering the wrong emotional question before anyone even clicked.

## Hook variants

1. **Belonging:** "I paid for a real studio shoot. My click rate didn't move an inch."
2. **Recognition:** "Everyone said the photo looked more premium. Nobody said it looked less safe."
3. **Fear_of_loss:** "Every day this thumbnail stays live, some other blanket gets the comfort click instead of mine."

## Production notes (Higgsfield)

- **Mode:** Per-scene precision — the ad alternates talking-head beats (1, 2, 5) with exact screen-UI states (3, 4); each needs individual scene control rather than one storyboard panel.
- **Reference kit:** Founder character sheet to keep face, wardrobe, and living room consistent across scenes 1, 2, and 5; screen-UI mock of the coach's Trust Gap scorecard; real product photos of the weighted blanket — both the cold listing shot and a cozy staged reference for the comparison beat; a competitor-style lifestyle reference for the split screen.
- **Negative prompt / no-text rule:** No captions, labels, or UI text baked into generated frames — all copy is added as post overlay per the TEXT ON SCREEN field. Do not add extra branding or badges to the competitor comparison shot.
- **Diegetic audio:** Living-room ambience, soft fabric rustle when she gestures to the blanket, laptop keyboard/mouse-click sounds under scenes 3-4, a light ambient pad rising only under the end card.
