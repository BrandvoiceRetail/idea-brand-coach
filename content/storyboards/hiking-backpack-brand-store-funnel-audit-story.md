## Strategy

The felt pain, in her words: "I was bracing myself. I genuinely thought the audit was going to come back and tell me my bullets were the problem, again, because that's always where I've been told to look." Two years of "fix your listing copy" advice had trained her to assume the fault always lives in the same place.

The specific funnel position surfaced is `brand_store_about`. `run_funnel_audit`'s weakest-link overlay clears the Amazon listing entirely and instead flags her Brand Store About page — a page she'd built once, years ago, and never revisited, not realizing a meaningful share of her shoppers were visiting it before they bought.

Hook trigger: **recognition** — the weak link was somewhere she wasn't even looking, because nobody had told her to look there.

## Hook variants

1. **recognition:** "I braced for the listing to fail the audit. The listing was fine. The page I'd never checked wasn't."
2. **fear_of_loss:** "I'd been fixing the same page for two years while shoppers were quietly leaving on a different one."
3. **permission:** "You're allowed to stop assuming it's always the listing. Sometimes it's the page you built once and forgot."

## Production notes (Higgsfield)

- **Mode:** Per-scene precision. The bracing beat (scene 1), the clean-listing surprise (scene 3), and the flagged-page reveal (scene 4) each carry a distinct emotional beat that storyboard-image mode would flatten — each needs its own reference framing.
- **Reference kit:** Persona character sheet for the founder-actor (same face, home-office setting, wardrobe throughout); product reference sheet for the hiking backpack from real product photography; screen-UI mocks of the funnel-audit overlay in both states — clean `amazon_listing_copy` (scene 3) and flagged `brand_store_about` (scene 4).
- **Negative prompt / no-text-in-frame:** Do not render legible touchpoint labels, score values, or audit copy inside generated frames — supply both overlay states as flat reference images and add every TEXT ON SCREEN line as a post overlay. On-screen disclosure required: "Actor portrayal. Composite scenario for illustration."
- **Diegetic audio:** Quiet home-office room tone throughout; a tense held breath under scene 1; trackpad clicks under scenes 2-4; a small relieved exhale (no music sting) under scene 6.
