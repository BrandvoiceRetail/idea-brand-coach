## Strategy

The felt pain, in her words: *"I have a welcome email. I checked the box. Retention is handled."* Except customers buy once and disappear — the box got checked, the actual leak never closed.

The funnel position is `welcome_series`, one touchpoint inside the Retention category. She assumed building it meant retention was covered. `get_funnel_coverage` runs the coverage audit across the retention touchpoints and shows `welcome_series` is the only one built — order confirmation, shipping email, packaging/unboxing, and winback/replenishment all sit empty.

The hook runs on **fear_of_loss** — the first-purchase momentum she worked for is quietly draining away through gaps she didn't know existed.

## Hook variants

1. **Fear_of_loss:** "I thought my welcome email meant retention was handled. It's the only retention thing I've built."
2. **Recognition:** "You're not wrong for building one welcome email. You're just not done."
3. **Permission:** "You don't need five new emails today. You need to see the four gaps first."

## Production notes (Higgsfield)

- **Mode:** Per-scene precision. The ad moves between a founder-actor beat and two distinct screen-capture panels (orders dashboard, coverage map); each needs its own composition rather than one continuous storyboard image.
- **Reference kit:** Founder character sheet (kitchen/home-office setting, consistent across scenes 1-2); screen-UI mock of the orders dashboard and the `get_funnel_coverage` retention coverage map for scenes 3-4; real product photo of the tea packaging as a light prop.
- **Negative prompt / no-text-in-frame:** No legible dashboard chrome or real order numbers baked into generated frames — supply flat reference images and add every TEXT ON SCREEN line as a post overlay. On-screen disclosure required: "Actor portrayal. Composite scenario for illustration." Never labeled or implied as a verified buyer.
- **Diegetic audio:** Quiet kitchen/home-office room tone throughout; laptop trackpad clicks under scenes 3-4; no music bed until the end-card sting.

## CTA & measurement

**End card copy:** "See your own retention gaps. Free diagnostic." **Destination:** ideabrandcoach.com/diagnostic. **Ad-level metric:** click-through rate from the ad to /diagnostic — the fear_of_loss hook targets founders who believe one retention touchpoint is enough, so a lift proves the ad got them to check their own coverage map. **Connection-level metric:** repeat-purchase rate once the other four retention touchpoints go live — proof the leak the coverage map exposed is actually closing, not just visible.
