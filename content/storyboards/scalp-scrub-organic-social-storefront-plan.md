## Strategy

Her felt pain, in her own words: *"My bio link took people somewhere. It just didn't feel like anywhere."* Her `organic_social_profile` content earns real attention — a follower watches her demo the scrub, gets curious, taps the link — and lands on a storefront that never closes the loop she just opened.

The funnel position is `organic_social_profile`, specifically the bio-link handoff into her own storefront. `generate_storefront_messaging_plan` rebuilds the hero, the tagline system, and the job-mapped category tiles so that click lands on an actual reason to buy, not a generic banner.

Hook trigger: belonging — the click already believed in her; the storefront is where that belief goes to die.

## Hook variants

1. **Belonging** — "My bio link took people somewhere. It just didn't feel like anywhere."
2. **Recognition** — "Click the link in bio. Land on a store that could be selling anything."
3. **Momentum** — "Every one of those social clicks was already sold on me. My storefront wasted it."

## Production notes (Higgsfield)

- **Mode:** Storyboard-image default — one continuous bathroom-sink setting across a short personal demo; a single multi-panel storyboard image drives one video job.
- **Reference kit:** Founder character sheet for continuity across all five shots; product reference sheet built from the real scalp-scrub jar and texture; screen-UI mocks of the Instagram bio-link tap-through, the generic storefront hero, and the coach's storefront messaging plan output.
- **Negative prompt / no-text-in-frame:** No baked-in captions or UI labels inside generated frames — every TEXT ON SCREEN line is added as a post overlay; render both storefronts as clean generic mocks, not trademarked platform screenshots.
- **Diegetic audio:** Bathroom-sink ambience, water and product sounds under scene 1; a phone-tap sound under scene 2; quiet laptop-key sounds under scenes 3-4; no music bed until the end card.

## CTA & measurement

**End card copy:** "Same clicks. A storefront that finally says why." **Destination:** ideabrandcoach.com/diagnostic. **Metric that proves the ad worked:** diagnostic-start rate among viewers who tap through — proof the belonging hook ("your own traffic deserves better than this") gets a founder to check her own storefront's messaging. **Metric that proves the fix itself worked:** repeat-visit rate on the storefront from that same bio-link audience — proof the new hero and tagline system, not just the click, is what earns a second look.
