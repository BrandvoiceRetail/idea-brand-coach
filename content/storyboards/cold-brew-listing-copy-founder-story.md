## Strategy

His felt pain, in his own words: *"I wrote every bullet straight off the ingredient panel — cold-brewed, low-acid, concentrate ratio — and it reads exactly like that. Correct. Forgettable."* He built the copy the way he'd fill out a spec sheet, because that's the information he had closest at hand.

The funnel position is `amazon_listing_copy`: the bullets a shopper reads after the click, deciding whether to trust this specific brand over the six other concentrates on the page. Spec-sheet language proves the product exists; it doesn't prove the brand understands the person buying it.

The hook runs on **identity**: hearing his own customers' actual words missing from his own page is a gut-check moment — the listing sounds like nobody who's ever actually made a cup with it.

## Hook variants

1. **Identity:** "My own customers had the words for this. My own listing never used a single one of them."
2. **Recognition:** "I wrote bullets off the ingredient panel. My customers write reviews about their mornings."
3. **Permission:** "You don't need a copywriter. You need to actually read what people already wrote about you."

## Production notes (Higgsfield)

- **Mode:** Storyboard-image default for the founder-story kitchen beats (single coherent character and setting across scenes), with a per-scene precision insert for the `ingest_evidence` screen recording and split-screen comparison, which need exact UI and copy states.
- **Reference kit:** Founder character sheet (consistent face, kitchen setting, wardrobe across all on-camera scenes); screen-UI mock of the coach's chat and the vocabulary-panel output; real product photo of the cold-brew concentrate bottle for continuity in the pour shot.
- **Negative prompt / no-text rule:** No text, quote marks, or caption chrome baked into generated frames — every quoted customer phrase and bullet comparison is a post-production overlay per the TEXT ON SCREEN field; do not invent additional customer quotes beyond the one specified.
- **Diegetic audio:** Ambient kitchen room tone (ice, pour, faint clink of glass), soft trackpad/UI tap on the screen-recording cuts, understated warmth in the founder's voice — no music bed until the end-card sting.

## CTA & measurement

**End card copy:** "Find the words already sitting in your reviews — free." **Destination:** ideabrandcoach.com/diagnostic. **Metric that proves the ad worked:** click-through rate from the ad to /diagnostic — the identity hook is built for founders who recognize their own spec-sheet bullets and click through to see what their customers actually say. Downstream, sessions-to-orders on the listing is what proves the rewritten bullets actually convert, not just read better.
