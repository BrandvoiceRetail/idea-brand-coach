## Strategy

The felt pain, in Marcus's words: "I've got the better product. I've got the better reviews. And I can't find my own listing in the search grid without typing the exact ASIN." He assumed the photo itself was the issue — bad lighting, wrong angle — but the real problem is that his main image reads exactly like three lookalike bags of chews shoppers have already scrolled past, so there's nothing telling them to stop on his.

The specific funnel position is `amazon_main_image`. `generate_main_image_title_plan` surfaces the differentiator Marcus already had — buried in his own bullet points — and turns it into the one thing his image and title should be saying at the scroll decision.

Hook trigger: **recognition** — the click-worthy detail was never missing, it was misplaced.

## Hook variants

1. **recognition:** "I couldn't find my own listing in the search grid. My dog's actual story was buried in my bullet points instead."
2. **fear_of_loss:** "Better product. Better reviews. Still losing every click to three lookalike bags of chews."
3. **identity:** "I wasn't selling 'joint chews.' I was selling 'don't watch your dog slow down.' My image never said that."

## Production notes (Higgsfield)

- **Mode:** Storyboard-image (default). This is a single-take founder narrative arc — one multi-panel storyboard image compresses the desk-to-dog-to-laptop beats cleanly into one video job without needing per-scene UI precision.
- **Reference kit:** Persona character sheet for Marcus (same face, home-office wardrobe, consistent lighting); a consistent reference image of his senior dog (greying muzzle, same coat markings across every scene); product reference sheet for the chew bag from the real product photo; a clean mock-UI reference of the coach chat and the rendered plan card for scenes 3-4 and 6.
- **Negative prompt / no-text-in-frame:** Do not let the model render legible bullet-point text or plan UI copy inside the frame — supply the plan card as a flat reference image and add every TEXT ON SCREEN line as a post overlay. On-screen disclosure required: "Actor portrayal. Composite scenario for illustration." — Marcus is a fictional composite founder, not a verified customer or real case study.
- **Diegetic audio:** Quiet home-office ambience throughout (a dog's tag jingle in scenes 2 and 5, keyboard clicks in scenes 3-4 and 6); VO reads as one continuous reflective take, not narration-over-music.
