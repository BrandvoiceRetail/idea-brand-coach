## Strategy

Her felt pain, in her own words: *"Forty-seven reviews. Forty-seven star ratings. Not one I could actually use anywhere."* At `review_request_flow`, her ask is generic — leave a rating — and generic asks get generic answers: five stars, "great, thanks," nothing with a face or a feeling attached to it.

The funnel position is `review_request_flow`: the moment right after delivery when a happy customer is most willing to say something real, if only she asked for something real. `generate_ugc_ad_plan` scripts a try-on-style testimonial built on her customer avatar's own identity trigger — a specific ask that gives her something worth putting on camera, not another star icon.

Hook trigger: identity — a five-star rating proves nothing about who bought it or why; the ask needs to be about that instead.

## Hook variants

1. **Identity** — "Five stars. Zero words. My review request wasn't asking for anything real."
2. **Recognition** — "Every review request I sent got the same three words back: 'Great, thanks!'"
3. **Fear_of_loss** — "A five-star rating with nothing else is a customer I'll never get to use anywhere."
